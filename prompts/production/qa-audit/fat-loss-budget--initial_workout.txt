Before your first heavy compound lift, always run through 2 lighter warm-up sets — this is non-negotiable, especially as a beginner.

**REST DAY (Choose one — Tuesday or Thursday)**
Complete rest. No gym, no structured exercise. Aim for 3,000–4,000 steps (just daily movement). Use this day to eat well, hydrate, and mentally recharge. Your muscles grow and fat loss happens when you're resting, so take it seriously.

---

**TRAINING INTENSITY & REP RANGES**

On your compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, bench, rows, overhead press), aim to leave 2–3 reps in reserve on most sets — you should feel strong and controlled, not grinding out the last rep. This is especially important as a beginner; it keeps your joints healthy and builds solid movement patterns. On isolation and accessory work (leg curls, flyes, raises, pushdowns), you can push closer to failure — aim for that last rep to feel genuinely hard. On the very last set of isolation exercises, if you hit strict failure, you're allowed 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" to squeeze out a bit more volume. But never do this on compound barbell lifts like squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges — those need pristine form to stay safe.

---

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (~5–8 mins after each session)**

After your workout, spend a few minutes on these stretches:
- Quad Stretch: 60 seconds per leg
- Hamstring Stretch (forward fold): 60 seconds
- Chest Stretch (doorway or arms behind back): 60 seconds
- Shoulder Stretch (cross-body): 30 seconds per arm
- Glute Stretch (figure-4 or pigeon): 60 seconds per side
- Child's Pose or Cat-Cow: 60 seconds

Keep the pace slow and steady — this isn't about being flexible; it's about bringing your heart rate down and signaling to your body that the session is over.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid — aim to push that to 7.5–9 hours on training days if you can. Your body does a lot of repair work while you sleep, especially when you're in a calorie deficit for fat loss.

Here are some practical tweaks for your routine:

1. **Chai cutoff:** Stop having chai or tea after 6 PM. I know it's part of your routine, but caffeine stays in your system for 5–6 hours. If you need something warm in the evening, switch to herbal tea or warm milk.

2. **Dinner timing:** You're eating at 9 PM, which is late. If possible, try moving dinner to 8 PM — this gives your digestion a head start before bed and can help with sleep quality.

3. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 mins before bed. The blue light messes with melatonin production, and scrolling keeps your mind active.

4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 65–68°F if you can). Indians often sleep with fans or AC — that's actually great for sleep quality.

5. **Consistency:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body loves routine.

6. **Post-workout:** If you're training in the evening, try to finish by 8 PM so you have time to wind down before sleep. A warm shower after the gym can help too.

7. **Hydration before bed:** Drink most of your 2L of water during the day and morning; taper off 1–2 hours before sleep to avoid waking up for bathroom trips.

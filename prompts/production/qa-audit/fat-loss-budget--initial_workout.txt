**WARMUP (Every Session — ~10 minutes)**

Start with a brisk 3-minute walk or light jog on the treadmill to get your heart rate up and loosen the joints. Then:
- Arm circles and shoulder rolls, 10 each direction
- Cat-cow stretches, 8–10 reps
- Bodyweight squats, 10 reps (focus on depth and control)
- Glute bridges, 12 reps (squeeze hard at the top)
- Inchworms, 8 reps
- Light band pull-aparts or chest opens, 12 reps

Once you're warm, move into your main lift with 2 lighter warm-up sets — nothing heavy, just movement to prep the nervous system.

---

**DAY 1 — Lower Power (Monday)**

Focus here is on strength and power through your legs. Squats are your star, so we're hitting them fresh when you're most alert.

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps (heavy, controlled descent, pause at the bottom for 1 second)
- Romanian Deadlift: 3 sets x 6 reps (feel the hamstring stretch, don't let your back round)

**Accessory Work:**
- Leg Press: 3 sets x 8 reps (go a bit heavier here since it's machine-based; less balance demand)
- Walking Lunges (dumbbell): 3 sets x 10 per leg (control the step, don't rush)

**Core:**
- Cable Woodchops: 3 sets x 10 per side (rotate through your core, not your arms)
- Dead Bug: 2 sets x 12 reps (move slowly, keep lower back flat)
- Plank Hold: 2 sets x 30–45 seconds (breathe, don't hold your breath)

**Daily step target:** 6,000 steps (you'll get some from walking lunges and moving between stations).

**Coaching note:** On the squat, leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set. You're building strength, not grinding to failure. Form > weight every single time.

---

**DAY 2 — Upper Pull & Push (Tuesday)**

This is a balanced upper day mixing horizontal and vertical pulling with some pressing to keep the shoulders healthy.

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 5 reps (chest up, drive elbows back, feel your lats engage)
- Barbell Bench Press or Dumbbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 6 reps (whichever feels better in your gym)

**Accessory Work:**
- Lat Pulldown (cable): 3 sets x 8 reps (pull elbows down and back, not just straight down)
- Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets x 8 reps (controlled tempo, slight knee dip if needed for stability)
- Face Pulls (cable): 3 sets x 12 reps (external rotation work, great for shoulder health)

**Core:**
- Pallof Press (cable): 3 sets x 10 per side (anti-rotation strength)
- Ab Wheel Rollout (or kneeling version): 2 sets x 8 reps (if this is too hard, use an ab machine instead)
- Bird Dogs: 2 sets x 10 per side (slow and controlled)

**Daily step target:** 5,500 steps (lighter day, office work will cover most of it).

**Coaching note:** On rows and bench, drive hard but controlled. These are strength sets, so leave 1–2 reps in reserve. Face pulls might feel light, but that's the point — shoulder health is non-negotiable.

**DAY 3 — Active Recovery (Wednesday)**

This is a mobility and stretching day. No heavy lifting. Think of it as movement for the sake of recovery.

- 10-minute easy walk or light cycling
- Full-body dynamic stretching: leg swings, arm circles, torso rotations
- Foam rolling (if available): calves, quads, IT band, upper back — 60 seconds each
- Yoga-style flow or simple stretching: hold each major stretch for 30–45 seconds
  - Quad stretch, hamstring stretch, hip flexor stretch, chest opener, shoulder stretch
- Light mobility work: hip circles, shoulder dislocates, cat-cow

**Daily step target:** 7,000–8,000 steps (keep moving gently; active recovery means active).

**Coaching note:** This day is about listening to your body. If something feels tight, spend extra time on it. You'll feel fresher for the next session.

**DAY 4 — Lower Hypertrophy (Thursday)**

Back to the lower body, but this time with higher reps and a bit more volume. Your quads and hamstrings will thank you.

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Front Squat or Goblet Squat: 3 sets x 8 reps (slightly higher reps, focus on the burn)
- Deadlift: 3 sets x 5 reps (still heavy, but single reps are fine here)

**Accessory Work:**
- Leg Press: 3 sets x 10 reps (push close to failure on the last set; 1–2 cheat reps okay if needed)
- Leg Curl (machine): 3 sets x 12 reps (hamstring focus, control the eccentric)
- Calf Raises (barbell or machine): 3 sets x 15 reps (higher reps, full range of motion)

**Core:**
- Ab Crunch (machine or cable): 3 sets x 12 reps (close to failure on the last set)
- Suitcase Carry (dumbbell): 2 sets x 30 seconds per side (anti-lateral flexion)
- Reverse Crunch: 2 sets x 12 reps (controlled, squeeze at the top)

**Daily step target:** 5,500 steps (this is a heavy session; conserve energy).

**Coaching note:** On leg press and leg curl, you can push a bit harder here. These are isolation moves, so it's okay to chase the pump and go closer to failure. Just keep your form tight.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (After Every Session — ~5 minutes)**

After you finish your last set, spend a few minutes stretching the major muscle groups:

- Quad stretch: 30–45 seconds per leg (standing or lying)
- Hamstring stretch: 30–45 seconds per leg
- Hip flexor stretch: 30–45 seconds per side (lunge position)
- Chest opener: 30–45 seconds (doorway or cross-body)
- Shoulder stretch: 30–45 seconds per arm
- Child's pose: 45–60 seconds (breathe, relax)

Keep it simple and consistent. This takes 5 minutes and will help with recovery and mobility.

**TRAINING INTENSITY PHILOSOPHY**

Here's the deal: on your heavy compounds (squats, deadlifts, rows, bench), you're aiming for 3–6 reps, and you should leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set. This is about building strength and staying safe as a beginner. Don't grind to failure on barbell lifts.

On your accessory work and isolation moves (leg press, leg curl, lateral raises, bicep curls, face pulls), you've got more room to push. Aim for the rep range listed, and on the last set, it's okay to push closer to failure. If you hit strict form failure, you can throw in 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" to extend the set — but only on isolation moves, never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. The cheat reps should be controlled; we're not bouncing or throwing weight around.

Core work every day: you'll notice each day has a different core combo. This keeps things fresh and hits different angles (rotation, anti-rotation, flexion, stability). Mix it up.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Let's lock it in at 7.5–9 hours for optimal recovery, especially with fat loss in the mix.

**Timing:** Since you're training in the evening, try to be in bed by 11 PM and up by 7–7:30 AM. This gives your body time to wind down after training and sleep through the night.

**Practical tips for better sleep:**

1. **Chai cutoff:** No chai or coffee after 4 PM. Caffeine lingers for 6+ hours, and you're already having it at 6:30 PM with snacks. If you need your evening chai ritual, switch to herbal or decaf after 4 PM.

2. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30–45 minutes before bed. The blue light messes with melatonin production. This is a big one — I know it's tempting, but your sleep quality will jump.

3. **Meal timing:** Your dinner is at 9 PM, which is late. Try to finish eating by 9:30 PM at the latest. Going to bed on a full stomach disrupts sleep quality. If you're hungry before bed, a small glass of milk or a handful of nuts is fine.

4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 18–20°C if possible). A fan helps. Your body sleeps better in cooler conditions.

5. **Consistency:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body thrives on routine.

6. **Stress management:** You mentioned moderate stress. Try 5–10 minutes of deep breathing or light meditation before bed. Helps settle the mind after a long workday.

7. **Hydration:** Drink most of your 2L of water during the day, not in the evening. Waking up to pee disrupts sleep.

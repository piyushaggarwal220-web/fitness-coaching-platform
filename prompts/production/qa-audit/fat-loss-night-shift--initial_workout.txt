Hey Divya,

I know night shifts are brutal on sleep and recovery, so we're keeping this plan simple and focused — three solid gym sessions a week that'll help you drop fat and build strength without wrecking you further. You mentioned loving squats and rows, so we're building around those. Since you're coming off shift in the mornings, we'll make sure the warm-up gets your body ready without dragging things out.

**Your Warm-Up (10 minutes before every session)**

Start with 5 minutes on the treadmill or bike at an easy pace — just enough to get your heart rate up and joints moving. Then run through some arm circles, leg swings front-to-back and side-to-side, hip openers, and a few bodyweight squats. Before you get into your main lifts, do 2 lighter warm-up sets — one with just the bar or very light weight, one at about 50% of your working weight. This primes your nervous system and keeps you safe.

**Your Split This Week**

We're running a simple full-body approach across three days — each session hits upper and lower body with a slightly different focus, so you're getting balanced work without overdoing any one area. You'll train Monday, Wednesday, Friday (or whatever three non-consecutive days work around your shifts).

---

**Day 1 — Lower Focus + Upper Pull**

1. Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps
   - Your favorite. Keep your core tight, chest up, and sit back into your hips. Control the descent, explode up.

2. Romanian Deadlift (Barbell): 3 sets x 6 reps
   - Hinge at the hips, slight knee bend, bar stays close to your legs. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings.

3. Leg Press: 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Feet shoulder-width, push through your heels. Don't lock out your knees at the top.

4. Cable Seated Row: 3 sets x 10 reps
   - Pull to your lower ribs, squeeze your shoulder blades together. You mentioned loving rows — own these.

5. Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip): 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Pull to your upper chest, elbows down and back. Control the return.

6. Dumbbell Bicep Curl: 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Strict form, no swinging. Squeeze at the top.

7. Hanging Knee Raises: 3 sets x 12 reps
   - If you can't hang, do these on a captain's chair or bench-supported.

8. Plank: 3 sets x 45 seconds
   - Elbows under shoulders, body in a straight line. Squeeze your glutes.

9. Russian Twists (Bodyweight or light dumbbell): 3 sets x 20 total reps
   - Sit back slightly, twist from your torso, not your arms.

**Step target today: 8,000 steps** — you're training, so try to get a short walk in post-workout or during your evening break before your shift.

---

**Day 2 — Upper Focus + Lower Power**

1. Dumbbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 6 reps
   - Press up and slightly together, control the descent. Keep your feet flat on the floor.

2. Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 6 reps
   - Hinge forward, back flat, pull the bar to your lower chest. Another row variation for you.

3. Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated): 3 sets x 10 reps
   - Press straight up, don't arch your lower back. Controlled tempo.

4. Cable Chest Fly: 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Slight bend in your elbows, squeeze your chest at the center. Feel the stretch on the way out.

5. Cable Lateral Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Light weight, lead with your elbows, don't swing.

6. Goblet Squat (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Hold a dumbbell at your chest, squat deep, elbows inside your knees.

7. Walking Lunges (Bodyweight or light dumbbells): 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
   - Step forward, drop your back knee, push through your front heel.

8. Cable Crunch: 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Kneel facing the cable, crunch down by flexing your abs, not pulling with your arms.

9. Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets x 20 total reps
   - Slow and controlled, opposite elbow to knee.

10. Side Plank: 3 sets x 30 seconds each side
    - Stack your feet, lift your hips, hold steady.

**Step target today: 8,000 steps** — another training day, so aim for the same.

**Day 3 — Active Recovery**

No gym today. Your body needs to repair. Go for a 20-30 minute walk at an easy pace — around your neighborhood or a park, nothing intense. Spend 15 minutes stretching the major muscle groups: quads, hamstrings, glutes, chest, shoulders, lats. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds, breathe deeply, and let your body release tension. If you have a foam roller, use it on your quads, calves, and upper back.

**Step target today: 6,000 steps** — light movement, nothing strenuous.

**Day 4 — Lower + Upper Push**

1. Barbell Front Squat (or Back Squat if front is uncomfortable): 4 sets x 5 reps
   - Keep your torso upright, core braced. If front squats hurt your wrists, stick with back squats.

2. Leg Press: 4 sets x 10 reps
   - Go a bit heavier today, but keep your lower back flat against the pad.

3. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift: 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Same hinge pattern as the barbell version, just with dumbbells at your sides.

4. Leg Curl (Machine): 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Squeeze your hamstrings at the top, control the descent.

5. Dumbbell Incline Press: 3 sets x 10 reps
   - Set the bench to 30-45 degrees, press up and together.

6. Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope): 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Keep your elbows pinned at your sides, extend fully, squeeze your triceps.

7. Dumbbell Lateral Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Same as Day 2, but with dumbbells. Control the weight, don't swing.

8. Lying Leg Raises: 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Lie flat, hands under your glutes, lift your legs to 90 degrees, lower slowly.

9. Plank with Shoulder Taps: 3 sets x 20 total taps
   - Hold a plank, tap your opposite shoulder, keep your hips stable.

10. Dead Bug: 3 sets x 12 reps per side
    - Lie on your back, extend opposite arm and leg, keep your lower back pressed to the floor.

**Step target today: 8,000 steps** — final training day of the week, keep moving.

**Post-Workout Stretching Routine (5-10 minutes after every training session)**

Run through these stretches after each gym day:

- Quad stretch (standing, pull your heel to your glutes): 45 seconds each side
- Hamstring stretch (seated or standing toe touch): 45 seconds
- Glute stretch (figure-4 or pigeon pose): 45 seconds each side
- Hip flexor stretch (lunge position, push your hips forward): 45 seconds each side
- Chest stretch (doorway or cable machine): 45 seconds
- Lat stretch (hang from a bar or pull-up station): 30 seconds
- Shoulder stretch (pull your arm across your body): 30 seconds each side

Breathe deeply, don't bounce, just hold and let the muscle release.

**Training Intensity**

For your big lifts — squats, deadlifts, presses, rows — leave 1-2 reps in the tank. You should finish each set knowing you could've done one or two more with good form. For isolation work — curls, flyes, leg curls, lateral raises — you can push closer to failure. On the last set of those, if you hit strict failure and want to squeeze out 1-2 controlled cheat reps (with a slight swing or assist), that's fine. But never cheat on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound — those require perfect form every rep.

---

**Sleep (This is Critical)**

I know night shifts make sleep tough, but you need to prioritize this. Aim for 7.5-9 hours in a dark, cool room. Since you finish your shift in the morning, try to sleep by 9-10 am and wake up around 5-6 pm. Here's what'll help:

- Blackout curtains or an eye mask — your room needs to be pitch dark.
- No chai or coffee after 6 am when you're winding down post-shift. Switch to herbal tea or warm milk.
- Put your phone on silent and keep it across the room. No scrolling before bed.
- Eat your main meal (dinner) at 8 pm before your shift, not right before you sleep. If you're hungry post-shift, have something light like curd or a banana.
- Keep your room cool — around 22-24°C if possible. Use a fan if you don't have AC.
- If your mind races when you lie down, try 5 minutes of deep breathing: inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 6.
- On your off days, try to get some sunlight exposure in the late afternoon or early evening to help regulate your circadian rhythm.

Your body won't change much if you're only sleeping 5 hours. Make this a non-negotiable.

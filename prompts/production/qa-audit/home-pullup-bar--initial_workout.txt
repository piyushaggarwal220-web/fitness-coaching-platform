**Pre-Workout Warm-Up (10 minutes)**
Start every session with this:
- 2–3 minutes light cardio (jumping jacks, light jogging on the spot, or brisk walking around your space)
- Arm circles and shoulder rolls, 10 each direction
- Cat-cow stretches, 8–10 reps
- Bodyweight squats, 10 reps
- Inchworms, 8 reps
- Glute bridges, 12 reps
- Then before your first heavy compound, run through 2 lighter warm-up sets at about 50–60% of your working weight (or fewer reps on bodyweight moves) — this primes your nervous system and joints.

---

**Monday — Upper Power**
Focus: Strength and neuromuscular control on your upper body compounds.

- Pull-ups: 5 sets x 4–6 reps (this is your main lift today — leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set; if you're hitting 6 reps easily, add a weighted vest or resistance band next week)
- Dips: 4 sets x 5–7 reps (same principle — controlled, strong reps; these are your second heavy compound)
- Pike push-ups: 3 sets x 8–10 reps (targets shoulders and upper chest; keep hips high and core tight)
- Inverted rows (using a low pull-up bar or sturdy table): 3 sets x 8–12 reps (builds back thickness and works the rear delts)
- Ab wheel rollouts (or plank holds if you don't have a wheel): 3 sets x 6–10 reps (core stability under load)
- Pallof holds (resistance band or makeshift — lean against a wall, arms extended): 2 sets x 20 seconds each side

Coach's note: On pull-ups and dips, quality over quantity. If you can't hit 4 reps, scale with a resistance band looped under your feet for assistance. On pike push-ups, the deeper you go, the harder it gets — no need to go full range if you're new to these; build up over weeks.

Daily step target: 6,500 steps (you'll naturally get some from training; aim for a short evening walk after dinner or break up your office day with more movement).

---

**Tuesday — Lower Hypertrophy**
Focus: Higher reps, time under tension, and metabolic stress for leg growth.

- Pistol squats or assisted pistol squats (use your pull-up bar for balance): 4 sets x 6–10 reps per leg (unilateral work is gold for glute and quad development; if full pistols are too tough, use a band looped over the bar and step into it for assistance)
- Single-leg Romanian deadlifts: 3 sets x 10–12 reps per leg (hamstring and glute focus; use a chair or wall for light balance support only)
- Bulgarian split squats (rear foot elevated on a chair or low surface): 3 sets x 10–12 reps per leg (quad and glute burner; controlled tempo)
- Glute bridges: 3 sets x 12–15 reps (high reps, squeeze hard at the top; if too easy, single-leg bridges or hold a weight)
- Dead bugs: 3 sets x 10 reps per side (anti-rotation core work; move slowly)
- Reverse crunches: 2 sets x 12–15 reps (lower ab focus)

Coach's note: On pistol squats, don't chase perfect form yet — assisted versions are totally fine. The key is controlling the descent and driving through your whole foot. On split squats, keep your torso upright and front knee tracking over your toes.

Daily step target: 5,500 steps (leg day is taxing; keep it moderate and recover well).

---

**Wednesday — Active Recovery**
No heavy lifting. Instead:
- 15–20 minute easy walk (around your neighborhood or indoors)
- Full-body mobility flow: hip openers, shoulder dislocations, thoracic rotations, calf stretches — 20–30 minutes total, moving slowly and breathing deeply
- Light stretching (see cooldown routine below)
- Hydrate well; have an extra glass of water or two

Daily step target: 7,000 steps (this is your active recovery day, so a bit more movement helps flush out soreness without taxing your system).

---

**Thursday — Upper Hypertrophy**
Focus: Higher reps and volume on upper body to drive muscle growth.

- Pull-ups: 4 sets x 8–12 reps (higher rep range today; use assistance if needed to stay in this range)
- Dips: 4 sets x 8–12 reps (same — hypertrophy focus; controlled descent, explosive press)
- Archer push-ups (hands close together, lean to one side): 3 sets x 6–10 reps per side (unilateral chest and shoulder work; builds asymmetry balance)
- Inverted rows: 3 sets x 12–15 reps (higher reps for back volume; squeeze shoulder blades together)
- Decline sit-ups (feet elevated on a chair): 3 sets x 10–15 reps (upper ab emphasis; controlled eccentric)
- Bicycle crunches: 2 sets x 15 reps per side (oblique and core stability)

Coach's note: On pull-ups and dips at higher reps, you can push a bit closer to failure — aim for 1–2 reps left in reserve. On archer push-ups, if they're too hard, do regular push-ups with a wider stance and focus on tempo (3 seconds down, 1 second up).

Daily step target: 6,500 steps (upper body day; you'll be fresher than leg day, so aim for normal activity).

---

**Friday — Lower Power**
Focus: Strength on lower body compounds, lower reps.

- Pistol squats or assisted pistol squats: 5 sets x 4–6 reps per leg (your main lower lift; heavy and controlled)
- Single-leg Romanian deadlifts: 4 sets x 5–7 reps per leg (strength on the posterior chain)
- Bulgarian split squats: 3 sets x 8–10 reps per leg (secondary strength work; heavier load than Tuesday)
- Step-ups (onto a sturdy chair or bench): 3 sets x 8–10 reps per leg (quad and glute; controlled tempo)
- Hollow body holds (or dead bugs with added difficulty): 3 sets x 20–30 seconds (core anti-extension)
- Side plank: 2 sets x 25–35 seconds per side (lateral core stability)

Coach's note: On pistol squats, if 4–6 reps is too heavy, dial back the assistance slightly and build up. The strength you build here translates to everyday life and athleticism. On step-ups, keep your torso upright and drive through your front heel.

Daily step target: 5,500 steps (another leg day; recovery is key).

**Saturday & Sunday — Rest Days**
No structured training. Light movement only (casual walk, stretching if you feel stiff). Aim for 4,000–5,000 steps total across both days. Focus on sleep, nutrition, and mental recovery.

**Training Intensity & Effort**
Here's the philosophy: On your heavy compounds (pull-ups, dips, pistol squats, RDLs), leave 1–2 reps in the tank. This keeps you safe, allows for consistent progression week to week, and prevents burnout. On isolation and accessory work (pike push-ups, inverted rows, split squats, crunches), you can push harder — aim for 2–3 reps from failure on most sets, and on the final set of an exercise, you can squeeze out 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" if needed (a tiny bit of momentum or body English is fine). Never do this on barbell or heavy compound movements like pistol squats, deadlifts, or lunges — those stay strict and controlled. If you're unsure, err on the side of control; it's better to leave a rep in the tank than to sacrifice form.

**Post-Workout Cooldown & Stretching (5–7 minutes)**
After every session, spend a few minutes bringing your heart rate down and stretching out the major groups you just hit:

- Chest stretch (doorway or hands behind head): 30–45 seconds each side
- Shoulder and lat stretch (arm across body): 30–45 seconds each side
- Tricep stretch (overhead, elbow bent): 30 seconds each side
- Forward fold (hamstring and lower back): 45–60 seconds
- Hip flexor lunge stretch (low lunge, back knee down): 45 seconds each side
- Quad stretch (standing or lying): 30–45 seconds each side
- Child's pose (full-body, breathing): 30–45 seconds

Breathe deeply and move slowly — this is your time to transition from training to recovery.

**Sleep & Recovery**
You're already hitting 7–8 hours, which is solid. To optimize for muscle growth and consistency, aim for 7.5–9 hours most nights. Here are some practical tweaks for your schedule:

1. **Chai cutoff**: Stop chai and caffeinated tea by 6:30 PM — your evening snack chai is fine, but nothing after that. Switch to herbal tea or warm milk if you want something warm before bed.

2. **Screen time**: Put your phone away by 10:30 PM. The blue light messes with melatonin production, and scrolling keeps your mind wired. If you're in bed by 10:45–11:00 PM, you're golden.

3. **Meal timing**: Your 9:00 PM dinner is late, which is common in Indian households. Aim to finish eating by 9:30 PM so your digestion isn't working overtime when you're trying to sleep. If you're hungry after, a small glass of warm milk or a handful of nuts is fine.

4. **Room setup**: Keep your bedroom cool and dark. Even a small fan helps. If you're in a shared space, a sleep mask can work wonders.

5. **Consistency**: Try to sleep and wake at the same time daily — even on weekends. Your body loves routine, and it'll boost recovery and energy.

6. **Pre-bed routine**: 20–30 minutes before bed, do some light stretching or breathing work (4-7-8 breathing: inhale for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8). It signals to your body that it's time to wind down.

7. **Hydration**: Drink your 2L during the day, but taper off after 7:00 PM to avoid middle-of-the-night bathroom trips.

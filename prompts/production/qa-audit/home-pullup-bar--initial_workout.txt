**Training Intensity & Rep Ranges — How Hard Should You Push?**

Compound lifts (pull-ups, dips, squats, rows) are where technique matters most. On these, leave 1–2 reps in the tank — that means you could do one or two more reps, but you stop before you do. This keeps your form tight and your joints healthy. You're building strength and muscle, not testing your max every session.

Isolation and accessory work (push-ups, split squats, leg raises, calf raises) — here's where you can push harder. Get to within 1–2 reps of failure, and on the last set of each exercise, if you hit strict failure, you can do a couple of controlled "cheat reps" (slight momentum or form adjustment) to squeeze out a few more. But never do this on compound barbell or bodyweight compound moves like squats, deadlifts, or dips. Keep those clean.

Core work sits somewhere in the middle — push it hard, but maintain form. No sloppy reps.

**Sleep & Recovery**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Aim for 7.5–9 hours on training days to maximize recovery. Here's what I'd suggest for your routine:

1. **Cut the evening chai by 6:00 PM.** I know chai is non-negotiable in Indian households, but caffeine after 6 PM will mess with your sleep quality. Enjoy your 6:30 PM snack of roasted chana, but stick to water or herbal tea after that.

2. **Eat your dinner 2–3 hours before bed.** You're eating at 9:00 PM, which is late. If you can shift dinner to 8:00–8:30 PM, you'll sleep better. If not, make sure it's lighter — roti and paneer is fine, but don't overload on rice or heavy curries close to bedtime.

3. **No screens 30 minutes before bed.** Phone, laptop, TV — all off. The blue light messes with melatonin production. Use this time to wind down, maybe do some light stretching or breathing.

4. **Keep your bedroom cool and dark.** If you're in a hot climate, a fan is your friend. If you can darken the room (curtains, eye mask), even better.

5. **Consistent sleep schedule.** Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on rest days. Your body thrives on rhythm.

6. **Hydration timing.** You're drinking 2L a day — good. But front-load it: drink most of your water before 6:00 PM. Sipping water right before bed means waking up for bathroom trips.

7. **Stress management.** You mentioned moderate stress. If work is heavy, take 5–10 minutes before bed to do some deep breathing or a body scan meditation. Even just lying in the dark and focusing on your breath helps.

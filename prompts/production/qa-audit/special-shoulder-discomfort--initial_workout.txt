## Warmup Routine (Do this before every training session — about 10 minutes)

Start with 5 minutes on the treadmill or bike, just enough to get your heart rate up and break a light sweat. Then move into some joint mobility — arm circles forward and back, leg swings front to back and side to side, hip circles, and a few cat-cow stretches on the floor. Finish with a set of 10-12 bodyweight squats and 10 push-ups against an incline (bench or bar set high) to wake up the muscles. Before you get into your main lifts each day, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe 40% and 60% of your working weight — to groove the movement and prep your nervous system.

## Day 1 — Lower Body Power

**Barbell Back Squat** — 4 sets x 5 reps  
Focus on depth and control. Keep your chest up, push your knees out, and drive through your heels. If the bar feels heavy on your back, try a high-bar position or use a pad until you build some comfort.

**Leg Press** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
You mentioned this is a favorite, so let's use it. Go deep, but don't let your lower back round off the pad at the bottom. Controlled descent, explosive push.

**Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)** — 3 sets x 6 reps  
This is all about the hamstrings and glutes. Keep a slight bend in your knees, hinge at the hips, and feel the stretch down the back of your legs. No rounding your lower back — if you feel that happening, reset your setup.

**Walking Lunges (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg  
Take your time, stay upright, and don't let your front knee cave inward. These will light up your quads and glutes.

**Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Squeeze at the top, lower with control. No swinging or bouncing.

**Seated Calf Raise** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Full range of motion — stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top, hold for a second.

**Core:**  
- Plank — 3 sets x 30-45 seconds  
- Lying Leg Raises — 3 sets x 12 reps  
- Russian Twists (Bodyweight or light dumbbell) — 3 sets x 20 total reps  

Aim for around 8,000-9,000 steps today — you're training, so stay lightly active outside the gym but don't overdo it.

## Day 2 — Upper Body Push

**Flat Barbell Bench Press** — 4 sets x 5 reps  
Plant your feet, squeeze your shoulder blades together, and press with control. Lower the bar to mid-chest, not your neck. Keep your elbows at about a 45-degree angle to protect your shoulders.

**Incline Dumbbell Press** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
Set the bench to about 30 degrees. This hits the upper chest without putting your shoulder in a risky position. Press up and slightly in, don't flare your elbows out wide.

**Cable Chest Fly** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Slight bend in your elbows, squeeze your chest at the center, and control the stretch on the way back. Think about hugging a tree.

**Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated, Neutral Grip)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
We're using a neutral grip (palms facing each other) to keep your shoulder safe. Press straight up, don't lean back. If you feel any pinching or pain, stop and let me know.

**Lateral Raise (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Light weight, controlled tempo. Lead with your elbows, not your hands, and don't swing. Stop just below shoulder height.

**Overhead Tricep Extension (Cable or Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Keep your elbows in place, don't let them flare out. Stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top.

**Tricep Pushdown (Cable)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Straight bar or rope, your choice. Lock your elbows at your sides and only move your forearms.

**Core:**  
- Cable Woodchop — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  
- Bicycle Crunches — 3 sets x 20 total reps  
- Dead Bug — 3 sets x 10 reps per side  

Step target: 8,000-9,000 steps.

## Day 4 — Lower Body Hypertrophy

**Goblet Squat (Dumbbell or Kettlebell)** — 4 sets x 10 reps  
Hold the weight at chest height, elbows pointing down. Squat deep, keep your torso upright, and drive through your heels. This is great for building your squat pattern and hitting your quads.

**Bulgarian Split Squat (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg  
Rear foot elevated on a bench. This will challenge your balance and absolutely torch your quads and glutes. Keep your torso upright and don't let your front knee cave in.

**Leg Press (Feet High and Wide)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Foot placement higher on the platform shifts more work to your glutes and hamstrings. Control the descent, explode up.

**Leg Extension (Machine)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Point your toes slightly out, squeeze your quads hard at the top, and lower with a 2-3 second tempo. This is pure quad isolation.

**Seated Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Same deal — squeeze at the top, control the descent. Focus on feeling your hamstrings do the work.

**Standing Calf Raise (Machine or Barbell)** — 3 sets x 20 reps  
High reps, full range. Calves respond well to volume, so don't rush these.

**Core:**  
- Hanging Knee Raises (or Lying if you can't hang yet) — 3 sets x 10 reps  
- Plank with Shoulder Taps — 3 sets x 20 total taps  
- Side Plank — 3 sets x 20-30 seconds per side

## Day 5 — Upper Body Pull

**Barbell Bent-Over Row** — 4 sets x 6 reps  
Hinge at the hips, keep your back flat, and pull the bar to your lower chest. Think about driving your elbows back, not just yanking with your arms. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top.

**Chest-Supported Dumbbell Row** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
You mentioned you love these, so here they are. Set the bench to about 30-45 degrees, lie face down, and row the dumbbells up toward your hips. No momentum, just pure back work.

**Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Pull the bar down to your upper chest, lean back slightly, and drive your elbows down and back. Don't just yank with your arms — think about pulling with your lats.

**Seated Cable Row (Neutral Grip)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Sit tall, pull the handle to your lower ribs, and squeeze your shoulder blades together. Control the stretch on the way back.

**Face Pulls (Cable)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
This is gold for your shoulders, especially with your impingement history. Pull the rope toward your face, elbows high, and rotate your hands out at the end. Focus on your rear delts and upper back.

**Dumbbell Bicep Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Stand tall, don't swing, and control the descent. Squeeze at the top.

**Hammer Curl (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Palms facing each other. This hits your brachialis and forearms a bit more than regular curls.

**Core:**  
- Cable Crunch — 3 sets x 15 reps  
- Mountain Climbers — 3 sets x 20 total reps  
- Pallof Press (Cable) — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  

Step target: 8,000-9,000 steps.

## Post-Workout Stretching Routine (5-10 minutes after each training session)

Hit the major muscle groups you trained that day. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds per side, breathe deep, and don't bounce. After lower days, focus on quads (standing quad stretch), hamstrings (seated or standing toe touch), glutes (figure-four stretch), hip flexors (kneeling lunge stretch), and calves (wall calf stretch). After upper days, hit your chest (doorway pec stretch), lats (overhead side bend or hanging from a bar), shoulders (cross-body arm stretch), and triceps (overhead tricep stretch). This will help with recovery and keep you mobile.

## Sleep and Recovery

You're getting 7-8 hours now, which is solid, but let's aim for 7.5-8 hours consistently — especially during this phase where we're asking your body to build muscle and recover from four training sessions a week. Based on your schedule, if you're training in the evenings and eating dinner around 9 pm, try to be winding down by 10:30-11 pm and asleep by 11:30 pm. That gets you up around 7-7:30 am, which should work with your office routine.

A few practical tips that work well for most people:

- Cut off chai and coffee by 4-5 pm — caffeine has a half-life of about 5-6 hours, so that evening cup can mess with your sleep even if you don't feel it.
- Finish dinner at least 2 hours before bed when possible. I know 9 pm is your usual time, so aim to be done eating by 9:15-9:30 pm and avoid heavy snacking after that.
- Dim the lights in your room an hour before bed and keep screens (phone, laptop, TV) out of the bedroom if you can. If you're scrolling before bed, use a blue light filter or night mode.
- Keep your room cool and dark — a fan or AC set to a comfortable temperature makes a big difference, especially in the warmer months.
- If your mind races at night, try a simple breathing routine — 4 seconds in through your nose, hold for 4, out through your mouth for 6. Do that for 5-10 rounds and it'll calm your nervous system down.
- Consistency matters more than perfection — try to go to bed and wake up at roughly the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body loves routine.

## Training Intensity and Effort

For the big compound lifts — squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows — leave 1-2 reps in the tank. That means if you think you could do 7 reps, stop at 5. We're building strength and technique here, not testing your limits every session. You should finish each set feeling like you worked hard but could have done a bit more if someone had a gun to your head.

For isolation and accessory work — leg curls, bicep curls, lateral raises, that kind of stuff — you can push closer to failure. The last couple of reps should be a grind, but still controlled. On the very last set of these exercises, if you hit technical failure (can't do another clean rep), you can squeeze out 1-2 controlled "cheat reps" with a tiny bit of momentum to finish strong — but only if the form doesn't completely break down. Never do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those need to stay strict and clean, every single rep.

If something hurts — not burns, but actually hurts, especially in your shoulder — stop immediately and swap the exercise. We're here to build muscle, not nurse injuries.

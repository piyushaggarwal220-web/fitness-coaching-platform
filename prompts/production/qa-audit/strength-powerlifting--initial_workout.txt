**WARMUP (10 minutes — every training day)**

Start with a brisk 2-3 minute walk on the treadmill or light jog to get your heart rate up. Then move through 5-6 minutes of joint mobility: arm circles and band pull-aparts (10 each), bodyweight squats (10), cat-cow stretches (8 each side), glute bridges (10), and a few inchworms. Finish with 2 lighter warm-up sets on your first compound lift before you load up to working weight — this primes your nervous system and greases the groove.

---

**Day 1 — Lower Power (Monday or your preferred first training day)**

Focus: Squat strength and posterior chain power. You'll feel this one, but it's the foundation for everything.

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Back Squat: 5 sets × 3-4 reps (heavy, controlled descent, 2-second pause at the bottom)
- Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 4 sets × 5 reps (keep the bar close, feel the hamstring stretch)
- Leg Press: 3 sets × 6 reps (go heavy here — your joints are fresh)

**Accessory:**
- Dumbbell Bulgarian Split Squats: 3 sets × 8 reps per leg (unilateral strength, great for balance)
- Cable Woodchops (high to low): 3 sets × 10 reps per side

**Core:**
- Barbell Rollouts: 3 sets × 8 reps (or use a dumbbell if needed — strict form, no hyperextension)
- Pallof Press (cable): 3 sets × 12 reps per side (anti-rotation, crucial for spinal stability)
- Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets × 10 reps (or knee raises if needed — control the swing)

**Daily Step Target:** 6,000–7,000 steps (training day, so add some walking before/after the gym or during your work day)

---

**Day 2 — Upper Push (Wednesday or 2 days after Day 1)**

Focus: Chest, shoulders, and triceps strength with hypertrophy finishers. This is where you build the press.

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Bench Press: 5 sets × 4-5 reps (tight upper back, elbows at ~45°, explosive up)
- Overhead Press (standing): 4 sets × 5 reps (core stays braced, no excessive arch)
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets × 6 reps (upper chest emphasis, controlled eccentric)

**Accessory:**
- Dumbbell Flyes: 3 sets × 10 reps (chest stretch, lighter weight, mind-muscle connection)
- Dumbbell Lateral Raises: 3 sets × 12 reps (shoulder health and shape)
- Rope Pushdowns (triceps): 3 sets × 12 reps (squeeze at the bottom, no momentum)

**Core:**
- Ab Wheel Rollouts: 3 sets × 8 reps (or kneeling if standing is too challenging — anti-extension)
- Suitcase Carries (dumbbell): 3 sets × 40 meters per side (grip strength, core stability)
- Dead Bugs: 3 sets × 12 reps per side (anti-rotation, great for lower back safety)

**Daily Step Target:** 6,500–7,500 steps (training day, keep momentum going)

**Day 3 — Active Recovery (Friday or 2 days after Day 2)**

This is your movement day — no heavy lifting, just mobility and blood flow.

- 15–20 minutes easy walk or light cycling (conversational pace)
- 10 minutes of dynamic stretching: leg swings, arm circles, torso twists, hip circles
- 10 minutes of static stretching: quad stretch (30 sec each), hamstring (30 sec each), chest doorway stretch (30 sec each), child's pose (60 sec)

**Daily Step Target:** 8,000–10,000 steps (recovery day, so get your steps in with a longer walk or by breaking them up throughout the day)

**Accessory:**
- Dumbbell Single-Arm Rows: 3 sets × 8 reps per side (unilateral strength, great for imbalances)

**Core:**
- Landmine Rotations: 3 sets × 10 reps per side (rotational power, great for spinal health)

**Daily Step Target:** 5,000–6,000 steps (heavy training day, so rest is priority — don't overdo steps)

**Days 5–7 (Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday) — Rest Days**

You've got three rest days to recover, eat well, and let your body adapt. On these days, keep steps between 4,000–5,500 — light movement is fine, but prioritize recovery.

**Training Intensity & Rep Ranges — How to Approach It**

On your main compound lifts (squat, bench, deadlift, rows, RDLs), you're working in the 3–6 rep range, which means heavy weight and low reps. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank on these — you should feel like you *could* do one or two more, but you stop before true failure. This keeps your form tight and your nervous system fresh for the next set.

On accessory and isolation work (flyes, lateral raises, curls, face pulls), you can push closer to failure — aim for the last set to be genuinely challenging. If you want to squeeze out a couple of controlled "cheat reps" after strict failure on isolation moves, that's fine, but *never* do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those lifts demand strict form because the risk of injury spikes when form breaks down.

Rest 2–3 minutes between heavy compound sets, 60–90 seconds on accessory work, and 45–60 seconds on isolation and core.

---

**Cooldown & Stretching (5–7 minutes after every session)**

Don't skip this. After your last set, spend a few minutes cooling down:

- Quad stretch (30 sec each leg, standing or kneeling)
- Hamstring stretch (30 sec each leg, forward fold or lying)
- Chest and shoulder stretch (doorway or cross-body, 30 sec each side)
- Hip flexor stretch (low lunge position, 30 sec each side)
- Child's pose or cat-cow (30–60 sec)

This takes 5–7 minutes and makes a huge difference in recovery and soreness.

**Sleep & Recovery — Your Secret Weapon**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Let's optimize it to hit 7.5–9 hours consistently. Your goal is to be in bed by 10:30–11:00 PM and wake around 7:00–7:30 AM.

Here are some practical tips for your lifestyle:

1. **Chai cutoff:** No chai or coffee after 4:00 PM. I know chai is a big part of your day, but the caffeine will mess with sleep quality. Stick to your 6:30 PM snack time, but keep it to herbal tea or milk if you want something warm.

2. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. The blue light from screens suppresses melatonin, and you need that to sleep deeply.

3. **Meal timing:** Your dinner at 9:00 PM is a bit late, especially with roti and paneer. Try to eat by 8:00–8:30 PM so your digestion isn't active when you're trying to sleep. A light snack (curd, banana) 1–2 hours before bed is fine.

4. **Room environment:** Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet. If you live in a noisy area, consider a white noise app or earplugs.

5. **Wind-down routine:** 20 minutes before bed, do something calm — light reading, stretching, or breathing exercises. Avoid work emails or stressful conversations.

6. **Water intake:** Your 2L during the day is good, but taper it off after 7:00 PM so you're not waking up to use the bathroom.

7. **Consistency:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on rest days. Your body thrives on routine.

Sleep is where the magic happens — your muscles recover, your hormones balance, and your strength improves. Don't treat it as an afterthought.

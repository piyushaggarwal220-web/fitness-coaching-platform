**WARMUP ROUTINE (10 minutes — do this before every session)**
- 2–3 minutes brisk walk or light jog on the treadmill
- Arm circles and shoulder dislocations with a light band or PVC pipe (10 reps each direction)
- Cat-cow stretches (10 reps)
- Bodyweight squats (15 reps, focus on depth and control)
- Glute bridges (15 reps, really squeeze at the top)
- Inchworms (10 reps)
- A couple of light arm swings and hip circles to loosen up

Then grab your lighter weights and do 2 warm-up sets of your first compound lift before moving into the working sets.

**DAY 1 — LOWER POWER (Monday)**
Focus: Heavy squat, explosive strength, posterior chain
Daily step target: 7,000–8,000 steps (training day — walk before or after the gym)

- Barbell Back Squat: 5 sets x 3–5 reps (heavy; leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set, focus on depth and control through the bottom)
- Barbell Deadlift: 4 sets x 3–4 reps (power focus; explosive off the floor, controlled eccentric)
- Leg Press: 3 sets x 6–8 reps (slightly higher reps here to accumulate volume after the main lifts)
- Walking Lunges (dumbbell): 3 sets x 8 reps per leg
- Core: Barbell Rollouts (or ab wheel if available): 3 sets x 8–12 reps; Pallof Press (cable): 3 sets x 10 reps per side

Cue: On the squat, drive through your midfoot and keep your chest up — you'll feel stronger. On deadlifts, the bar stays close to your shins the whole way up.

---

**DAY 2 — UPPER STRENGTH (Tuesday)**
Focus: Bench press, heavy horizontal pressing, rowing
Daily step target: 6,000–7,000 steps (training day)

- Barbell Bench Press: 5 sets x 4–6 reps (heavy; controlled descent, explosive drive off the chest)
- Barbell Rows (or Dumbbell Rows if you prefer): 4 sets x 5–6 reps (match the bench intensity; pull hard and squeeze at the top)
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 8–10 reps (slightly higher reps, but still strong)
- Dumbbell Flyes: 3 sets x 10–12 reps (chest stretch and squeeze; push closer to failure here)
- Core: Dead Bug (dumbbell): 3 sets x 10 reps per side; Suitcase Carries (dumbbell): 3 sets x 40 metres per side

Cue: On rows, think about pulling your elbows back, not your hands forward — bigger lat engagement. On bench, full control on the way down; don't bounce the bar.

---

**DAY 3 — ACTIVE RECOVERY (Wednesday)**
Daily step target: 4,000–5,000 steps (light activity; yoga, stretching, or a gentle walk)

No heavy lifting today. Instead, spend 30–40 minutes on mobility and light stretching:
- 5 minutes easy walking or cycling
- Thoracic rotations (10 per side)
- World's greatest stretch (5 per side)
- Deep bodyweight squats held for 30 seconds (2–3 reps)
- Pigeon pose (60 seconds each side)
- Child's pose (60 seconds)
- Cat-cow (10 reps)
- Quad stretches, hamstring stretches, hip flexor stretches (30–60 seconds each)
- Light foam rolling if you have access (calves, quads, back — 60 seconds each area)

This is your recovery day — let your body breathe, stay mobile, and prep for the final two heavy days.

---

**DAY 4 — UPPER HYPERTROPHY (Thursday)**
Focus: Volume, muscle building, secondary pressing and pulling
Daily step target: 6,500–7,500 steps (training day)

- Dumbbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 8–10 reps (higher reps, really control the eccentric — 2-second lowering)
- Cable Rows (or Dumbbell Rows): 4 sets x 8–10 reps (same rep range; keep tension on the lats)
- Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets x 8–10 reps
- Dumbbell Lateral Raises: 3 sets x 12–15 reps (lighter weight, high reps for shoulder health and shape)
- Dumbbell Pullovers: 3 sets x 12 reps (chest and lats; controlled stretch at the bottom)
- Core: Cable Woodchops: 3 sets x 12 reps per side; Hanging Knee Raises (or Lying Leg Raises): 3 sets x 10–12 reps

Cue: On hypertrophy days, tempo matters — 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up. On isolation work (lateral raises, pullovers), you can push the last set or two closer to failure, even a couple of controlled cheat reps if needed after strict form fails — just never on compound barbell lifts like squat or deadlift.

---

**DAY 5 — LOWER HYPERTROPHY (Friday)**
Focus: Quad and hamstring volume, glute strength, posterior chain
Daily step target: 6,500–7,500 steps (training day)

- Leg Press: 4 sets x 8–10 reps (higher reps for volume; full range of motion)
- Romanian Deadlift (dumbbell or barbell): 3 sets x 8–10 reps (hamstring and lower back focus)
- Leg Curls (cable or dumbbell leg curl if available): 3 sets x 10–12 reps
- Dumbbell Lunges: 3 sets x 10 reps per leg (controlled, full range)
- Dumbbell Goblet Squats: 3 sets x 12–15 reps (lighter, higher reps for quad pump)
- Core: Ab Wheel Rollouts: 3 sets x 10–12 reps; Landmine Rotations (or Cable Rotations): 3 sets x 12 reps per side

Cue: On the leg press, go deep without letting your lower back round off the pad. On RDLs, hinge at the hips, keep a slight knee bend, and feel the hamstring stretch.

---

**DAYS 6 & 7 — REST DAYS (Saturday & Sunday)**
Daily step target: 3,000–4,000 steps (very light activity — walk around, run errands, stay mobile but don't overdo it)

Full rest from the gym. If you're feeling good and want to stay active, a 20–30 minute walk is fine, but the focus here is recovery. Eat well, hydrate, and get your sleep dialed in.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (5–10 minutes after every training session)**
Do this right after your workout while you're still warm:
- Chest stretch (doorway or against a wall): 45 seconds each side
- Shoulder and tricep stretch: 45 seconds each side
- Quad stretch: 45 seconds each leg
- Hamstring stretch: 45 seconds each leg
- Hip flexor stretch: 45 seconds each side
- Cat-cow: 5–10 reps
- Child's pose: 60 seconds
- Spinal twist (lying): 45 seconds each side

Stay in each stretch, breathe deeply, and don't force anything — you're working on mobility and recovery, not flexibility competition.

**TRAINING INTENSITY NOTES**
Since you're advanced and chasing strength, here's the golden rule: on your main compound lifts (squat, bench, deadlift, rows), leave 1–2 reps in the tank on every set. You should feel strong and in control, not grinding out your last rep in desperation. This keeps you safe, lets you recover better, and you can add weight or reps next week.

On secondary and isolation work (flyes, lateral raises, leg curls, pullovers), you can push harder — aim to hit near failure on the last set or two, and if you've got a cheat rep or two left in the tank on isolation moves after strict form breaks down, that's fine. Just never do this on barbell compounds; those stay strict and controlled.

Rest 2–3 minutes between heavy compound sets, 60–90 seconds for secondary lifts, and 45–60 seconds for isolation work. This keeps your nervous system fresh for the big lifts and keeps your heart rate up for metabolic stress on the smaller movements.

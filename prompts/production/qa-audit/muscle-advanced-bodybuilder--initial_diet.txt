**MONDAY**

*Breakfast (8:30 am):*
Have 2 idli with 1 katori (approx 150g) sambar and a small bowl of coconut chutney. On the side, 1 cup curd (200g) with a pinch of salt.
(P: 12g | C: 38g | F: 4g | ~260 kcal)

*Mid-morning (10:30 am):*
Handful of roasted peanuts (approx 30g) and 1 glass water.
(P: 8g | C: 6g | F: 15g | ~180 kcal)

*Lunch (1:30 pm):*
1.5 cups cooked rice (approx 240g) with 1 katori (approx 150g) dal, a small bowl of mixed sabzi (approx 80g — spinach, carrot, beans), and 1 tsp ghee mixed in.
(P: 18g | C: 62g | F: 8g | ~420 kcal)

*Evening snack (6:30 pm):*
1 cup tea with 2 roasted chana (approx 40g total).
(P: 6g | C: 20g | F: 2g | ~110 kcal)

*Post-workout (if training today, around 8:00 pm):*
1 scoop whey protein mixed in 1 cup milk (200ml) with 1 banana (approx 120g).
(P: 28g | C: 35g | F: 5g | ~310 kcal)

*Dinner (9:00 pm):*
2 rotis with 150g paneer bhurji (paneer scrambled with onions and tomatoes), and a small bowl of salad (lettuce, cucumber, tomato).
(P: 32g | C: 30g | F: 18g | ~420 kcal)

**Quick notes as you go:**

- You're hitting the gym 6 days a week in the evening, so I've timed the whey shake post-workout on training days. On rest days, just skip that shake and you're good.
- Ghee is your friend here — it's calorie-dense and helps with absorption of fat-soluble vitamins. Use it generously but measure it (1 tsp = roughly 5g).
- Rotate your dals and curries to keep things interesting: masoor, chana, rajma, chickpea — they all bring different nutrients and keep your taste buds happy.
- Your 6:30 pm tea and chana slot is locked in — I've kept it consistent because routine is what makes consistency stick.
- If you ever swap rice for rotis at lunch, just adjust: 1.5 cups rice ≈ 3 rotis, so swap proportionally and the macros stay roughly the same.
- On rest days (if you take one), you can skip the post-workout shake and add an extra snack or adjust dinner slightly — but since you're training 6 days, this plan covers you well.

You've got the tools, the routine, and the foods you love. Now it's about showing up and staying consistent. Let's make this 12 weeks count.

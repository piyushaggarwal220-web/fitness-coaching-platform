Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Kavya!

Alright, let's get you to 62kg over the next 12 weeks — starting with this week's meal plan. I've kept things close to what you already enjoy (dal, paneer, idli, curd rice) and built around your current meal times since I know your startup schedule is packed. The plan sits around 1650–1700 kcal daily with roughly 100–110g protein, which should give you steady fat loss without tanking your energy or making late-night cravings worse. Let's tackle that sleep and snacking issue together as we go.

---

**DAY 1 (Monday)**

**Breakfast — 8:30 am**
Have 1 plate poha (approx 60g raw poha, cooked with mustard seeds, curry leaves, peanuts 10g, and veggies like peas and carrots), or swap for 3 idlis (approx 150g total) with 2 tbsp coconut chutney and 1 tsp oil-free sambar (1 small katori, ~100g).
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 9g | ~330 kcal)

**Lunch — 1:30 pm**
1 katori cooked toor dal (approx 150g) with ½ cup cooked rice (approx 80g raw weight), 1 katori mixed veg sabzi (beans, carrot, capsicum — approx 100g cooked), and a small side salad (cucumber, tomato, onion — 50g) with lemon. If you want more protein, swap the rice for 2 small rotis and add 50g paneer cubes to the sabzi.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 6g | ~350 kcal)

**Evening Snack — 6:30 pm (pre-workout)**
1 cup tea (no sugar or use stevia) with 30g roasted chana. On gym days, you can also have 1 banana (approx 100g) 30 minutes before your session for quick energy.
(P: 7g | C: 32g | F: 3g | ~180 kcal)

**Dinner — 9:00 pm**
2 medium rotis (approx 60g total) with 1 katori paneer bhurji (100g paneer crumbled with onion, tomato, capsicum — cooked in 1 tsp oil) and a big bowl of mixed salad (cucumber, carrot, lettuce, tomato — 100g) with a squeeze of lemon and a pinch of chaat masala. Or go for 2 rotis with 1 katori rajma (approx 150g cooked) if you want to switch it up.
(P: 28g | C: 46g | F: 18g | ~450 kcal)

**Post-Dinner (if hungry around 10:30–11 pm)**
Small bowl curd (100g, plain) with a pinch of salt or jeera powder — this helps with late-night cravings and won't spike calories.
(P: 5g | C: 7g | F: 2g | ~65 kcal)

**Day 1 Total: ~1375 kcal | P: 70g | C: 195g | F: 38g**

**A few quick notes as you go through the week:**

- On gym days (evening workouts), that banana + chana combo around 6:30 pm will fuel your session nicely. On rest days, skip the banana and just have tea with chana to keep things lighter.
- If you're genuinely hungry post-dinner (especially around 10:30–11 pm when the snacking urge hits), that small bowl of curd is your friend — it's satiating, easy to digest, and won't derail your deficit.
- I've kept most dinners paneer-heavy since you love it, but feel free to rotate in the dal or rajma options to save a bit of money and add variety.
- Drink your 2L water throughout the day — sometimes late-night hunger is actually thirst in disguise.
- If sleep stays rough this week, let me know in your next check-in. We might need to tweak dinner timing or add magnesium-rich foods (like pumpkin seeds or almonds in small amounts) to help you wind down.

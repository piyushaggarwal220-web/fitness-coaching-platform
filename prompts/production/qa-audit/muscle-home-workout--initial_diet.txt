**Quick notes as you go:**
- Swap rice for roti any day if you prefer—just keep portions similar (1.5 cups rice ≈ 2–3 rotis).
- Your roasted chana is a solid protein snack; buy it in bulk and dry-roast at home to save money.
- Don't skip the curd or buttermilk—they're affordable, gut-friendly, and add protein without fuss.
- If paneer prices spike, swap 150g paneer for 100g soya chunks + 50g curd cheese (paneer) mixed, or use extra dal on those days.
- Prep dal in bulk on weekends (moong, arhar, masoor) and freeze in portions—saves time and money.
- Light spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger-garlic paste) are your friends; they cost almost nothing and make every meal taste different.
- On training days (evenings), make sure you've had your lunch + afternoon snack well before your workout—you'll have solid energy.

You've got this, Meera. Stick to the rhythm, keep your portions close to what's listed, and let's build some solid muscle over the next 12 weeks. Reach out if anything feels off or if you need a swap.

**DAY 1 — LOWER POWER** (Evening)

Focus: Build strength in your lower body and prime your legs for the week. Squats are your star here.

Warmup:
- Brisk walk or light jog in place: 3–4 min
- Hip circles, leg swings, knee hugs, arm circles: 5–6 min
- 5 slow bodyweight squats with a chair for support

Main Work:
1. Banded Squats (Resistance Band around a door anchor or held overhead)
   - 2 warm-up sets x 8 reps (light tension)
   - 4 sets x 5 reps (heavy tension, controlled pace — 2 seconds down, 1 second up, leave 1–2 reps in reserve)
   - *Coach tip: Keep your chest up, knees tracking over your toes. If you feel wobbly, hold onto a chair or door frame for balance.*

2. Single-Leg Banded Squats (or split squats with rear foot elevated on a low surface)
   - 3 sets x 8 reps per leg (moderate tension)
   - *Cue: This builds stability and addresses imbalances. Don't rush — control the descent.*

3. Banded Romanian Deadlift (RDL) — great for hamstrings and posterior chain
   - 3 sets x 6 reps (heavy tension, hinge at the hips, minimal knee bend)
   - *Form check: Imagine pushing your hips back like you're closing a car door with your butt.*

4. Core Superset (perform back-to-back, then rest 60 seconds):
   - Dead Bug: 3 sets x 12 reps per side (lying on your back, opposite arm and leg extend slowly)
   - Banded Pallof Press (using door anchor at chest height): 3 sets x 10 reps per side (anti-rotation core work)

Finisher: 3 sets x 20 reps of banded glute bridges (light band, focus on the squeeze at the top)

Cooldown & Stretch (5 min):
- Quad stretch (hold door frame, pull foot to glute): 45 sec per leg
- Hamstring stretch (fold forward, gentle): 45 sec
- Hip flexor stretch (low lunge position): 45 sec per side
- Child's pose: 60 sec

Daily Step Target: 7,000–8,000 steps (includes your training session and normal daily movement).

**DAY 2 — UPPER PULL & CORE** (Evening, 48 hours after Day 1)

Focus: Rows are your main event here — building a strong back is crucial for posture, shoulder health, and fat loss. We'll add pulling variations and upper body accessory work.

Warmup:
- Brisk walk or light jog: 3–4 min
- Arm circles, shoulder rolls, band pull-aparts (light band): 5–6 min
- 5 slow wall pushups

Main Work:
1. Banded Rows (Anchor band at chest height on door, or use a low anchor and row from a slight lean)
   - 2 warm-up sets x 8 reps (light tension)
   - 4 sets x 5 reps (heavy tension, explosive pull, 1-second pause at chest, controlled return — leave 1–2 reps in reserve)
   - *Coach cue: Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of each rep. Think "elbows back, not hands forward."*

2. Banded Lat Pulldown (Band anchored high on door, pull down to chest)
   - 3 sets x 8 reps (moderate-heavy tension)
   - *Focus: Lead with your elbows, not your hands. Control the return — don't let the band snap you back.*

3. Banded Bicep Curls
   - 3 sets x 12 reps (moderate tension, slow 2-second lower)

4. Banded Reverse Flyes (or band pull-aparts at shoulder height)
   - 3 sets x 15 reps (light tension, rear shoulders and upper back)

5. Core Superset (perform back-to-back, then rest 60 seconds):
   - Plank Hold (forearm plank): 3 sets x 40–60 seconds
   - Banded Woodchops (anchor band at shoulder height, rotate and press diagonally down): 3 sets x 12 reps per side

Cooldown & Stretch (5 min):
- Chest stretch (clasp hands behind back, gentle pull): 45 sec
- Shoulder stretch (cross arm over chest, hold at elbow): 45 sec per arm
- Lat stretch (reach overhead and lean gently to the side): 45 sec per side
- Seated forward fold: 60 sec

Daily Step Target: 6,500–7,500 steps (lighter movement day, but still active).

**DAY 3 — FULL BODY HYPERTROPHY** (Evening, 48 hours after Day 2)

Focus: Balanced upper and lower work with higher reps to drive muscle endurance and metabolic stress — great for fat loss. This day ties everything together.

Warmup:
- Brisk walk or light jog: 3–4 min
- Dynamic stretches: leg swings, arm circles, cat-cow, hip circles: 5–6 min
- 5 slow squats + 5 slow wall pushups

Main Work:

   - 3 sets x 10 reps (moderate tension, steady pace)

2. Banded Chest Press (band anchored low on door, press forward at chest height)
   - 3 sets x 10 reps (moderate tension)
   - *Cue: Control the return — don't let the band snap you back. 2-second lower, explosive press.*

3. Banded Rows (lighter tension than Day 2, higher reps)
   - 3 sets x 10 reps (moderate tension)

4. Banded Lateral Raises (light band, side raises for shoulder width)
   - 3 sets x 15 reps

5. Banded Leg Curls (band around foot and upper leg, curl lying on stomach or seated)

6. Banded Tricep Extensions (band anchored overhead, press down)

7. Core Finisher (perform as a circuit, minimal rest):

**TRAINING INTENSITY & FORM NOTES**

Since you're new to structured training, here's what "leaving reps in reserve" means: On your heavy compound lifts (squats, rows, deadlifts), you should finish each set feeling like you could do 1–2 more reps if you absolutely had to. This keeps your joints safe and allows you to recover properly for your next session. On isolation and lighter accessory work (curls, raises, leg curls, etc.), you can push closer to failure — that last set should feel challenging. If you hit true failure on an isolation move, a couple of controlled "cheat reps" (slight form bend to squeeze out extra volume) are fine, but never on your heavy compounds. Form always comes first.

Also, because you're training at home with bands, the tension curve is different than a barbell — bands get harder as they stretch. Use that to your advantage. Control the eccentric (lowering) phase especially, as that's where you build strength and muscle.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already hitting 7–8 hours, which is solid. To optimize recovery for fat loss and training, aim for 7.5–9 hours when you can. Here are some practical tweaks for your lifestyle:

1. **Chai cutoff:** No chai or tea after 6:30 PM. I know it's tough in India, but the caffeine will mess with your sleep if you're having your evening snack tea at 6:30. Stick to herbal tea or just water after that.

2. **Dinner timing:** You're eating dinner at 9 PM, which is late. If possible, try shifting it to 8:30 PM — gives you 30 minutes to digest before bed. If 9 PM is locked in due to your schedule, that's okay, but aim for lighter portions (roti, paneer, salad is good; just don't overload the roti).

3. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. Blue light messes with melatonin production. If you like scrolling, use a blue light filter or night mode.

4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool if possible — around 18–20°C is ideal, but even opening a window for fresh air helps.

5. **Consistency:** Try to sleep and wake at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body will adapt and sleep quality will improve.

6. **Pre-bed ritual:** 15 minutes of light stretching or breathing exercises before bed signals your body it's time to wind down. You can do some of the stretches from your cooldown routine.

7. **Hydration:** You're drinking 2L of water, which is good. Taper it after 7 PM so you're not waking up to use the bathroom at 2 AM.

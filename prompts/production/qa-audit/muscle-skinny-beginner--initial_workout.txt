**WARM-UP (Every Session — ~10 minutes)**

Start every workout like this:
- 2–3 min brisk walk or light treadmill jog
- 5–10 arm circles and leg swings (10 each direction)
- 10 bodyweight squats (slow, controlled)
- 10 cat-cow stretches
- 10 glute bridges
- A few light band pull-aparts if available

Then, before you hit your main lifts, run through 2 lighter warm-up sets — nothing heavy, just to groove the movement and get your nervous system ready.

---

**DAY 1 — Lower Power & Strength**

*Goal: Build leg strength and power. Aim for 5000–6000 steps today (training day).*

- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps
  *This is your anchor. Load it heavy enough that the last rep feels hard but clean. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set. Drive through your heels, chest up.*

- Barbell Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets x 6 reps
  *Hinge at the hips, feel the hamstring stretch. Controlled descent — no bouncing.*

- Leg Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
  *Go a touch heavier here. Full range of motion, but don't lock out your knees at the top.*

- Leg Curls (machine or cable): 3 sets x 12 reps
  *Squeeze hard at the top. This is accessory work, so push closer to failure on the last set.*

- Core: Barbell Rollouts (or Ab Wheel): 3 sets x 8–10 reps
  *Controlled, not rushed. Your core will thank you.*

---

**DAY 2 — Upper Push & Hypertrophy**

*Goal: Build chest, shoulders, and triceps. Aim for 5000–6000 steps today (training day).*

- Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 6 reps
  *Pause briefly at the chest, explode up. Full control, no bouncing off your ribs.*

- Dumbbell Incline Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
  *Dumbbells give you a deeper stretch. Controlled descent, powerful drive up.*

- Overhead Press (barbell or dumbbell): 3 sets x 6 reps
  *Strict form. No leg drive. Shoulders, chest, and triceps locked in.*

- Cable Flyes (or Pec Deck): 3 sets x 12 reps
  *Squeeze at the midpoint. Feel the stretch. Last set, push to near failure.*

- Tricep Rope Pushdowns: 3 sets x 12 reps
  *Full range, controlled. Last set, a couple of cheat reps are okay after strict failure.*

- Core: Dead Bugs: 3 sets x 10 reps per side
  *Slow and deliberate. This teaches you spinal stability.*

---

**DAY 3 — Active Recovery**

*Target: 7000–8000 steps. Light movement day.*

No heavy lifting. Instead:
- 15–20 min easy walk or light cycling
- 10–15 min mobility flow: wrist circles, shoulder dislocates, hip openers, thoracic rotations
- Light stretching (hold each 45–60 seconds): hamstrings, quads, hip flexors, chest, shoulders
- If you fancy, 2–3 min of core breathing work (dead bugs, bird dogs, glute bridges — all slow and controlled)

This day keeps you moving without taxing your nervous system. Perfect for a Friday after two hard sessions.

---

**DAY 4 — Upper Pull & Back**

*Goal: Build back thickness and strength. Aim for 5000–6000 steps today (training day).*

- Barbell Rows (Pendlay or Chest-Supported): 4 sets x 5 reps
  *This is your second anchor lift. Heavy, controlled, explosive drive. Think about pulling your elbows back, not just your hands.*

- Lat Pulldowns (or Assisted Pull-ups if you're strong enough): 3 sets x 8 reps
  *Full stretch at the top, explosive pull. Mind the mind-muscle connection.*

- Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets x 8 reps per side
  *Single-arm work. Lots of core stability here. Don't let your torso rotate.*

- Face Pulls (cable): 3 sets x 15 reps
  *High reps, light weight. Shoulder health and rear delt work. Squeeze at the top.*

- Barbell Curls: 3 sets x 8 reps
  *Strict form. Elbows stay at your sides. Last set, a couple of cheat reps after strict failure is fine.*

- Core: Pallof Presses (cable): 3 sets x 10 reps per side
  *Anti-rotation work. Huge for core strength and stability.*

---

**DAY 5 — Rest Day**

*Target: 3000–4000 steps (light, leisurely movement only).*

Full rest. Your muscles grow when you recover. If you're feeling restless, a slow walk is fine, but keep it chill. No structured training.

**TRAINING INTENSITY NOTES**

Here's the rule: on your heavy compounds (squats, deadlifts, bench, rows, overhead press), you should leave 1–2 reps in the tank. You're not going to absolute failure on these — that's how you stay healthy and recover well. On isolation and accessory work (leg curls, flyes, pushdowns, face pulls, curls), you can push harder. On the last set of isolation exercises, if you hit strict failure and want a couple of controlled "cheat reps" to squeeze out extra volume, that's okay — but never do this on barbell compounds like squats or deadlifts. Form is non-negotiable.

---

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (After Every Session — ~5 minutes)**

After you finish, spend a few minutes stretching the main muscles you trained:
- Chest: doorway stretch or cross-body shoulder stretch (30–60 sec each side)
- Shoulders: overhead tricep stretch (30–60 sec each side)
- Quads: standing quad stretch (30–60 sec each leg)
- Hamstrings: forward fold or seated hamstring stretch (30–60 sec)
- Lower back: child's pose or lying knee-to-chest (30–60 sec each side)

Don't rush this. It helps with soreness, mobility, and signals to your body that the session is done.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid. To optimize for muscle growth, aim for 7.5–9 hours when you can. Here's what works in an Indian context:

1. **Chai cutoff:** No chai or tea after 7 PM. I know it's part of the routine, but caffeine will mess with your sleep. Stick to your 6:30 PM tea with roasted chana, then switch to water or warm milk if you're peckish later.

2. **Meal timing:** Your 9 PM dinner is late. Try to finish by 8:30 PM if possible — gives your body time to digest before bed. If you're training in the evening, a small post-workout snack (banana + curd or a glass of milk) 30–45 min after you finish is fine, but nothing heavy.

3. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. The blue light messes with melatonin production. If you're scrolling in bed, you're not sleeping.

4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool. 18–20°C is ideal, but even just opening a window helps.

5. **Consistency:** Try to hit the bed around the same time every night, even on rest days. Your body loves routine.

6. **Light stretching before bed:** 5–10 minutes of gentle stretching or breathing work signals your nervous system to wind down. Great after a training day.

7. **Avoid heavy meals close to sleep:** Your 9 PM dinner is already on the later side. If it's heavy (lots of roti and paneer), you might sleep less deeply. Balance it with lighter options when you can.

Your current 7–8 hours is a good baseline. As you increase training volume and intensity, prioritize hitting that 8–9 hour range — that's where the magic happens for muscle gain.

**WARMUP ROUTINE (10 minutes — do this before every session)**
Start with 2–3 minutes of brisk walking or light jogging on the treadmill to get your heart rate up and loosen your joints. Then move through 5–6 minutes of dynamic mobility: arm circles (20 each direction), bodyweight squats (15 reps, focus on depth), scapular push-ups or wall push-ups (10 reps), leg swings forward/back and side-to-side (10 each leg), and some torso twists (20 total). Finish with a few cat-cow stretches and you're ready to roll. Before you get into your main lifts, always run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe 50% of your working weight for 5–6 reps, then 70% for 3 reps. It primes your nervous system and keeps injuries out of the picture.

**DAY 1 — LOWER POWER (Monday)**

Focus: Squat strength, posterior chain, core stability

*Barbell Back Squat*
- 4 sets × 4–5 reps
- Rest 3 minutes between sets
- Cue: Drive through your midfoot, keep your chest up, and control the descent. Leave one rep in the tank on the first two sets; on set 3, push to 6 reps if you can stay strict. Set 4 is your heavy one — 4 reps, full effort.

*Leg Press*
- 3 sets × 6–8 reps
- Rest 2 minutes
- This is your secondary strength move. Go heavy but controlled — no lockout bouncing.

*Romanian Deadlift (Dumbbell or Barbell)*
- 3 sets × 8 reps
- Rest 90 seconds
- Feel the hamstring stretch at the bottom. This builds posterior chain resilience.

*Leg Curl (Machine or Cable)*
- 3 sets × 12 reps
- Rest 60 seconds
- Squeeze hard at the top; control on the way down.

*Ab Wheel Rollout (or Assisted)*
- 3 sets × 8–10 reps
- Rest 60 seconds
- If you can't do it from the knees, use the machine or a cable. This is serious core work.

*Pallof Press (Cable)*
- 2 sets × 10 reps per side
- Rest 45 seconds

*Dead Bug*
- 2 sets × 12 reps per side
- Rest 45 seconds
- Slow and controlled. This ties everything together.

**TRAINING INTENSITY GUIDANCE**

Here's how I want you to approach intensity: On your compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, bench, rows), leave a rep or two in the tank on the first couple of sets. By set 3 and 4, push harder — you should finish feeling like you could do one or two more reps, but not more than that. This is the sweet spot for strength and hypertrophy without grinding yourself into the ground.

On isolation work (leg curls, lat pulldowns, lateral raises, curls, extensions), you can push closer to failure. The last set of these movements, if you hit strict failure and want to squeeze out one or two cheat reps (slight momentum, partial range), that's fine — but never do this on squats, deadlifts, Romanian deadlifts, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those movements demand perfect form to stay safe.

Rest periods: Use them. Don't scroll Instagram for 5 minutes and then jump back in. Rest 2.5–3 minutes on heavy compounds, 90 seconds on secondary lifts, and 60 seconds on isolation and core work. This matters for strength and recovery.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid, but let's aim for 7.5–9 hours to really support muscle growth. Since you train in the evenings (usually wrapping up around 8–8:30 pm), try to be in bed by 10:30–11 pm and aim to wake around 7–7:30 am. Here are some practical tweaks for your routine:

1. **Cut off chai and tea by 5 pm** — I know chai is non-negotiable in India, but the caffeine will mess with your sleep if you're drinking it late. Your 6:30 pm snack can still include chana, just skip the tea or switch to herbal.

2. **No screens 45 minutes before bed** — this is tough, but blue light disrupts melatonin. Put the phone away after 10 pm.

3. **Eat dinner earlier if possible** — you're eating at 9 pm, which is close to bedtime. If you can shift dinner to 8–8:15 pm, your digestion won't interfere with sleep. If not, keep portions reasonable at night.

4. **Keep your room cool and dark** — aim for 16–18°C if you can. Use blackout curtains or an eye mask.

5. **Avoid heavy meals 2–3 hours before bed** — your 9 pm dinner is cutting it close. If you're hungry late, a light snack (banana, curd, or a handful of nuts) is fine, but nothing heavy.

6. **Stick to a consistent sleep schedule** — same bedtime and wake time, even on weekends. Your body loves routine.

7. **If you're wired after training, do 5 minutes of deep breathing or a quick meditation** — training can spike cortisol, so wind down properly.

Good sleep is where the muscle actually grows, so don't skimp on this.

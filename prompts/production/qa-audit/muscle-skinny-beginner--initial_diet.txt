7-Day Muscle Gain Diet Plan: Hey Amit,

Great to have you on board! I can see you're aiming to hit 68 kg in 12 weeks, and honestly, the fact that you've already identified that eating *enough* is your challenge puts you ahead of most guys starting out. We're going to fix that this week—your meal plan is built around your existing routine (breakfast at 8:30, lunch at 1:30, tea at 6:30, dinner at 9), and I've packed in the calories and protein you need to build muscle without forcing you to eat at weird times. **MONDAY** (Non-veg day)

**Breakfast (8:30 am)**
Have 2 idli with 1 katori (approx 150g) sambar and 1 tbsp (20g) coconut chutney, plus 1 glass (200ml) full-fat milk with a pinch of turmeric.
(P: 12g | C: 48g | F: 8g | ~310 kcal)

Or swap the idli for 1.5 cups (approx 120g) poha mixed with 2 beaten eggs, 1 small onion, and a pinch of turmeric—fry it in 1 tsp oil and serve with a squeeze of lemon.
(P: 18g | C: 42g | F: 14g | ~380 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm)**
Have 1.5 cups (approx 200g) cooked rice with 1 katori (approx 150g) dal (any kind—arhar, moong, masoor), 1 small bowl (approx 100g) mixed sabzi (carrot, beans, peas, capsicum), and 1 tbsp (10g) ghee stirred through.
(P: 16g | C: 62g | F: 10g | ~420 kcal)

Or keep the rice and dal the same, but swap the sabzi for 80g paneer bhurji (crumbled paneer cooked with onion and tomato in 1 tsp oil).
(P: 22g | C: 58g | F: 14g | ~450 kcal)

**Evening snack (6:30 pm)**
1 cup (approx 150ml) chai (strong tea with milk) and a small handful (approx 40g) roasted chana, plus 2 slices (approx 60g) whole wheat bread with 1 tbsp (20g) peanut butter.
(P: 14g | C: 35g | F: 11g | ~290 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm)**
Have 3 rotis (approx 180g) with 150g paneer cooked in a light tomato-onion gravy (use 1 tbsp oil, 1 medium onion, 1 medium tomato, 1/2 tsp turmeric, salt), and a small side salad (cucumber, tomato, lettuce, 1 tsp oil + lemon).
(P: 26g | C: 50g | F: 16g | ~480 kcal)

**A few quick tips as you go:**

- **On the ghee:** I've built it into lunch most days because it's calorie-dense and helps you hit your targets. Don't skip it or swap it for less oil—that's the whole point.
- **Chutney swap:** If you get bored of coconut chutney, make a quick peanut chutney (roasted peanuts, green chilli, salt, lemon, water—blend it up). Same macros, different vibe.
- **Paneer tip:** Buy it in bulk on weekends and store it in the fridge in salted water. Saves time and money through the week.
- **Dal variety:** Rotate between arhar, moong, and masoor—they all work, and switching keeps your palate fresh.
- **Salad:** Stick with raw cucumber, tomato, lettuce, and lemon. No dressing needed if you're already getting fat from oil in the meals. Keeps it simple.
- **Water:** You're at 2L—try to bump it to 3L on gym days. Helps with recovery and keeps hunger honest.

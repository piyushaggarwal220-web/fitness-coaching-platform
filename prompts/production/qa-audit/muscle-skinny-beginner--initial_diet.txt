**A few practical tips:**

— **Paneer tip:** Buy paneer from a local vendor if you can — it's usually fresher and cheaper than supermarket packs. Store it in salted water in the fridge, and it'll last 5-6 days. Paneer bhurji is your friend here; it's quick, tasty, and packs protein.

— **Dal variety:** Don't stick to one type of dal all week. Moong, masoor, toor, arhar, chana dal — they all have slightly different flavours and textures. Rotating them keeps things interesting and ensures you get a range of micronutrients.

— **Roasted chana:** Buy dried chana (chickpeas) in bulk and roast them at home with a pinch of salt and maybe some chaat masala. Way cheaper than packaged snacks, and it's a protein powerhouse. Keep a jar ready for your evening tea.

— **Curd rice:** This is gold for you. It's light, tasty, and pairs brilliantly with a paneer dish. If you make it fresh, add a pinch of salt, a few curry leaves, and maybe some roasted peanuts on top.

— **Ghee:** A teaspoon in your dal or rice goes a long way for flavour and calories. Don't skip it — it's affordable and essential.

— **Milk:** If plain milk feels boring, warm it up with a pinch of turmeric, a touch of honey, or a small pinch of cardamom. Small changes make it easier to drink consistently.

— **Salad:** Keep it simple — cucumber, tomato, lettuce, maybe a carrot. A squeeze of lemon and a pinch of salt is all you need. Salad is free nutrition; don't overthink it.

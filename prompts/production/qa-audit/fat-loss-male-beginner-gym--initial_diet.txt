**MONDAY**

*Breakfast (8:30 am):*
Have 1.5 cups poha [approx 120g] made with 1 tsp oil, mixed with 1 small onion, 1 green chilli, and a pinch of turmeric. Serve with a glass of lemon juice squeezed over it and a small side of roasted peanuts [approx 30g]. If you want to swap the poha, go for 2 idlis [approx 100g] with a small bowl of sambar [approx 150g] instead.
(P: 8g | C: 42g | F: 8g | ~280 kcal)

*Lunch (1:30 pm):*
Have 1 katori cooked rice [approx 150g], 1 katori dal [approx 150g cooked], and a small bowl of mixed sabzi (carrot, beans, peas) [approx 100g] cooked with 1 tsp oil. Add a pinch of salt and cumin.
(P: 12g | C: 58g | F: 4g | ~320 kcal)

*Evening snack (6:30 pm):*
Have your regular cup of tea with 1 small cup roasted chana [approx 40g] on the side. If you want something different, swap the chana for a small bowl of curd [approx 100g] with a pinch of salt and pepper.
(P: 8g | C: 20g | F: 3g | ~130 kcal)

*Dinner (9:00 pm):*
Have 2 rotis [approx 80g], 100g paneer cooked with onions and tomatoes (paneer bhurji style), and a small side salad with cucumber, tomato, and lemon dressing [approx 150g]. Use 1 tsp oil for the paneer.
(P: 22g | C: 32g | F: 12g | ~310 kcal)

I've noticed the daily totals are running a bit light (around 1150–1160 kcal most days). Here's what I want you to do: **add a mid-morning snack on most days** — a banana [approx 120g], a slice of whole-wheat bread with 1 tbsp peanut butter, or a small bowl of roasted chana with jaggery. So by adding those mid-morning snacks (roughly 300–400 kcal, 20–25g protein), you'll hit the sweet spot.

**A few practical tips as you go:**

- **Dal & rice:** Cook dal with a pinch of turmeric and cumin for better digestion and flavor. If you're bored, swap regular dal for masoor or moong — they cook faster and taste slightly different.
- **Fish:** Pan-fry it in minimal oil with lemon, turmeric, and salt. Takes 5 minutes. No need to overcomplicate it.
- **Paneer:** It's your friend for protein and satiety. Keep it simple — pan-fry with onions and tomatoes, or marinate in yogurt and spices before cooking.
- **Curd rice:** Make it fresh; it's light, filling, and great for dinner. Mix in cucumber, carrot, and a pinch of salt.
- **Weekends:** I know you mentioned weekend eating is tricky. This plan gives you a bit more flexibility on Saturday and Sunday (slightly more jaggery, a couple of eggs), but the structure stays the same. Stick to meal times and portions, and you'll be fine.
- **Tea:** Keep it black or with minimal milk. If you need milk, use 1/4 cup instead of 1/2 cup to save calories.

Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Deepak!

Great to have you on board. I know banking hours can be brutal and stress eating is real — so we've built this week's plan around your usual meal times and the foods you already enjoy. The goal is simple: create a sustainable deficit that gets you moving toward 62kg without feeling like you're starving or spending hours in the kitchen. Let's keep it practical and tasty.

---

**DAY 1 (Monday)**

**Breakfast — 8:30 am**
Have 1 plate poha (approx 60g raw poha, cooked with curry leaves, mustard seeds, and peanuts — about 15g peanuts). Add a handful of roasted chana (20g) on the side. Or swap for 3 idlis with 2 tbsp coconut chutney and 1 tbsp peanut chutney.
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 10g | ~340 kcal)

**Lunch — 1:30 pm**
1 katori cooked dal (approx 150g moong or masoor dal), 1 small katori rice (about 80g cooked), 1 katori mixed veg sabzi (lauki, beans, or bhindi — around 100g), and a small bowl of curd (100g). If you want more protein, swap the rice for 2 rotis and add 50g paneer cubes to the sabzi.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack — 6:30 pm**
Tea with 30g roasted chana. Keep it light — this is just to hold you till dinner.
(P: 6g | C: 16g | F: 2g | ~105 kcal)

**Dinner — 9:00 pm**
2 rotis with 100g paneer bhurji (cooked with onions, tomatoes, and a pinch of turmeric) and a big bowl of salad (cucumber, tomato, carrot — about 150g, squeeze some lemon). Or swap the bhurji for 1 katori rajma (approx 150g cooked) if you're craving something different.
(P: 26g | C: 48g | F: 14g | ~430 kcal)

**DAY 2 (Tuesday)**

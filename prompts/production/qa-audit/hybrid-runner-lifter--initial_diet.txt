Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Aditya!

Alright, let's get you sorted for the week. You're juggling running and lifting at a pretty high volume, so recovery nutrition is going to be key here — we need to fuel those evening sessions properly and help your body bounce back without feeling sluggish. I've kept things close to your usual meal times and built around the foods you already enjoy (dal, paneer, idli, curd rice), all within your ₹8000/month budget. Expect around 2100 kcal daily with solid protein spread across the day to support your athletic performance goal.

**Monday**

*Breakfast – 8:30 am*
Start with 3 idlis (approx 150g total) served with 2 tbsp coconut chutney and 1 small bowl sambar (about 100ml). If you want a bit more protein, add a small katori (roughly 100g) of plain curd on the side.
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 8g | ~320 kcal)

*Lunch – 1:30 pm*
Go with 1 cup cooked rice (about 150g), 1 katori moong dal tadka (approx 150g cooked), one small katori mixed veg sabzi, and a side of cucumber-tomato salad. Alternatively, swap the rice for 2 medium rotis if you feel like it.
(P: 18g | C: 68g | F: 10g | ~430 kcal)

*Evening Snack – 6:30 pm (Pre-Workout)*
Your usual tea with 30g roasted chana. On training days, this sits well before your evening session — light but enough to keep energy steady.
(P: 6g | C: 15g | F: 2g | ~100 kcal)

*Post-Workout (around 7:45 pm)*
1 medium banana with 200ml low-fat milk — quick, easy, gets some carbs and protein in right after you finish.
(P: 8g | C: 40g | F: 3g | ~215 kcal)

*Dinner – 9:00 pm*
2 rotis with 1 katori paneer bhurji (about 100g paneer), a small bowl of stir-fried bhindi or beans, and a side salad (cucumber, tomato, onion with a squeeze of lemon). If you want to switch it up, replace the paneer with 1 katori rajma for a change.
(P: 28g | C: 50g | F: 18g | ~480 kcal)

*Before Bed (optional)*
A small katori of curd (100g) if you're still a bit hungry — helps with digestion and adds a little extra protein.
(P: 5g | C: 6g | F: 2g | ~60 kcal)

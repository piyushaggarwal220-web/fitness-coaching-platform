**Day 1 — Lower Body Power**

Today's all about building strength in your legs and posterior chain. Since you're new to this, focus on controlled reps and solid depth on squats — don't rush the descent.

- **Barbell Back Squat** — 4 sets x 5 reps  
  Keep your chest up, break at the hips and knees together, and aim for just below parallel. Breathe in at the top, brace your core, and exhale as you drive up. Rest 3 minutes between sets.

- **Leg Press** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
  Feet shoulder-width, push through your heels. Don't let your lower back round at the bottom. Rest 2 minutes.

- **Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)** — 3 sets x 6 reps  
  Hinge at the hips, keep a slight bend in the knees, and feel the stretch in your hamstrings. Bar stays close to your legs the whole way. Rest 2.5 minutes.

- **Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  Control the negative — don't just let the weight drop. Squeeze at the top. Rest 90 seconds.

- **Standing Calf Raise (Machine or Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
  Full range of motion — stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top. Rest 60 seconds.

**Core Work:**

- **Plank** — 3 sets x 30 seconds  
  Elbows under shoulders, glutes tight, don't let your hips sag.

- **Lying Leg Raises** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  Press your lower back into the floor, lift with control, lower slowly.

- **Russian Twists (Bodyweight or Light Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 20 reps (10 each side)  
  Sit back slightly, twist from the torso, not just your arms.

**Daily Steps:** Aim for 7,000 steps today — you've trained, so keep moving but don't overdo it. A walk after dinner helps digestion and keeps you active without adding stress.

**Day 2 — Upper Body Hypertrophy**

We're going a bit higher rep today to build some muscle endurance and get a good pump going. You'll feel this one tomorrow.

- **Barbell Bench Press** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
  Grip just outside shoulder-width, lower the bar to mid-chest, press up with control. Keep your shoulder blades squeezed together. Rest 2.5 minutes.

- **Barbell Bent-Over Row** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
  Hinge at the hips, slight knee bend, pull the bar to your lower chest. Elbows stay close to your body. This is one of your favorites, so own it. Rest 2.5 minutes.

- **Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  Press straight up, don't arch your back excessively. Rest 2 minutes.

- **Lat Pulldown (Cable)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  Pull to your upper chest, squeeze your shoulder blades together at the bottom. Rest 90 seconds.

- **Cable Chest Fly** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
  Slight bend in the elbows, focus on the squeeze in the middle. Rest 60 seconds.

- **Cable Bicep Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  Keep your elbows pinned, curl with control, squeeze at the top. Rest 60 seconds.

- **Cable Tricep Pushdown** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  Elbows stay at your sides, extend fully at the bottom. Rest 60 seconds.

**Core Work:**

- **Hanging Knee Raises (or Lying if Hanging is Too Hard)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  Lift your knees to hip height, control the swing.

- **Cable Woodchop** — 3 sets x 12 reps each side  
  Rotate from your core, not just your arms. Keep your hips stable.

- **Dead Bug** — 3 sets x 10 reps each side  
  Press your lower back into the floor, extend opposite arm and leg slowly.

**Daily Steps:** Target 7,000 steps again. A short walk after your evening session helps bring your heart rate down and clears your head after a long day at the bank.

**Sleep Guidelines**

You're already getting 7-8 hours, which is solid, but let's tighten it up a bit. Aim for 7.5-8.5 hours consistently — your body repairs and builds muscle while you sleep, and fat loss happens more efficiently when you're well-rested.

Given your 8:30 am breakfast and evening workout schedule, try to be in bed by 10:30-11:00 pm and asleep by 11:00-11:30 pm. That gets you up around 7:00-7:30 am, which should work with your banking hours.

A few tips to help you sleep better:

- **Cut off chai and coffee by 4:00 pm.** Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours, so anything after mid-afternoon can mess with your sleep, even if you don't feel it.

- **Keep your dinner at 9:00 pm, but make it lighter if you can.** A heavy meal right before bed can interfere with sleep quality. If you're hungry later, a small bowl of curd is fine.

- **Dim the lights and put your phone away by 10:00 pm.** Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin. If you need to use your phone, switch it to night mode or use blue light blocking glasses.

- **Keep your room cool and dark.** If you don't have AC, a fan and blackout curtains help. Your body sleeps better in a cooler environment.

- **Try a short wind-down routine.** 10 minutes of light stretching, deep breathing, or reading (not on a screen) signals to your body that it's time to sleep.

- **Avoid scrolling social media or watching intense shows right before bed.** Your brain needs time to downshift. Save the Netflix thrillers for earlier in the evening.

- **If work stress is keeping you up, try a brain dump before bed.** Write down anything on your mind for 5 minutes — tasks, worries, whatever. Getting it out of your head and onto paper helps.

**Training Intensity Guidance**

You're new to this, so we're not chasing failure on every set. For your big compound lifts — squats, deadlifts, presses, rows — I want you to leave 1-2 reps in the tank. That means if you think you could do 7 reps, stop at 5 or 6. This keeps your form clean and your nervous system fresh, and it's safer given your mild hypertension.

On isolation and accessory work — leg curls, bicep curls, lateral raises, calf raises — you can push a bit closer to failure, maybe leaving just 1 rep in reserve. On the last set of these exercises, if you hit failure with strict form and feel like you can squeeze out 1-2 more reps with a little bit of momentum or assistance (like a slight swing on a curl or a small push on a press), that's fine — but only on isolation moves, and only on the last set. Never do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound lift. Those require full control and clean form every single rep.

If something feels off — sharp pain, dizziness, or your blood pressure spiking — stop immediately. We're here to build you up, not break you down.

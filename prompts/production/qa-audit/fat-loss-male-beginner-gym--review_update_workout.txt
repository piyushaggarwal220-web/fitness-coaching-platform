**Upper/Lower Split – 4 Days Per Week**

You've completed your foundation phase with solid progress. We're now shifting to a structured upper/lower split to increase training frequency per muscle group while keeping session length around 60 minutes. Volume has been increased by approximately 15% through added sets and slight rep adjustments. Continue focusing on form and progressive overload.

**Day 1 – Upper Body (Push Focus)**
1. Barbell Bench Press – 4 sets x 8 reps
2. Dumbbell Shoulder Press – 3 sets x 10 reps
3. Cable Tricep Pushdown – 3 sets x 12 reps
4. Dumbbell Lateral Raise – 3 sets x 12 reps
5. Cable Chest Fly – 3 sets x 12 reps

**Day 2 – Lower Body (Quad Focus)**
1. Barbell Back Squat – 4 sets x 8 reps
2. Leg Press – 3 sets x 12 reps
3. Walking Dumbbell Lunges – 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
4. Leg Extension (machine or cable) – 3 sets x 15 reps
5. Standing Calf Raise – 3 sets x 15 reps

**Day 3 – Upper Body (Pull Focus)**
1. Barbell Bent-Over Row – 4 sets x 8 reps
2. Lat Pulldown (cable) – 4 sets x 10 reps
3. Dumbbell Hammer Curl – 3 sets x 12 reps
4. Cable Face Pull – 3 sets x 15 reps
5. Dumbbell Shrug – 3 sets x 12 reps

**Day 4 – Lower Body (Posterior Focus)**
1. Romanian Deadlift (barbell or dumbbell) – 4 sets x 10 reps
2. Leg Curl (machine or cable) – 4 sets x 12 reps
3. Bulgarian Split Squat (dumbbells) – 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
4. Cable Pull-Through – 3 sets x 15 reps
5. Seated Calf Raise – 3 sets x 15 reps

**Rest Days:** 3 days per week. Space training days evenly (e.g., Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri or Mon/Wed/Fri/Sat).

**Tempo & Rest:**
- Control the eccentric (lowering) phase: 2–3 seconds
- Rest 90–120 seconds between compound sets, 60–90 seconds for isolation exercises
- Aim to increase weight by 2.5–5% when you hit the top end of the rep range with good form

**Warm-Up (5–7 minutes):**
- 3 minutes light cardio (treadmill walk or bike)
- Dynamic stretches: arm circles, leg swings, bodyweight squats
- 1–2 light warm-up sets for your first exercise

**Cool-Down (3–5 minutes):**
- Light stretching for major muscle groups worked that day

This split ensures balanced volume across push/pull/legs while keeping your favourite movements (squats and rows) prominent. Track your weights and aim for small weekly improvements.

**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):** Have 2 cups poha [approx 150g] mixed with 1/2 cup roasted peanuts [approx 50g], a pinch of turmeric, and squeeze of lemon. Skip the oil — just dry roast the peanuts and mix through. Or swap the poha for 3 idli [approx 150g] with 1/4 cup roasted chana powder mixed into coconut chutney [approx 40g chutney]. (P: 18g | C: 42g | F: 14g | ~380 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):** 1.5 katori cooked rice [approx 200g], 1 katori dal [approx 150g cooked], 1 small bowl mixed sabzi [approx 120g — carrot, beans, peas], 1 tbsp ghee mixed through the rice. Or keep the rice and dal the same but swap sabzi for 100g paneer bhurji cooked with onion and tomato. (P: 26g | C: 58g | F: 10g | ~440 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm):** 1 cup tea [approx 200ml] with 3–4 roasted chana [approx 30g], no sugar (use jaggery if you need sweetness — just 1 small piece). (P: 8g | C: 18g | F: 2g | ~120 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** 2 rotis [approx 80g], 150g paneer cooked in tomato-onion gravy with a touch of cream [approx 1 tbsp], side salad with cucumber, tomato, and carrot [approx 150g], squeeze of lemon. (P: 24g | C: 28g | F: 18g | ~380 kcal)

**Quick Pointers for the Week:**

- You're hitting roughly 2000–2100 kcal daily with about 160–170g protein — perfect for fat loss without feeling deprived. Your existing eating times work great, so we've kept breakfast, lunch, evening tea, and dinner exactly where they are.
- Dal is your friend here — it's cheap, fills you up, and packed with protein. Rotate between moong, masoor, and toor through the week so it doesn't get boring.
- Paneer is in there 3 times a week because you love it and it's solid protein. On the other days, we're leaning on chickpeas and chana to keep costs down and variety up.
- Ghee is 1 tbsp per lunch and dinner — that's about 9g fat per meal, which is fine. Don't skip it; it helps with satiety and nutrient absorption.
- Tea with roasted chana at 6:30 is your snack anchor. The chana gives you protein and keeps hunger at bay until dinner.
- Drink your 2L water through the day — especially between meals. It'll help with the deficit and keep energy steady during your evening workouts.
- One last thing: you mentioned struggling with consistency. That's normal. This plan uses foods you already eat, so there's no learning curve. Just follow it as written, and by week 3 it'll feel automatic.

You've got a solid plan. Stick with it, hit your training sessions, and we'll check in soon. Let's go!

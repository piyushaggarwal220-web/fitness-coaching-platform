Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Suresh,

Great to have you on board! Your goal is solid — dropping from 78 kg to 62 kg in 12 weeks is totally doable, and I've built this plan around your actual eating schedule (breakfast at 8:30, lunch at 1:30, evening tea at 6:30, dinner at 9). The key to staying consistent, which you mentioned is a challenge, is keeping meals simple, affordable, and something you actually enjoy. I've kept your favourites — dal, paneer, idli, curd rice — front and centre, and everything fits within your ₹8000 budget. Let's go.

---

**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):**
Have a bowl of poha (about 150g cooked) mixed with 1 medium onion, a pinch of turmeric, and a small handful of roasted peanuts (roughly 20g). Add a squeeze of lemon juice for zing.
(P: 8g | C: 45g | F: 8g | ~280 kcal)

**Mid-morning (11:00 am):**
A glass of plain curd (about 150ml, unsweetened).
(P: 6g | C: 6g | F: 3g | ~60 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):**
Have 1 katori (approx 150g) cooked white rice, 1 katori (approx 120g) mixed dal (any mix you like — masoor, chana, or moong), and a small bowl of sabzi (about 100g cooked spinach or bottle gourd with minimal oil). Drizzle a tiny bit of ghee on the rice if you want.
(P: 18g | C: 65g | F: 6g | ~360 kcal)

**Evening snack (6:30 pm):**
A cup of tea with about 30g roasted chana (chickpeas) on the side — no sugar in the tea if you can manage it, or just half a teaspoon.
(P: 8g | C: 15g | F: 3g | ~100 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):**
Have 2 medium rotis (about 80g total), 100g paneer (fresh or fried lightly in a non-stick pan with a pinch of salt and chilli), and a small side salad with cucumber, tomato, and onion (about 150g total). A squeeze of lime on the salad.
(P: 25g | C: 30g | F: 15g | ~350 kcal)

**Daily totals: ~1550 kcal | P: 65g | C: 161g | F: 35g**

**Quick tips as you go:**

• Cook your dal with a pinch of turmeric and a bay leaf — adds flavour with zero calories.
• When you're frying paneer, use a non-stick pan with minimal oil (just a light spray or a few drops). It'll taste just as good and save you calories.
• Your 6:30 pm tea snack is solid — keep it consistent. It'll stop you from overeating at dinner.
• If you're ever short on time, poha takes 5 minutes and is your cheapest, quickest breakfast.
• Drink water between meals, especially before lunch and dinner — it helps with hunger and keeps you on track.

**QUICK NOTES:**

- **Oil:** Use 1 tsp (approx 5g) per sabzi or curry — measure it out to stay consistent.
- **Dal:** Stick to moong, masoor, chana dal, or rajma. Cook with minimal oil and a pinch of turmeric and cumin.
- **Paneer:** Buy in bulk if you can — it's your best friend for protein and satiety. Grill or pan-fry it rather than deep-frying.
- **Chana:** Roast it at home in a dry pan with a pinch of salt and roasted cumin powder — way cheaper than store-bought and tastes better.
- **Curd:** Aim for plain, unsweetened curd (not sweetened yogurt). It's a protein boost and digestion aid.
- **Salad:** Keep it simple — cucumber, tomato, carrot, onion, and a squeeze of lemon. No dressing needed.
- **Consistency:** Eat at roughly the same times each day. Your body will thank you, and your energy for evening training will stay stable.
- **Water:** You're already drinking 2L — keep it up, especially on training days. Add a bit more if you're sweating heavily.

You've got the discipline and the training experience — now let's lock in the eating side. Stick to this, hit your sessions hard, and we'll check in after a couple of weeks to see how you're feeling. You've got this.

Let's go, Harpreet.

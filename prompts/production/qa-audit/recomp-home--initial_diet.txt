Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Pooja!

So excited to get you started on this recomp journey! I've built this week's plan around your usual meal timings and the foods you already love — dal, paneer, idli, curd rice — while keeping things affordable and realistic for your ₹8000/month budget. The goal here is to fuel your body well, support your 3x/week evening workouts, and start shifting your body composition without feeling deprived. Let's make this week count!

**DAY 1 (Monday)**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):** Have 1.5 cups poha (approx 60g raw poha flakes) cooked with 1 tsp oil, curry leaves, peanuts (15g), and veggies like peas and carrots. Pair it with 1 glass (200ml) low-fat milk or curd on the side.
(P: 14g | C: 58g | F: 10g | ~370 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):** Go with 1 cup cooked rice (approx 150g cooked), 1 katori dal (approx 150g cooked moong or masoor dal), and 1 small katori mixed veg sabzi (around 100g — beans, carrot, capsicum). Add a small side salad with cucumber and tomato.
(P: 18g | C: 62g | F: 6g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm):** Your usual tea with 30g roasted chana. If you're training today, have this about 45 minutes before your workout for a little energy boost.
(P: 6g | C: 16g | F: 2g | ~105 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** 2 medium rotis (approx 70g total) with 100g paneer bhurji (cooked with onion, tomato, and minimal oil), plus a big bowl of salad (cucumber, carrot, tomato, lemon). The paneer gives you solid protein to recover from your evening session.
(P: 28g | C: 45g | F: 16g | ~440 kcal)

**Post-Workout (if training today):** A small glass of buttermilk (chaas) or 1 cup curd (150g) with a pinch of salt — keeps it simple and helps with recovery.
(P: 6g | C: 9g | F: 2g | ~75 kcal)

*Daily Total: P: 72g | C: 190g | F: 36g | ~1360 kcal*

This plan is designed to support your recomp goal — enough protein to maintain and build lean muscle, moderate carbs to fuel your workouts and daily activity, and healthy fats to keep hormones balanced. The calorie level is set around maintenance with a slight deficit to encourage gradual fat loss while preserving muscle, especially with your 3x/week training.

**A few quick tips as you go:**
- Meal prep your dal, sabzi, and chutneys on Sunday evening — it'll make weekday lunches and dinners so much easier.
- If you feel hungry between meals, add a small fruit (apple, orange, or a small banana) or a handful of roasted peanuts — just keep portions reasonable.
- On training days (Monday, Wednesday, Friday), try to have your evening snack about 45-60 minutes before your workout, and get some protein in within an hour after (the curd or buttermilk options work great).
- Stay consistent with your 2L water intake — it really helps with digestion, energy, and even hunger management.
- If a meal feels too heavy or light, adjust portion sizes slightly — but try to keep the overall structure intact for the week so we can see how your body responds.

## Warmup Routine (10 minutes, every training day)

Before you touch a barbell, spend about 10 minutes getting your body ready. Start with 5 minutes of light cardio — treadmill walk at a brisk pace, or the bike if your gym has one. Then move into some joint mobility: arm circles, leg swings (front-to-back and side-to-side), hip openers, and a few cat-cows or world's greatest stretches. Finish with a couple of sets of bodyweight squats and some band pull-aparts or light cable rows to wake up your back. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe an empty bar for squats, or light dumbbells for presses — just to groove the pattern and get your nervous system firing.

## Day 1 — Lower Body Strength

**Barbell Back Squat** — 4 sets x 5 reps  
This is your anchor. Control the descent, stay tight through your core, and drive through your heels. Leave a rep or two in the tank on each set — we're building strength, not testing your max.

**Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
Focus on the hinge. Keep the bar close to your body, chest proud, and feel the stretch in your hamstrings. No rounding through your lower back.

**Leg Press** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Feet shoulder-width, push through your whole foot. Don't let your lower back peel off the pad at the bottom.

**Walking Lunges (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg  
Keep your torso upright, don't let your front knee cave in. These will light up your glutes.

**Lying Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Squeeze at the top, control the negative. Hamstrings need love too.

**Cable Crunch** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Think about pulling your ribcage toward your pelvis, not just bending forward. Slow and controlled.

**Plank Hold** — 3 sets x 30-45 seconds  
Brace like someone's about to punch you in the stomach. No sagging hips.

**Hanging Knee Raise (or Lying Leg Raise if no bar available)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
Control the swing. If you're on the ground, press your lower back into the floor.

**Daily step target:** Aim for 7,000 steps today — you're training, so don't stress if you hit 6,500. Just stay generally active outside the gym.

## Day 2 — Upper Body Hypertrophy

**Dumbbell Bench Press** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
Press through your whole hand, not just your palm. Keep your shoulder blades pulled back and down. You can push these closer to failure than the squats — maybe one rep left in the tank on the last set.

**Seated Cable Row** — 4 sets x 10 reps  
You love rows, so let's make them count. Pull to your lower ribcage, squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end. Don't just yank with your arms.

**Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
Press straight up, not forward. Keep your core tight so you're not arching your lower back.

**Lat Pulldown (Cable)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Pull to your upper chest, lean back slightly. Think about driving your elbows down and back, not just bending your arms.

**Dumbbell Lateral Raise** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Light weight, strict form. Lead with your elbows, not your hands. These will burn — that's the point.

**Cable Bicep Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Slow and controlled. No swinging. If you want to squeeze out a couple of cheat reps on the very last set after you've hit strict failure, fine — but only on this, never on compounds.

**Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope Attachment)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Keep your elbows pinned to your sides. Squeeze at the bottom.

**Russian Twist (Bodyweight or Light Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 20 reps (10 per side)  
Rotate from your torso, not just your arms. Keep your chest up.

**Bicycle Crunch** — 3 sets x 15 reps per side  
Slow and deliberate. Don't just flail your legs around.

**Side Plank Hold** — 3 sets x 20-30 seconds per side  
Stack your hips, don't let them sag. Your obliques will thank you later.

**Daily step target:** Aim for 7,000 steps again. If you're feeling good, push for 8,000.

## Day 3 — Active Recovery

No weights today. Your body needs a break, but that doesn't mean sitting on the couch all day. Go for a 20-30 minute walk — outside if you can, treadmill if not. Keep it easy, conversational pace. Follow it up with 15-20 minutes of light stretching and mobility work. Hit your hip flexors, quads, hamstrings, chest, lats, and shoulders. If your gym has a foam roller, spend some time on your quads, IT band, and upper back. This is about recovery, not punishment.

**Daily step target:** Aim for 8,000-9,000 steps today. You're not training, so use this as a chance to move more throughout the day — take the stairs, walk during phone calls, that sort of thing.

## Day 4 — Lower Body Hypertrophy

**Goblet Squat (Dumbbell)** — 4 sets x 10 reps  
Hold a heavy dumbbell at your chest, squat deep, and drive up. This is a great squat variation for building your quads and keeping your torso upright.

**Bulgarian Split Squat (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg  
These are brutal, but they work. Keep your front shin vertical, don't let your knee shoot forward. You'll feel this in your glutes and quads.

**Leg Extension (Machine)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Squeeze at the top, control the descent. Don't just let the weight drop.

**Seated Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Same deal as the lying version earlier in the week — squeeze at the top, slow on the way down.

**Cable Pull-Through** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
This is a hip hinge, just like the RDL. Keep your back flat, push your hips back, and squeeze your glutes at the top.

**Standing Calf Raise (Machine or Dumbbell)** — 4 sets x 20 reps  
Full range of motion. Stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top. Calves respond to volume.

**Reverse Crunch** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Lift your hips off the ground, don't just swing your legs.

**Dead Bug** — 3 sets x 10 reps per side  
Press your lower back into the floor. Move slowly and with control.

**Cable Woodchop** — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  
Rotate from your core, not your arms. This hits your obliques and ties your upper and lower body together.

**Daily step target:** Aim for 7,000 steps. You're training again, so prioritize recovery outside the gym.

## Day 5 — Upper Body Strength

**Barbell Bent-Over Row** — 4 sets x 6 reps  
Your other favorite. Hinge at the hips, keep your back flat, and pull the bar to your lower ribcage. These are heavy, so leave a rep or two in reserve.

**Dumbbell Incline Press** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
Set the bench to about 30-45 degrees. Press up and slightly in, like you're trying to bring the dumbbells together at the top.

**Dumbbell Single-Arm Row** — 3 sets x 10 reps per side  
Brace yourself on a bench, let the dumbbell hang, and pull it to your hip. Squeeze your lat at the top.

**Cable Face Pull** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Pull to your face, not your chest. Think about pulling the rope apart at the end. This is great for your rear delts and upper back health.

**Dumbbell Hammer Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Keep your wrists neutral, elbows pinned. No swinging.

**Overhead Cable Tricep Extension** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
Keep your elbows high and still. Only your forearms should move.

**Dumbbell Shrug** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
Straight up and down. Squeeze your traps at the top, don't roll your shoulders.

**Hanging Leg Raise (or Lying if needed)** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
Same as Day 1 — control the movement, no swinging.

**Cable Pallof Press** — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  
Press the cable straight out in front of you and hold. Your core is fighting rotation here — that's the point.

**Plank to Downward Dog** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
Start in a plank, push your hips up into downward dog, then return to plank. This stretches and strengthens at the same time.

**Daily step target:** Aim for 7,000 steps. Keep it consistent.

## Post-Workout Stretching Routine (5-10 minutes, after every training day)

After you finish your last set, don't just bolt out of the gym. Spend 5-10 minutes stretching the muscles you just trained. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds per side, breathe deeply, and let your body start the recovery process. Here's what to hit:

- **Quads:** Standing quad stretch or couch stretch  
- **Hamstrings:** Seated forward fold or standing toe touch  
- **Hip Flexors:** Low lunge or kneeling hip flexor stretch  
- **Glutes:** Pigeon pose or figure-four stretch  
- **Chest:** Doorway stretch or hands-behind-back stretch  
- **Lats:** Overhead lat stretch or child's pose  
- **Shoulders:** Cross-body shoulder stretch and behind-the-head tricep stretch  
- **Lower Back:** Cat-cow or child's pose  

Don't rush this. It's part of the session.

## Sleep: Let's Fix This

Kavya, I'm not going to sugarcoat it — sleeping less than 6 hours is actively working against your fat loss goal. Your body burns fat, builds muscle, and manages hunger hormones while you sleep. If you're not sleeping, you're fighting an uphill battle. Let's aim for 7.5-8 hours a night. I know that's a big jump from where you are now, so here's how we're going to get there:

1. **Set a non-negotiable bedtime.** If you need to be up at 7 am, you need to be in bed by 11 pm, lights out by 11:15 pm. Work backward from your wake-up time and protect that window.

2. **Cut off chai and coffee by 4 pm.** I know evening chai is a ritual, but the caffeine is keeping you wired. Switch to herbal tea or warm milk with a pinch of turmeric and honey after 4 pm.

3. **Eat dinner by 8 pm if possible.** You're currently eating at 9 pm, which is late. Try to shift it earlier so your body isn't digesting a heavy meal right before bed. If 8 pm isn't realistic, at least make dinner lighter — more sabzi and dal, less roti and paneer.

4. **No screens 30 minutes before bed.** That means no phone, no laptop, no TV. The blue light messes with your melatonin production. If you need to wind down, read a book or listen to a podcast.

5. **Keep your bedroom cool and dark.** If you don't have blackout curtains, get a sleep mask. If your room is hot, use a fan. Your body sleeps better in a cool, dark environment.

6. **Create a wind-down routine.** Same thing every night — brush your teeth, wash your face, spend 5 minutes stretching or doing deep breathing, then get into bed. Your brain will start associating this routine with sleep.

7. **If your mind is racing, do a brain dump.** Keep a notebook by your bed. If you're lying there thinking about work or tomorrow's to-do list, write it down and let it go. You'll deal with it in the morning.

This is just as important as the training. Treat it like a non-negotiable part of the plan.

## A Few Notes on Intensity

You're new to structured training, so let's talk about how hard to push. On the big compound lifts — squats, deadlifts, rows, presses — leave a rep or two in the tank. That means if you think you could do 7 reps, stop at 5. If you could do 10, stop at 8. We're building strength and technique, not testing your limits every set.

On the isolation and accessory work — leg curls, bicep curls, lateral raises, ab work — you can push closer to failure. That means if you're supposed to do 12 reps, you should be struggling on rep 11 and barely getting rep 12. If you could easily do 15, the weight is too light.

On the very last set of isolation exercises — and only isolation exercises — if you hit strict failure and want to squeeze out a couple of controlled cheat reps (a little body English, a slight swing), that's fine. But never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those require perfect form every single rep.

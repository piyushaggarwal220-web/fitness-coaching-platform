**WEEKLY STRUCTURE**

You'll train on three non-consecutive days (e.g., Monday, Wednesday, Friday works well). Each session is structured to hit all major muscle groups across the week, so you get balanced development without burning out. We're mixing strength and muscle-building work — compound lifts first when you're fresh, then isolation and core to finish strong.

---

**BEFORE EVERY SESSION: Warmup (10 minutes)**

Start with a 2–3 minute brisk walk or light jog in place to get your heart rate up. Then move through some basic mobility: 10 arm circles each direction, 10 leg swings forward and back (each leg), 5–8 bodyweight squats (slow and controlled), 5–8 pushups or incline pushups against a wall or bench if needed. Finish with 30 seconds of jumping jacks or high knees. This gets your body ready without eating into your 45-minute session.

Before your first heavy compound lift each day, do 2 lighter warm-up sets — just 50–60% of the weight you'll use for your working sets. This primes your nervous system and keeps you safe.

---

**DAY 1 — LOWER POWER & CORE** *(e.g., Monday)*

This is your foundation day. We're starting with goblet squats because you love them and they're perfect for building leg strength and muscle.

**Main Lifts:**
- Goblet Squat: 4 sets × 5 reps (heavy, controlled tempo — think 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up; leave 1–2 reps in the tank)
- Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets × 6 reps (hinge at the hips, feel the hamstring stretch; strict form, no bouncing)

**Accessory & Isolation:**
- Dumbbell Lunges (alternating): 3 sets × 10 reps per leg (controlled, step length that feels stable)
- Single-Leg Calf Raises (holding a dumbbell or wall): 2 sets × 12 reps per leg

**Core Work:**
- Dead Bugs: 3 sets × 12 reps (slow, deliberate; opposite arm and leg extend fully)
- Dumbbell Farmer's Carry: 2 sets × 40 meters (grip and core engagement)
- Hollow Body Hold: 2 sets × 20 seconds (lie on your back, press lower back into floor, engage core)

**Daily step target:** 6,000 steps (include a short walk after your session to aid recovery)

---

**DAY 2 — ACTIVE RECOVERY** *(e.g., Tuesday)*

No heavy lifting today — this is about movement and mobility. Take a 20–30 minute walk (aim for 8,000 steps), then spend 15 minutes on light stretching and mobility work: hip openers, shoulder rolls, gentle spinal twists, quad stretches, hamstring stretches. Hold each stretch for 45–60 seconds. Think of this as active rest — you're moving, but not taxing your muscles. If you feel like it, some light yoga or just walking around your neighborhood is perfect. This helps blood flow and keeps soreness at bay.

**Daily step target:** 8,000 steps

**DAY 3 — UPPER PUSH & PULL** *(e.g., Wednesday)*

This day balances pushing and pulling movements to keep your shoulders, chest, and back balanced.

**Main Lifts:**
- Dumbbell Bench Press (or floor press if you don't have a bench): 4 sets × 5 reps (controlled descent, explosive push — 2 seconds down, 1 second up; leave 1–2 reps in reserve)
- Dumbbell Row (single-arm, supported on a bench or knee): 4 sets × 6 reps per side (retract the scapula, drive the elbow back, really squeeze the lat; strict form first)

**Accessory & Isolation:**
- Dumbbell Overhead Press: 3 sets × 8 reps (core tight, no arching; this is about shoulder strength)
- Dumbbell Flyes (or machine equivalent, or incline floor flyes): 3 sets × 10 reps (controlled, feel the chest stretch and squeeze)
- Dumbbell Bicep Curls: 2 sets × 12 reps (last set can go close to failure with 1–2 controlled cheat reps if needed)

**Core Work:**
- Plank Hold: 3 sets × 30–45 seconds (neutral spine, shoulders over wrists)
- Dumbbell Woodchops (or Pallof Press with resistance band if available): 2 sets × 10 reps per side (rotational control, anti-rotation strength)
- Bird Dogs: 2 sets × 10 reps per side (opposite arm and leg, slow and controlled)

**Daily step target:** 6,000 steps (include a walk after training)

**Core Work:**
- Dumbbell Suitcase Carries (one dumbbell, walk without leaning): 2 sets × 40 meters per side (anti-lateral flexion, core stabilization)

**TRAINING INTENSITY & FORM NOTES**

Here's the philosophy: on your heavy compound lifts (goblet squats, RDLs, bench press, rows, overhead press), you're aiming for strength, so we keep reps lower (5–6) and weight heavier. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank — you should feel like you *could* do one or two more, but you stop. This keeps you safe, especially as a beginner, and builds strength without unnecessary joint stress.

For isolation exercises (flyes, curls, lateral lunges, calf raises), we push a bit harder. You can take these closer to failure — where the last rep is tough but still controlled. On the very last set of isolation work, if you want to squeeze out 1–2 extra reps with a slight form adjustment (like a small swing on curls), that's okay. But never do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges — those demand strict form every rep.

Always prioritize moving well over moving heavy. A perfect 5-rep goblet squat beats a sloppy 8-rep attempt every time.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING** *(after every session, 5–7 minutes)*

After you finish your last set, take 5 minutes to stretch the major muscles you just worked:

- Quad Stretch (standing or lying): 45 seconds per leg
- Hamstring Stretch (forward fold or lying): 60 seconds
- Hip Flexor Stretch (half-kneeling): 45 seconds per side
- Chest Stretch (doorway or cross-body): 45 seconds per side
- Shoulder & Lat Stretch (arm overhead, lean gently): 45 seconds per side

Just breathe, relax into each stretch, and let your body cool down. This 5 minutes makes a huge difference in recovery and mobility over time.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already hitting 7–8 hours, which is excellent — that's the foundation of muscle growth. Let's protect that and maybe push it to 7.5–9 hours when you can. Your body builds muscle while you sleep, so this is non-negotiable.

**Practical tips for better sleep** (tailored to your life):

1. **Chai cutoff:** Stop your evening chai by 6:00 PM. I know chai is a ritual, but caffeine after 6 PM can mess with sleep quality. If you crave something warm after dinner, try herbal tea or warm milk with a pinch of turmeric.

2. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. The blue light messes with melatonin production. If you need to check something, use a blue light filter.

3. **Meal timing:** Your 9:00 PM dinner is fine, but try to finish eating by 9:30 PM. Going to bed on a full stomach can cause discomfort and poor sleep quality.

4. **Consistent timing:** Aim to sleep and wake at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body loves routine. If you're aiming for 7.5–8.5 hours and waking at 6:30 AM, aim for bed by 10:30–11:00 PM.

5. **Room environment:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 65–68°F if possible), dark, and quiet. If you share space, use a fan or white noise app.

6. **Post-workout nutrition:** Your snack at 6:30 PM (roasted chana and tea) is good timing — it's a couple hours before bed, so digestion won't interfere. Keep it light.

7. **Relaxation ritual:** Spend 10 minutes doing something calm before bed — light reading, breathing exercises, or just lying down and thinking about your day. It signals to your body that sleep is coming.

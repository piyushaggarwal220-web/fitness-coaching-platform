Here's how we're structuring your week: three focused dumbbell sessions that alternate between lower-body power, upper-body strength, and a full-body metabolic finisher. Each session is built around compound movements (your goblet squats and dumbbell rows will get plenty of love), with accessory work to build muscle and core stability. You'll warm up the same way each session, and we'll keep everything under 45 minutes so it fits your evening routine.

**WARM-UP (All Sessions — ~10 minutes)**
Start with a 2-minute brisk walk or light jog on the spot to get your heart rate up. Then move through: 10 arm circles each direction, 10 bodyweight squats, 10 cat-cows (on your hands and knees, flowing between arching and rounding your spine), 5 inchworms, 10 glute bridges, and 10 scapular push-ups (hands on the ground, just moving your shoulder blades without bending elbows). Finish with 30 seconds of light dynamic stretching — leg swings, torso twists. This preps your nervous system and joints without fatiguing you before the main work.

**SESSION 1 — LOWER POWER (Monday or your preferred Day 1)**
Focus here is building strength in your legs and glutes with heavier loading and lower reps. Your goblet squat is the star today.

Before you get into your main lifts, do 2 lighter warm-up sets of goblet squats — just the movement pattern and breathing, nothing heavy.

Goblet Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps — this is your main strength builder. Use a weight that feels challenging on rep 5 but you could do maybe 1-2 more if pushed. Keep your chest upright, elbows inside your knees, and pause for a half-second at the bottom. Leave a rep or two in reserve; we're not grinding out every last rep on compounds.

Dumbbell Bulgarian Split Squat: 3 sets x 8 reps per leg — find a low bench, chair, or step at home. Back foot elevated, front leg does the work. This builds unilateral strength and glute activation.

Dumbbell Deadlift (or Trap Deadlift if you have a trap bar, otherwise regular dumbbell deadlift): 3 sets x 5 reps — hinge from the hips, keep the dumbbells close to your legs, and drive through your heels. Same rule: leave a rep or two in reserve.

Dumbbell Lateral Lunge: 3 sets x 10 reps per side — step out wide, sink into one leg, keep the other straight. This hits the outer quads and glutes differently.

Core Work:
- Dead Bug: 3 sets x 12 reps per side — lie on your back, opposite arm and leg extend while the other two stay bent. Slow and controlled.
- Plank Hold: 3 sets x 30-45 seconds — keep your hips level, core tight, neutral spine.

Step target for today: 7,000 steps (includes your workout movement).

**SESSION 2 — UPPER STRENGTH & HYPERTROPHY (Wednesday or your preferred Day 2)**
Today we're building upper-body muscle and strength. Your dumbbell rows will be the anchor, and we'll pair them with pressing and isolation work.

Warm-up as described above, then 2 lighter warm-up sets of dumbbell rows.

Dumbbell Row (Single-Arm or Double-Arm — your choice): 4 sets x 6 reps — row the dumbbells to your ribcage, squeeze your shoulder blade back, and control the descent. This is a strength day, so heavy enough that rep 6 is tough. Leave a rep in reserve.

Dumbbell Floor Press or Incline Push-up (hands on a low bench): 3 sets x 8 reps — builds pressing strength without needing a bench. Control the descent, pause briefly at the chest, and drive back up.

Dumbbell Overhead Press: 3 sets x 8 reps — standing or half-kneeling, press the dumbbells overhead. Full-body tension; don't lean back excessively.

Dumbbell Bicep Curl: 3 sets x 12 reps — strict form, no swinging. Last set can go to near-failure with maybe 1-2 controlled "cheat reps" (slight body English) after strict failure if needed, but keep it clean.

Tricep Dips (using a chair or bench): 3 sets x 10-12 reps — elbows tucked, lower until elbows are about 90 degrees. Isolation work, so push this closer to true failure.

Core Work:
- Dumbbell Pallof Press: 3 sets x 10 reps per side — hold a dumbbell at chest height, press it away from your body while resisting rotation. Anti-rotation strength.
- Dead Bug: 3 sets x 10 reps per side (different from Session 1, just for variety).

Step target for today: 7,000 steps.

**SESSION 3 — LOWER HYPERTROPHY & METABOLIC (Friday or your preferred Day 3)**
Higher reps, moderate weight, and a bit more volume. This session builds muscle endurance and work capacity while keeping intensity high.

Warm-up as usual, then 2 lighter warm-up sets of goblet squats.

Goblet Squat: 3 sets x 12 reps — lighter than Session 1, but the reps are higher. Focus on the pump and muscle connection. Last set can go closer to failure.

Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets x 10 reps — hip hinge movement, slight knee bend, feel the hamstring and glute stretch. Never use cheat reps on RDLs; keep it strict.

Dumbbell Step-Up: 3 sets x 10 reps per leg — step up onto a bench or sturdy surface, drive through the front leg. Excellent for glute and quad hypertrophy.

Dumbbell Goblet Calf Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps — hold a dumbbell at chest, rise up on your toes, pause, lower. Higher reps for smaller muscle groups.

Dumbbell Chest Flye (lying on the floor or a low bench): 3 sets x 12 reps — arc the dumbbells in a wide motion, feel the chest stretch and squeeze. Isolation work, push closer to failure.

Core Work:
- Dumbbell Weighted Crunch: 3 sets x 15 reps — hold a light dumbbell across your chest, crunch up. Higher reps for core.
- Side Plank: 3 sets x 30 seconds per side — anti-lateral flexion strength.

Step target for today: 7,000 steps.

Focus on hip flexors, hamstrings, shoulders, and thoracic spine. No heavy lifting, no intensity. Just movement and breathing.

**REST DAY (Sunday or your preferred full rest day)**
Complete rest. No structured movement beyond normal daily activity. This is when your body adapts and grows.

Step target: 3,000-4,000 steps (just normal daily movement).

**TRAINING INTENSITY & REP STRATEGY**
Here's the framework: on compound lifts (goblet squat, deadlifts, rows, presses), leave 1-2 reps in the tank on most sets. You should feel like you *could* do one or two more, but you stop before grinding. This builds strength safely and sustainably, especially as a beginner. On isolation and accessory work (curls, flyes, calf raises, etc.), you can push closer to true failure — that last set of bicep curls or chest flyes should feel tough. If you're doing strict reps and hit failure, you can use 1-2 controlled "cheat reps" to eke out a bit more volume, but *never* on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell/dumbbell compound movement. Keep those strict. The idea is to build good movement patterns first, then add intensity over time.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (After Each Session — ~5 minutes)**
After your last set, take 2-3 minutes to breathe and let your heart rate settle. Then move through these stretches, holding each for 30-60 seconds:
- Quad Stretch (standing or lying): 30 seconds per leg
- Hamstring Stretch (forward fold or lying): 30-45 seconds
- Hip Flexor Stretch (half-kneeling or standing): 30 seconds per side
- Chest Stretch (doorway or cross-body): 30 seconds per side
- Shoulder Stretch (cross-body arm pull): 30 seconds per side
- Child's Pose: 45-60 seconds

No need to be perfect; just move gently and breathe. This helps with recovery and mobility.

**PROGRESSION NOTES**
Every 2 weeks, aim to add 1-2 reps to your compounds or go slightly heavier if the weight feels easy. Small, sustainable jumps. If an exercise feels off or uncomfortable, swap it for a similar movement — there's always an alternative. Listen to your body, and let me know if anything doesn't feel right.

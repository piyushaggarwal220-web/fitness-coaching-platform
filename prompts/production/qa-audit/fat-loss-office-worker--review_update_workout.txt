Your check-in shows the scale has plateaued for two weeks and training felt average. Your adherence during weekdays is solid, but weekends are looser. This week we're progressing your training split to keep your muscles adapting and your metabolism elevated. You've been following an Upper/Lower/Full split, so we're now moving to a 4-day split hitting two body parts per session. This will increase training density slightly while keeping session length around 60 minutes. Volume stays similar overall, but we're adding variety to shock the muscles and break through the plateau.

**Weekly Structure:**
- Day 1: Chest + Back
- Day 2: Legs + Shoulders
- Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery (light walk, stretching)
- Day 4: Arms + Core
- Day 5: Full Body Power
- Day 6: Rest
- Day 7: Rest

**Warm-up (every session):** 5 minutes light cardio (treadmill walk or cycle) + dynamic stretches (arm circles, leg swings, bodyweight squats) for 3–5 minutes.

**Cooldown (every session):** 5 minutes static stretching focusing on muscles trained that day.

---

**Day 1: Chest + Back**

1. Barbell Bench Press – 4 sets x 8 reps (increase weight slightly from last phase if possible)
2. Barbell Bent-Over Rows – 4 sets x 8 reps (your favorite, keep form strict)
3. Incline Dumbbell Press – 3 sets x 10 reps
4. Cable Seated Rows – 3 sets x 10 reps
5. Dumbbell Chest Flyes – 3 sets x 12 reps
6. Lat Pulldown – 3 sets x 12 reps

Rest 90–120 seconds between sets. Focus on controlled tempo, especially on the lowering phase.

---

**Day 2: Legs + Shoulders**

1. Barbell Back Squats – 4 sets x 8 reps (your favorite, aim to add 2.5–5 kg if last week felt good)
2. Leg Press – 3 sets x 12 reps
3. Romanian Deadlifts (RDL) – 3 sets x 10 reps
4. Dumbbell Shoulder Press – 4 sets x 8 reps
5. Dumbbell Lateral Raises – 3 sets x 12 reps
6. Cable Face Pulls – 3 sets x 15 reps

Rest 90–120 seconds between compound lifts, 60–90 seconds for accessory moves.

---

**Day 3: Rest or Active Recovery**

Take full rest or do 20–30 minutes of light walking, yoga, or stretching. Aim for 5,000+ steps if possible. Hydrate well and focus on sleep.

---

**Day 4: Arms + Core**

1. Barbell Bicep Curls – 4 sets x 10 reps
2. Cable Tricep Pushdowns – 4 sets x 10 reps
3. Dumbbell Hammer Curls – 3 sets x 12 reps
4. Overhead Dumbbell Tricep Extension – 3 sets x 12 reps
5. Cable Rope Curls – 3 sets x 12 reps
6. Plank Hold – 3 sets x 30–45 seconds
7. Cable Crunches – 3 sets x 15 reps

Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. Keep core tight during all arm exercises.

---

**Day 5: Full Body Power**

1. Goblet Squats – 3 sets x 12 reps (moderate weight, focus on depth)
2. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts – 3 sets x 10 reps
3. Push-Ups – 3 sets x AMRAP (as many reps as possible with good form)
4. Dumbbell Rows (single arm) – 3 sets x 10 reps each arm
5. Dumbbell Shoulder Press – 3 sets x 10 reps
6. Leg Curls (machine) – 3 sets x 12 reps
7. Plank Hold – 2 sets x 30–45 seconds

Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. This session is slightly lighter to allow recovery before the weekend.

---

**Day 6 & 7: Rest**

Full rest days. Prioritize sleep (7–8 hours), hydration (2+ liters), and stick to your meal plan. Use these days to meal prep for the week ahead if possible. Aim for at least 4,000–5,000 steps through casual walking.

---

**Cardio:**
- 3 sessions of 30 minutes brisk walking per week (on rest days or post-workout).
- Target: Keep heart rate moderate, around 120–130 bpm. This supports fat loss without interfering with recovery.
- Aim for 5,000+ steps daily; use evenings after dinner to add a 10–15 minute walk if needed.

---

**Key Focus This Week:**
- Progressive overload: If an exercise felt easy last phase, add 2.5–5 kg or 1–2 reps.
- Time under tension: Control the eccentric (lowering) phase — count 2–3 seconds down.
- Adherence on weekends: Plan your meals and workouts in advance. Consistency breaks plateaus.
- Hydration: 2+ liters daily, especially around training.
- Sleep: Protect your 7–8 hours. Poor sleep kills fat loss and performance.

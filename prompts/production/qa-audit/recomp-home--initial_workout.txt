Hey Pooja!

I've put together your first week based on what you shared — 3 solid training days at home with your dumbbells and bands, built around squats and rows like you wanted. Since you're aiming for recomposition and working around an office schedule, we're going to focus on full-body sessions that hit all the major muscle groups each time you train, keeping things efficient and effective in your 60-minute window. You mentioned consistency has been a struggle, so I've kept the structure simple and repeatable — nothing fancy, just good movement patterns done well.

Before every session, take about 10 minutes to warm up properly. Start with 3-4 minutes of marching in place or light jogging on the spot to get your heart rate up and blood flowing. Then move into some joint mobility — arm circles forward and back, leg swings front to back and side to side, hip circles, ankle rolls, and a few cat-cow stretches on all fours. Finish with 10-15 bodyweight squats and 10 glute bridges to wake up your lower body. Once you're warm, before you jump into your first heavy exercise each day, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe 40-50% of your working weight for 8-10 reps, then 60-70% for 5-6 reps. This primes your muscles and nervous system without tiring you out.

A quick note on intensity: on your main compound movements (squats, rows, presses), leave 1-2 reps in the tank — you should finish each set knowing you could've squeezed out one or two more if absolutely needed. On isolation and accessory work, you can push closer to failure, especially on the last set. If you want to add a couple of controlled cheat reps after you hit strict failure on the final set of an isolation move, that's fine — but never on squats, lunges, or any big compound lift. Keep those strict and controlled.

**Day 1 — Full Body Power**

Goblet Squat — 4 sets x 6 reps
Hold a dumbbell vertically at your chest, feet shoulder-width apart. Squat down until your thighs are parallel or just below, keeping your chest up and core tight. Drive through your heels to stand. Focus on depth and control here — no bouncing at the bottom.

Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift — 4 sets x 8 reps
Hold dumbbells in front of your thighs, feet hip-width. Hinge at the hips, pushing them back as the weights slide down your thighs, keeping a slight bend in your knees. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings, then drive your hips forward to stand tall. Keep your back flat the entire time.

Dumbbell Bench Press (Floor Press) — 4 sets x 8 reps
Lie on your back on a mat, knees bent, feet flat. Press dumbbells up from chest level, elbows at about 45 degrees to your body. Lower until your upper arms gently touch the floor, then press back up. No bouncing off the floor — pause briefly at the bottom.

Bent-Over Dumbbell Row — 4 sets x 8 reps
Hinge forward at the hips, back flat, knees slightly bent, dumbbells hanging straight down. Pull the weights to your ribcage, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top. Lower with control. Think about pulling your elbows back, not just lifting the weight with your hands.

Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Standing or Seated) — 3 sets x 10 reps
Press dumbbells overhead from shoulder height, keeping your core braced. Don't arch your lower back excessively — think about pressing your ribs down as you press the weights up.

Resistance Band Bicep Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps
Stand on the band, handles in each hand, curl up with control. Squeeze at the top, lower slowly.

Resistance Band Tricep Extension (Overhead) — 3 sets x 12 reps
Anchor the band under your feet, hold the handles overhead, elbows pointing forward. Extend your arms up, squeezing your triceps at the top.

Plank — 3 sets x 30-45 seconds
Forearms on the ground, body in a straight line from head to heels. Keep your core tight and don't let your hips sag.

Dead Bug — 3 sets x 10 reps per side
Lie on your back, arms extended toward the ceiling, knees bent at 90 degrees. Lower opposite arm and leg toward the floor while keeping your lower back pressed into the mat. Alternate sides.

Bicycle Crunches — 3 sets x 15 reps per side
Slow and controlled — bring opposite elbow to knee, fully extend the other leg. Focus on the twist and contraction, not speed.

Aim for around 7,000-8,000 steps today — you're already at 5,000 most days, so adding a 10-15 minute walk after dinner or during lunch will get you there easily.

**Day 2 — Active Recovery**

Today's all about movement without taxing your muscles. Go for a 20-30 minute walk at a comfortable pace — morning or evening, whatever fits your schedule. Follow it up with 15-20 minutes of light stretching and mobility work. Focus on your hips, hamstrings, quads, chest, and shoulders. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds, breathe deeply, and don't force anything. You can also throw in some cat-cow stretches, child's pose, and gentle spinal twists. The goal is to feel loose and recovered, not worked.

Step target: 8,000-10,000 steps — a bit higher since you're not lifting today. Break it up throughout the day if needed.

**Day 3 — Full Body Hypertrophy**

Dumbbell Bulgarian Split Squat — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
Rear foot elevated on a chair or bench, front foot flat on the ground. Lower down until your front thigh is parallel to the floor, then drive back up. Keep your torso upright and core engaged. These are tough — take your time and focus on balance.

Dumbbell Stiff-Leg Deadlift — 3 sets x 12 reps
Similar to the RDL but with straighter legs. Hinge at the hips, lower the dumbbells toward your feet, feel the stretch in your hamstrings, then pull back up by squeezing your glutes. Keep your back flat.

Dumbbell Floor Press (Close Grip) — 3 sets x 12 reps
Same setup as Day 1, but bring your hands closer together — this shifts more emphasis to your triceps and inner chest.

Single-Arm Dumbbell Row — 3 sets x 12 reps per arm
Place one hand and knee on a bench or chair for support, row the dumbbell with the other arm. Pull your elbow back and up, squeezing your lat at the top. Control the descent.

Dumbbell Lateral Raise — 3 sets x 15 reps
Standing, slight bend in your elbows, raise the dumbbells out to the sides until they're at shoulder height. Lower with control. Don't swing or use momentum — keep it strict.

Resistance Band Face Pull — 3 sets x 15 reps
Anchor the band at chest height (or loop it around a sturdy post), pull the handles toward your face, elbows high and out to the sides. Squeeze your rear delts and upper back at the end of each rep.

Dumbbell Hammer Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps
Palms facing each other throughout the movement. Curl up, squeeze, lower slowly.

Dumbbell Overhead Tricep Extension — 3 sets x 12 reps
Hold one dumbbell with both hands overhead, lower it behind your head by bending your elbows, then extend back up. Keep your elbows pointing forward, not flaring out.

Reverse Crunches — 3 sets x 15 reps
Lie on your back, knees bent, lift your hips off the ground by pulling your knees toward your chest. Lower with control — no swinging.

Side Plank — 3 sets x 20-30 seconds per side
Forearm on the ground, body in a straight line, hips stacked. Keep your core tight and don't let your hips drop.

Mountain Climbers (Slow and Controlled) — 3 sets x 20 reps total
Plank position, bring one knee toward your chest, then switch legs. Focus on keeping your hips level and core engaged — no bouncing.

Step target: 7,000-8,000 steps again — same as Day 1.

**Day 4 — Rest Day**

Full rest today. No structured workout, but stay lightly active — a gentle 10-15 minute walk if you feel like it, some light stretching before bed, nothing intense. Let your body recover and adapt.

Step target: 5,000-6,000 steps — just your normal daily movement, no need to push it.

**Day 5 — Full Body Strength & Conditioning**

Dumbbell Sumo Squat — 4 sets x 8 reps
Feet wider than shoulder-width, toes pointed slightly out, hold a dumbbell vertically between your legs. Squat down, keeping your chest up and knees tracking over your toes. This variation hits your inner thighs and glutes a bit more.

Dumbbell Walking Lunge — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
Hold dumbbells at your sides, step forward into a lunge, back knee almost touching the ground, then step the back leg forward into the next lunge. Keep your torso upright and core tight. If space is tight, do reverse lunges in place instead.

Dumbbell Chest Fly (Floor) — 3 sets x 12 reps
Lie on your back, arms extended above your chest with a slight bend in your elbows. Lower the dumbbells out to the sides in a wide arc until your upper arms touch the floor, then bring them back up. Feel the stretch across your chest.

Resistance Band Seated Row — 4 sets x 12 reps
Sit on the floor, legs extended, loop the band around your feet. Pull the handles toward your torso, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Slow and controlled — no jerking.

Dumbbell Arnold Press — 3 sets x 10 reps
Start with dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing you. As you press up, rotate your palms forward so they face away at the top. Reverse the motion on the way down. Great for hitting all three heads of your shoulders.

Resistance Band Kickback — 3 sets x 15 reps per arm
Loop the band around a sturdy anchor at waist height, hold one handle, elbow bent at 90 degrees. Extend your arm back, squeezing your tricep at the top.

Resistance Band Curl (21s) — 2 sets
7 reps bottom half (band to mid-bicep), 7 reps top half (mid-bicep to shoulder), 7 reps full range. No rest between segments. This will burn — embrace it.

Leg Raises — 3 sets x 12 reps
Lie on your back, legs straight, lift them up to 90 degrees, lower them back down with control without letting your lower back arch off the floor.

Russian Twists — 3 sets x 20 reps total (10 per side)
Sit on the floor, knees bent, lean back slightly, hold a dumbbell at your chest. Twist your torso side to side, tapping the weight on the floor beside you. Keep your core engaged the whole time.

Plank to Down Dog — 3 sets x 10 reps
Start in a plank, push your hips up and back into a downward dog position, then return to plank. This adds a dynamic stretch and core challenge.

Step target: 7,000-8,000 steps — same as your other training days.

**Post-Workout Stretching Routine (Do this after every training session)**

Spend 5-10 minutes stretching the major muscle groups you worked. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds per side, breathe deeply, and relax into it. Here's a simple flow:

Standing Quad Stretch — pull one foot toward your glutes, keep your knees together.

Seated Hamstring Stretch — sit with one leg extended, reach toward your toes, keep your back straight.

Pigeon Pose or Figure-4 Stretch — great for your hips and glutes.

Child's Pose — stretches your lower back, lats, and shoulders.

Chest Doorway Stretch — place your forearm on a wall or doorframe, turn your body away to stretch your chest and front shoulder.

Tricep Overhead Stretch — reach one arm overhead, bend at the elbow, pull your elbow gently with the other hand.

Seated Spinal Twist — sit cross-legged, twist your torso to one side, hold, then switch.

**Sleep & Recovery**

You're already doing well with 7-8 hours most nights — let's lock that in and make the quality even better. Aim for 7.5-9 hours consistently, ideally getting to bed by 10:30-11:00 pm so you're well-rested for work and your evening training sessions. Here are a few practical tweaks that'll make a difference:

Cut off chai and coffee by 4:00 pm — caffeine can linger in your system for 6-8 hours, so an evening cup might be messing with your sleep more than you realize.

Keep your dinner timing where it is (9:00 pm), but try to keep it lighter on training days — a heavy meal right before bed can disrupt sleep quality. Roti, paneer, and salad is perfect; just maybe go easy on the oil or ghee.

Put your phone away at least 30 minutes before bed. I know it's tempting to scroll, but the blue light and mental stimulation will keep you wired. If you need to wind down, try reading or listening to something calming instead.

Keep your room cool and dark — even a small amount of light can interfere with melatonin production. If you can, use blackout curtains or an eye mask.

Stick to a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends. Your body thrives on routine, and going to bed and waking up at roughly the same time every day will help you fall asleep faster and wake up feeling more refreshed.

If your mind tends to race at night, try a 5-minute breathing exercise before bed — 4 counts in, hold for 4, out for 6. It signals your nervous system to relax.

Avoid heavy screen time right after dinner — if you're watching TV or on your laptop, try to wrap it up by 10:00 pm so you have a proper wind-down window.

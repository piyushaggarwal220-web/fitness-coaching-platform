On the very last set of isolation exercises, if you hit strict failure, you can squeeze out a couple of controlled cheat reps, but keep that to isolation only. Never do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or barbell compounds — form breaks down too easily and that's where injuries happen.

**Day 1 — Lower Power (Squats + Posterior Chain)**

Your step target today: 8,000 steps (training day — get your walk in before or after the gym).

Warmup: 2–3 min brisk walk or light bike, shoulder circles and arm swings x 10 each direction, bodyweight squats x 15, leg swings x 10 each leg.

Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps
— Two warm-up sets first at lighter weight. This is your main lift for the day. Focus on depth — chest up, knees tracking over your toes, drive through your heels. You should feel strong and controlled, not rushed.

Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets x 6 reps
— These pair beautifully with squats. Feel the hamstring and glute stretch as you hinge. Controlled descent, no bouncing.

Leg Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
— Machine-based, so form is locked in. Go deep but don't lock out your knees at the top. Steady tempo.

Leg Extensions: 2 sets x 12 reps
— Quad isolation. Control the negative (lowering) phase — take 2–3 seconds on the way down. Last set, push to near failure.

Core work (pick one and stick with it for the week):
— Weighted Cable Crunches: 3 sets x 12 reps (squeeze hard at the top, 1-second pause).

Cooldown: 5–6 minutes of static stretching — quad stretch (30 sec each leg), hamstring stretch (30 sec each leg), hip flexor lunge (30 sec each side), glute stretch (30 sec each side).

**Day 2 — Upper Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)**

Your step target today: 6,000 steps (lighter day — you're training in the evening, so morning walk or just natural steps through the office).

Warmup: 2–3 min light cardio, arm circles x 15 each direction, band pull-aparts x 15, light pushups or machine press x 10.

Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 5 reps
— Two warm-up sets to start. Chest down, shoulder blades retracted, feet planted. Lower the bar to your mid-chest, pause briefly, and drive back up. Control the weight — don't let it crash down.

Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
— Slight incline (30 degrees). Dumbbells give you a bigger range of motion than the barbell. Chest engaged, core tight.

Cable Flye (or Machine Pec Deck): 3 sets x 10 reps
— Isolation for the chest. Squeeze at the center, controlled return. Last set, push close to failure.

Overhead Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
— Shoulders. Stand tall, core braced. Press straight up, slight forward angle. No arching the lower back excessively.

Tricep Rope Pushdown: 2 sets x 12 reps
— Cable work. Full extension at the bottom, controlled return. Last set, take it close to failure.

Core work (different from Day 1):
— Dead Bug: 3 sets x 10 reps per side (lie on your back, extend opposite arm and leg, controlled and deliberate).

Cooldown: Chest doorway stretch (30 sec each side), shoulder cross-body stretch (30 sec each arm), tricep overhead stretch (30 sec each arm).

**Day 4 — Upper Pull (Back, Rows, Biceps)**

Your step target today: 8,000 steps (training day).

Warmup: 2–3 min light bike or walk, arm circles and band pull-aparts x 15, light lat pulldown or assisted pull-ups x 10.

Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 5 reps
— Two warm-up sets first. This is the money lift for your back. Chest up, slight knee bend, row the bar to your lower chest. Squeeze your shoulder blades together. Control the descent — don't let it drop.

Cable or Machine Row (Seated): 3 sets x 8 reps
— Rows again, but from a different angle. Chest to the pad, strong retraction. You're hitting your back from multiple angles — this matters for balanced development.

Lat Pulldown: 3 sets x 8 reps
— Pull the bar down to your chest, elbows slightly in front of your body. Feel the lats engage. Controlled return.

Face Pulls (Cable): 2 sets x 12 reps
— Great for shoulder health and rear delts. Rope attachment, pull to face level, squeeze the rear delts. Higher reps, lighter weight.

Dumbbell Bicep Curls: 2 sets x 10 reps
— Isolation for the arms. Full range of motion, no swinging. Squeeze at the top, controlled lower. Last set, push close to failure.

Core work (different again):
— Pallof Press (Cable): 3 sets x 10 reps per side (stand perpendicular to the cable, press the handle straight out, resisting rotation).

Cooldown: Lat stretch (30 sec each side, grab your wrist overhead and lean), shoulder reverse stretch (30 sec each arm), bicep stretch (30 sec per arm).

**Sleep & Recovery**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is excellent. Let's keep that locked in and maybe aim for 7.5–9 hours on training days if you can. Your current eating pattern has dinner at 9 pm, which is fine, but try to finish eating by 10 pm so you have a solid 2–3 hour window before bed.

Here are some practical tweaks for better sleep, especially in an Indian household:

1. **Chai cutoff**: No chai or coffee after 4 pm. I know it's part of the routine, but that caffeine will mess with your sleep quality. Switch to herbal tea or just water in the evening.

2. **Meal timing**: Dinner at 9 pm is okay, but make it lighter on training days if possible — a smaller portion of roti and paneer, or lighter sabzi. Heavy meals close to bed can disrupt sleep.

3. **Screen time**: Put your phone away 30–45 minutes before bed. I get it — scrolling is easy, but the blue light messes with melatonin. Read something or just wind down.

4. **Room temperature**: If you can, keep your bedroom cool and dark. Even cracking a window helps if it's hot.

5. **Pre-bed routine**: 10–15 minutes of light stretching or breathing exercises before bed. Calm your nervous system.

6. **Water timing**: Drink most of your 2 liters during the day and earlier evening. Taper off by 8 pm so you're not waking up for bathroom trips.

7. **Consistency**: Try to sleep and wake at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body loves routine.

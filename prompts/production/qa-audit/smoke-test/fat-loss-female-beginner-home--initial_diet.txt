Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Sneha!

Great to have you on board. I've built this plan around your current eating schedule—breakfast at 8:30, lunch at 1:30, evening tea at 6:30, and dinner at 9—so it fits naturally into your day without feeling forced. Your goal is to hit 62 kg in 12 weeks, and consistency is the real challenge, so we're keeping the food familiar: lots of dal, paneer, idli, and curd rice that you already love. The plan sits at a gentle 300-kcal deficit, which means steady fat loss without the hunger that kills motivation. You've got this.

---

**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):** 2 cups poha [~150g] with 1 tbsp peanuts [~15g] crushed through it, a pinch of turmeric, and squeeze of lemon. Side of 1 small bowl curd [~100g]. Toast the peanuts first in a dry pan for 30 seconds—brings out the flavour.
(P: 18g | C: 42g | F: 10g | ~310 kcal)

**Mid-morning (11:00 am):** 1 glass buttermilk [~200ml].
(P: 6g | C: 8g | F: 1g | ~55 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):** 1 katori cooked rice [~150g], 1 katori mixed dal [~150g, approx 60g cooked], and 1 small bowl sabzi—spinach or pumpkin with a tsp of oil [~100g]. Finish with a small pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon.
(P: 32g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~420 kcal)

**Evening snack (6:30 pm):** 1 cup tea [~150ml, made with 1/2 cup milk] with a small handful roasted chana [~30g] on the side. No sugar, or just a tsp if you need it.
(P: 18g | C: 28g | F: 4g | ~190 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** 2 rotis [~80g] with 100g paneer bhurji (paneer scrambled with onion, tomato, and green chilli in 1 tsp oil) and a small side salad—cucumber, tomato, lettuce, no dressing or just a squeeze of lemon.
(P: 54g | C: 38g | F: 29g | ~575 kcal)

**Daily total:** P: 128g | C: 174g | F: 52g | ~1550 kcal

**Quick notes:**
- Drink 2–3 litres of water daily, especially on training days. Herbal tea (no sugar) counts.
- On training days (whenever you do your 3 sessions), have an extra 150g curd or a handful of roasted chana post-workout if you feel hungry—it'll help recovery without blowing the plan.
- Paneer and dal are your friends here—they're affordable, filling, and pack protein. Buy paneer in bulk and freeze it if your budget allows.
- If you're bored with a meal halfway through the week, swap any lunch with another lunch, or any dinner with another dinner—the macros are balanced across the board.
- Consistency beats perfection. If you miss a meal or go over one day, just pick up the next day. You've got 12 weeks; one meal won't derail you.

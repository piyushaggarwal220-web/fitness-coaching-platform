**Warmup Routine (10 minutes before every session)**

Start with 5 minutes of brisk walking on the treadmill or easy cycling to get your heart rate up and blood flowing. Then move into some joint mobility: arm circles forward and back (10 each way), hip circles (10 each direction), gentle leg swings front-to-back and side-to-side (10 each leg), and ankle rolls (10 each direction). Finish with a few bodyweight glute bridges (15 reps) and standing knee raises (10 each leg) to wake up your lower body. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe 40% and 60% of your working weight — to groove the movement pattern and prep your joints.

**Day 1 — Lower Body Strength**

*Daily step target: 7,000 steps (training day)*

1. Goblet Squat (controlled depth, stop at 90 degrees) — 4 sets x 6 reps
   *Keep your torso upright, core braced. Go down only to parallel or slightly above — no need to go deep with your knee history. Control the descent, pause at the bottom for a beat, then drive up through your heels.*

2. Leg Press (feet mid-platform, moderate stance) — 3 sets x 10 reps
   *This is knee-friendly as long as you don't let your knees cave in. Push through your whole foot, not just your toes. Stop before your lower back rounds off the pad.*

3. Romanian Deadlift (barbell or dumbbells) — 3 sets x 8 reps
   *Hinge at the hips, slight knee bend, keep the bar close to your shins. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings. No bouncing at the bottom — smooth and controlled.*

4. Seated Leg Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Curl your heels toward your glutes, squeeze at the top for a second, then lower with control. Don't let the weight stack slam.*

5. Cable Pull-Through — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Great glute and hamstring burner. Hinge at the hips, let the cable pull you back slightly, then drive your hips forward to stand tall. Squeeze your glutes hard at the top.*

6. Seated Cable Row (underhand grip) — 4 sets x 10 reps
   *Since you love rows, let's hit them here. Pull to your lower ribs, squeeze your shoulder blades together, keep your chest up. Don't lean back excessively — let your lats do the work.*

7. Plank (on forearms) — 3 sets x 30-45 seconds
   *Keep your hips level, don't let them sag or pike up. Brace your core like someone's about to poke your stomach.*

8. Dead Bug — 3 sets x 10 reps per side
   *Press your lower back into the floor, extend opposite arm and leg slowly, return, switch. Move with control — no rushing.*

9. Standing Cable Crunch — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Hold the rope behind your head, crunch down by flexing your abs, not by pulling with your arms. Exhale hard at the bottom.*

**Day 3 — Upper Body Push**

*Daily step target: 7,000 steps (training day)*

1. Dumbbell Bench Press (flat or incline) — 4 sets x 6 reps
   *Press the dumbbells up and slightly in toward each other at the top. Lower with control until you feel a stretch in your chest. Keep your feet flat on the floor, core tight.*

2. Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press — 3 sets x 8 reps
   *Press straight up, don't arch your lower back excessively. At the top, your biceps should be by your ears. Lower to about ear level, then press back up.*

3. Cable Chest Fly (mid-height) — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Stand in a staggered stance for balance. Bring the handles together in front of your chest with a slight bend in your elbows. Squeeze your chest at the peak, then open back up slowly.*

4. Dumbbell Lateral Raise — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Lift the dumbbells out to your sides until your arms are parallel to the floor. Lead with your elbows, not your hands. Lower slowly — don't just drop them.*

5. Overhead Tricep Extension (cable or dumbbell) — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Keep your elbows close to your head, extend fully at the bottom, squeeze your triceps. Don't let your elbows flare out.*

6. Cable Tricep Pushdown (rope attachment) — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Pull the rope apart at the bottom and squeeze your triceps hard. Keep your elbows pinned to your sides — don't let them drift forward.*

7. Hanging Knee Raise (or Captain's Chair) — 3 sets x 10 reps
   *Lift your knees toward your chest, pause, lower with control. If hanging is tough on your grip, use the captain's chair or a bench for support.*

8. Bicycle Crunch — 3 sets x 15 reps per side
   *Bring your elbow toward the opposite knee, extend the other leg out. Move deliberately — it's not a race.*

9. Side Plank (each side) — 3 sets x 20-30 seconds per side
   *Stack your feet, keep your body in a straight line from head to heels. Don't let your hips sag.*

**Day 5 — Upper Body Pull**

*Daily step target: 7,000 steps (training day)*

1. Lat Pulldown (wide grip) — 4 sets x 8 reps
   *Pull the bar down to your upper chest, lean back slightly, drive your elbows down and back. Squeeze your lats at the bottom, then control the weight back up.*

2. Barbell Bent-Over Row (overhand grip) — 4 sets x 6 reps
   *Hinge at the hips, keep your back flat, pull the bar to your lower chest. This is one of your favorites, so let's load it up a bit. Keep your core tight and don't round your back.*

3. Single-Arm Dumbbell Row — 3 sets x 10 reps per arm
   *Plant one knee and hand on a bench, row the dumbbell up to your hip. Let your shoulder blade move — pull it back at the top.*

4. Face Pull (cable, rope attachment) — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Pull the rope toward your face, elbows high, squeeze your rear delts and upper back. This is great for shoulder health.*

5. Dumbbell Bicep Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Keep your elbows pinned to your sides, curl up with control, squeeze at the top, lower slowly. No swinging.*

6. Hammer Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Same as regular curls, but keep your palms facing each other. Hits your biceps and forearms.*

7. Reverse Crunch — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Lie on your back, lift your hips off the floor by curling your knees toward your chest. Lower with control — don't just drop your legs.*

8. Russian Twist (bodyweight or light dumbbell) — 3 sets x 20 reps total (10 per side)
   *Sit with your knees bent, lean back slightly, twist your torso side to side. Keep your core engaged the whole time.*

9. Plank with Shoulder Tap — 3 sets x 10 taps per side
   *Hold a plank, lift one hand to tap the opposite shoulder, return, switch. Keep your hips stable — don't let them rock side to side.*

**Post-Workout Stretching Routine (5-10 minutes after every training session)**

After your workout, spend a few minutes stretching the muscles you just trained. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds per side, breathe deeply, and don't bounce. Here's a simple flow:

- Standing quad stretch (pull your heel toward your glutes)
- Standing hamstring stretch (prop your heel on a bench, lean forward gently)
- Hip flexor stretch (lunge position, push your hips forward)
- Chest stretch (doorway or corner stretch, arms at shoulder height)
- Lat stretch (grab a pole or doorframe, lean back and to the side)
- Shoulder stretch (pull one arm across your chest with the other hand)
- Tricep stretch (reach one arm overhead, bend at the elbow, pull gently with the other hand)
- Seated spinal twist (sit on the floor, twist gently to each side)

This doesn't need to be complicated — just hit the major muscle groups, breathe, and let your body cool down.

**Training Intensity Guidance**

For your main compound lifts (goblet squats, leg press, rows, bench press, lat pulldowns), leave 1-2 reps in the tank. That means if you could do 8 reps, stop at 6. This keeps the quality high and reduces injury risk, especially as a beginner. For isolation and accessory work (curls, lateral raises, leg curls, etc.), you can push closer to failure — maybe leave just 1 rep in reserve, or even hit true failure on the last set if you're feeling good. On that last set of isolation moves, after you hit strict failure, you can allow a couple of controlled "cheat reps" — a little body english to squeeze out 1-2 more reps — but never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or any barbell compound. Those need to stay strict and controlled for safety.

**Sleep Recommendations**

You're already doing well with 7-8 hours, so let's keep that consistent and maybe push toward the higher end when you can. Aim for 7.5-9 hours per night, ideally getting to bed by 10:30-11:00 pm so you're well-rested for work and training. Here are a few practical tips that fit your lifestyle:

- Cut off chai and coffee by 4:00 pm — caffeine lingers longer than you think, and it can mess with your deep sleep even if you fall asleep fine.
- Keep your phone out of the bedroom, or at least switch it to airplane mode an hour before bed. Scrolling Instagram or WhatsApp right before sleep spikes your cortisol and delays melatonin release.
- Since you're eating dinner around 9:00 pm, try to finish at least 1.5-2 hours before bed. A heavy meal right before sleep can disrupt your rest, especially with paneer or roti.
- Keep your bedroom cool and dark — use a fan or AC if needed, and blackout curtains or an eye mask if light is an issue.
- If your mind races at night (common with moderate stress from work), try 5 minutes of deep breathing before bed: inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 6. Repeat 10 times. It calms your nervous system.
- Stick to the same sleep and wake times on weekends — your body loves routine, and shifting your schedule by 2-3 hours on Saturday-Sunday can throw off your whole week.
- If you wake up to use the bathroom at night, keep the lights dim. Bright light tells your brain it's morning and makes it harder to fall back asleep.

Sleep is where your body repairs and burns fat, so treat it like part of your training plan.

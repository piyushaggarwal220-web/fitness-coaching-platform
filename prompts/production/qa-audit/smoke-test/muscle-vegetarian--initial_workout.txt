**WARMUP (8–10 minutes before every session)**

- 2–3 minutes brisk walk or light jog on the treadmill
- 10 arm circles each direction, 10 leg swings each leg (forward/back and side-to-side)
- 10 bodyweight squats (slow, controlled)
- 5 inchworms
- 10 cat-cow stretches
- 5 glute bridges

Then before your first heavy compound (squat, deadlift, or row), run through 2 lighter warm-up sets — nothing fancy, just get the movement pattern and blood flowing.

---

**DAY 1 — Lower Power (Monday)**

Focus: Strength and size on your lower body. We're leading with squats because they're your jam and they'll drive the most growth.

- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps (heavy, controlled descent, pause 1 second at the bottom)
- Leg Press: 3 sets x 8 reps (after the barbell work, this is your hypertrophy finisher)
- Romanian Deadlift: 3 sets x 6 reps (keep your chest up, feel the hamstring stretch)
- Leg Curl (machine): 3 sets x 10 reps (squeeze at the top, no momentum)
- Core: Barbell Rollouts (or Ab Wheel): 3 sets x 8 reps, then Pallof Press (cable): 3 sets x 10 each side

*Coach's note: On squats, leave 1–2 reps in the tank — we're building strength, not ego. RDLs should feel like a hamstring stretch; don't chase heavy weight here.*

**Daily step target: 6,000–7,000 steps** (training day, so a bit more active than usual)

---

**DAY 2 — Upper Push & Row (Tuesday)**

Focus: Chest, shoulders, and back. Rows are in here because they're essential for balance and your favourite.

- Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 5 reps (chest up, pull elbows back, squeeze shoulder blades)
- Barbell Bench Press (or Dumbbell if you prefer): 4 sets x 6 reps (controlled tempo, pause 1 second at the chest)
- Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets x 8 reps (strict form, no leg drive)
- Cable Chest Fly: 3 sets x 12 reps (feel the stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the middle)
- Core: Dead Bug: 3 sets x 10 each side, then Suitcase Carry (dumbbell): 3 sets x 30 seconds each side

*Coach's note: Rows first — that's where your strength is, so we hit it fresh. On bench, control the descent; that's where the muscle is made.*

**Daily step target: 6,000–7,000 steps** (training day)

**DAY 3 — Active Recovery (Wednesday)**

  - 90/90 hip stretch: 60 seconds each side

Focus: Volume and pump on the lower body. This is higher reps, more metabolic stress.

- Leg Press: 4 sets x 10 reps (full range, controlled tempo)
- Bulgarian Split Squat (dumbbell): 3 sets x 10 each leg (great for unilateral strength and balance)

- Core: Decline Sit-ups (or Ab Bench): 3 sets x 12 reps, then Reverse Crunch: 3 sets x 15 reps

*Coach's note: Higher reps here means you can push closer to failure — take the last set of leg extensions and curls to near-failure, but keep form tight. No sloppy reps.*

**Daily step target: 6,000–7,000 steps** (training day)

**DAYS 6–7 — Rest (Saturday & Sunday)**

Full rest. No gym, no structured training. You can move around (walk, light stretching at home if you feel stiff), but these are recovery days. Your body builds muscle when you rest, so treat this seriously.

**Daily step target: 3,000–4,000 steps** (just normal daily movement, no push)

---

**TRAINING INTENSITY NOTES**

On your compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows, bench), aim to leave 1–2 reps in the tank on most sets — you should feel like you *could* do one or two more, but you stop. This keeps your joints healthy and sustainable. On isolation work (leg curls, chest flys, bicep curls, etc.), you can push closer to failure on the last set of each exercise, and if you want to squeeze out 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" after strict failure, that's fine — but *never* do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those lifts demand perfect form; sloppy reps invite injury.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (5–7 minutes after every session)**

- Quad stretch: 60 seconds each leg (standing or lying)
- Hamstring stretch: 60 seconds each leg
- Hip flexor stretch: 60 seconds each side
- Chest stretch (doorway or cross-body): 60 seconds each side
- Lat stretch (overhead reach): 60 seconds each side
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 60 seconds each arm
- Child's pose or cat-cow: 60 seconds

Keep it slow and breathe deeply — this is your signal to your nervous system that the session is done.

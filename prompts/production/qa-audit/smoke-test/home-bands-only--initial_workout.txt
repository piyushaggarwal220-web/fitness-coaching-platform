**TRAINING INTENSITY & REP QUALITY**

Here's how to approach the weights:

On your **compound lifts** (squats, RDLs, rows, chest press), aim to finish each set with 1–2 reps still in the tank. If you hit 5 reps and could've done 7, you're in the sweet spot. This keeps you safe, builds strength sustainably, and leaves room for progression. Never sacrifice form to chase a rep.

On your **accessory and isolation work** (leg curls, bicep curls, face pulls, glute bridges, etc.), you can push closer to failure — that last set especially. If you hit 12 reps and have nothing left, that's perfect. Only on your final set of isolation moves, if you reach strict failure, you're allowed 1–2 controlled partial reps (like a shortened range of motion) to squeeze out extra volume. But never — and I mean never — do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those demand pristine form always.

Rest 90–120 seconds between heavy compound sets, 60 seconds between accessories. Listen to your body; if you need 10 extra seconds, take it.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already nailing 7–8 hours, which is excellent. Here's how to protect and improve that:

**Target: 7.5–9 hours per night, ideally 11:00 PM–7:00 AM or 11:30 PM–7:30 AM.**

Given your eating schedule (dinner at 9:00 PM), try to finish eating by 9:15–9:30 PM, which gives your digestive system 1.5–2 hours to settle before bed. This helps sleep quality.

**Seven practical tips for your routine:**

1. **Chai cutoff:** No tea or coffee after 5:00 PM. Your evening chai at 6:30 PM is fine, but avoid any caffeine after that — it lingers for 6–8 hours.

2. **Screen time:** Put your phone and laptop away by 10:30 PM. The blue light messes with melatonin. If you must check something, use a blue-light filter or night mode.

3. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 18–20°C if possible, or at least well-ventilated). A cooler room promotes deeper sleep.

4. **Consistent timing:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body loves routine.

5. **Pre-bed wind-down:** 15–20 minutes before sleep, do something calm — read, light stretching, or meditation. Avoid work emails or stressful news.

6. **Hydration:** Drink your 2L of water throughout the day, but taper off by 8:00 PM to avoid nighttime bathroom trips.

7. **Meal timing:** Your 9:00 PM dinner is late; if it's heavy (lots of paneer, roti), it can disrupt sleep. Try to keep dinner lighter on training days — focus on dal, roti, and plenty of veggies. Paneer is fine, but don't overload.

Sleep is where the magic happens — muscle recovery, fat loss hormones, and stress management all depend on it. Protect those hours.

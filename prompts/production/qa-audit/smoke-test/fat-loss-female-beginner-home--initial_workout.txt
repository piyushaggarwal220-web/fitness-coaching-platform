**DAY 1 — LOWER BODY POWER (Monday or whenever you prefer)**

Focus: Heavy compound work with bands, controlled tempo. This is your squat day — we're building strength and power here.

Exercises:
1. Banded Squats — 5 sets x 5 reps
   - Loop your band under your feet and hold the ends. Before you load up, do 2 warm-up sets with lighter band tension to groove the movement. Keep your chest up, weight in your heels, and drive through the whole foot. Think about sitting back into your hips rather than collapsing forward. Leave 2-3 reps in reserve on the main sets.

2. Banded Romanian Deadlifts — 4 sets x 6 reps
   - Same band setup. This is a hip-hinge movement — chest stays proud, slight knee bend, and you're feeling the stretch in your hamstrings. Control the descent; don't bounce at the bottom.

3. Banded Step-Ups (alternating legs) — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
   - Use a sturdy chair or bench. Band around your feet or held at chest. Step up, drive through the front heel, squeeze at the top. This is single-leg strength and balance.

4. Banded Lateral Walks — 3 sets x 12 steps each direction
   - Band around your legs just above the knees. Quarter-squat position, slight forward lean, step sideways with control. Glutes should be firing.

5. Dead Bug — 3 sets x 10 reps each side
   - Lie on your back, arms extended toward ceiling, knees bent 90°. Lower opposite arm and leg slowly, don't let your lower back arch off the floor. This is core control, not speed.

6. Bird Dog — 3 sets x 10 reps each side
   - On hands and knees, extend opposite arm and leg, squeeze your glute at full extension. Pause 1 second, return. Keep your hips level — no rotating.

7. Pallof Hold (using band) — 3 sets x 20 seconds each side
   - Anchor your band to a door or sturdy object at chest height. Stand perpendicular, hold the band at your chest, resist rotation. This teaches anti-rotation stability.

Daily step target: 6,000–7,000 steps. You're training in the evening, so get a walk in during lunch or after work to hit this.

**DAY 2 — UPPER BODY & ROWS (Wednesday or the day after your rest day)**

Focus: Pulling strength, horizontal and vertical pressing, shoulder stability. Your rows are the star here.

Exercises:
1. Banded Rows (high anchor) — 5 sets x 5 reps
   - Anchor band high, hinge slightly at hips, pull with elbows, squeeze your shoulder blades together hard at the end. This is a strength set — heavy tension, controlled. Think about pulling your elbows to your hips, not your hands to your chest. Do 2 warm-up sets first.

2. Banded Face Pulls — 4 sets x 10 reps
   - Band anchored at eye level. Pull toward your face, externally rotate your shoulders at the end (think "W" shape with your arms). This is shoulder health and rear delt work.

3. Banded Chest Press (standing or kneeling) — 4 sets x 8 reps
   - Band around your back, hold ends at chest, press forward. Control the eccentric (the return) — 2 seconds out, 3 seconds back. Squeeze hard at full extension.

4. Banded Overhead Press — 3 sets x 8 reps
   - Band under your feet, press overhead. Keep your core tight, don't arch your lower back excessively. Pause briefly at the top.

5. Banded Bicep Curls — 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Band under feet, curl up. Squeeze at the top for 1 second, lower with control. You can go a bit closer to failure here — aim for the last 1-2 reps to feel tough.

6. Banded Tricep Extensions (overhead) — 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Band overhead, elbows bent, extend arms upward. Again, controlled descent. These can go closer to failure.

7. Plank to Down-Dog — 3 sets x 10 reps
   - Start in plank, push your hips up and back into a down-dog position, return to plank. Core and shoulder stability.

8. Dead Bug — 3 sets x 10 reps each side
   - Same as Day 1. Consistency builds core strength.

Daily step target: 5,500–6,500 steps. This is a training day, so a lighter step count is fine — focus on the quality of the session.

**DAY 3 — FULL BODY BALANCE (Friday or Saturday)**

Focus: Compound movements, metabolic conditioning, total-body integration. Shorter rest periods, higher density.

Exercises:
1. Banded Squats — 4 sets x 6 reps
   - Same setup as Day 1, but slightly lighter band tension to allow for faster reps. Still controlled, still leaving 2 reps in reserve.

2. Banded Rows (lower anchor) — 4 sets x 6 reps
   - Anchor the band lower this time (around waist height). More of a horizontal row. Heavy, strong pulls.

3. Banded Lateral Raises — 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Band under feet, raise arms out to shoulder height. Light and controlled, squeeze at the top. Shoulder stability and aesthetics.

4. Banded Reverse Pec Deck (or band pull-aparts) — 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Hold band at chest width, pull the band apart to your sides, squeeze shoulder blades. Lighter weight, higher reps, great for posture.

5. Banded Leg Curls (lying or standing) — 3 sets x 12 reps
   - Anchor band low, loop around your foot, curl your heel toward your glute. Hamstring isolation.

6. Banded Glute Bridges — 3 sets x 15 reps
   - Band around your thighs above the knees, lie on your back, feet flat, drive hips up. Squeeze glutes hard at the top. This can go closer to failure — feel the burn.

7. Hollow Body Hold — 3 sets x 20 seconds
   - Lie on your back, arms extended overhead, legs straight, press your lower back into the floor, tighten everything. Full-body tension and core awareness.

8. Side Plank — 3 sets x 20 seconds each side
   - Keep your body in a straight line, engage your obliques and glutes. This is anti-lateral flexion strength.

Daily step target: 7,000–8,000 steps. You've got energy on this day — use it. A walk in the morning or during the day will help you hit this.

**TRAINING INTENSITY & REP RANGES**

Here's how to think about effort:

- **Compound lifts** (squats, deadlifts, rows, presses): Leave 2–3 reps in reserve on your main sets. If you could theoretically do 8 reps, stop at 5–6. This builds strength safely and lets you recover for the next session.

- **Isolation work** (curls, raises, leg curls, face pulls): Push these harder. Aim to finish the last set with 1–2 reps left in the tank, or even go to true failure on the very last set of each exercise. You can use a couple of controlled "cheat reps" here if needed (like slight body English on a bicep curl), but never on barbell or band compounds like squats, deadlifts, or rows.

- **Core exercises**: These should feel controlled and deliberate. Core isn't about going to failure; it's about tension and stability.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (5–7 minutes after each session)**

Don't skip this — it helps with recovery and mobility:

- Quad stretch (standing or lying): 45 seconds each leg
- Hamstring stretch (seated or standing): 45 seconds each leg
- Hip flexor stretch (low lunge): 45 seconds each side
- Chest stretch (doorway or band): 45 seconds each side
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 30 seconds each arm
- Child's pose: 60 seconds
- Cat-cow (5 slow reps)

Just breathe, relax, and let your body settle. This is as important as the workout itself.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already doing great with 7–8 hours — let's lock that in and maybe push toward 7.5–9 hours. Sleep is where the magic happens for fat loss and recovery.

Ideal timing: Aim to be in bed by 10:30–11:00 PM, which gives you a solid 8 hours before a typical 7:00–7:30 AM wake-up.

Practical tips for better sleep:

1. **Cut chai by 5:00 PM** — I know chai is life, but caffeine after 5 PM will mess with your sleep. Enjoy your evening tea earlier, or switch to herbal/decaf after lunch.

2. **No screens 30–45 minutes before bed** — Phone, laptop, TV. The blue light keeps your brain wired. Use this time to read, stretch, or just lie down.

3. **Dinner timing** — You're eating dinner at 9:00 PM, which is late. Try to move it to 8:00–8:30 PM if possible, so you're not digesting heavily when you sleep. If 9:00 PM is fixed, keep portions reasonable and lighter (more roti and salad, less paneer).

4. **Cool room** — Keep your bedroom cool (around 65–68°F / 18–20°C if possible). A fan or AC helps. Your body sleeps better when it's cool.

5. **Consistent bedtime** — Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body loves routine.

6. **Water cutoff** — Stop heavy water intake 1–2 hours before bed so you're not waking up for bathroom trips. You're already hitting 2L during the day, which is solid.

7. **Stress management** — Moderate stress is normal, but if you're wound up before bed, try 5 minutes of deep breathing (4 seconds in, 6 seconds out) or progressive muscle relaxation. This calms your nervous system.

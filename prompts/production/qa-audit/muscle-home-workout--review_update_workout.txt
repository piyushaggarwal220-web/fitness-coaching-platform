I see you managed one workout this week despite work and family chaos — that's a win, not a failure. Life happens, and showing up even once means you're still in the game. This week we're simplifying to help you rebuild momentum. Instead of the upper/lower/full split, we're moving to a 2-body-part push/pull structure across your 4 days to keep sessions efficient (~45 min) and reduce decision fatigue. You'll hit each major movement pattern twice per week with moderate volume so you can stay consistent without feeling buried.

**Weekly Structure:**
- **Day 1 (Monday): Push A** — Chest + Shoulders focus
- **Day 2 (Wednesday): Pull A** — Back + Biceps focus  
- **Day 3 (Friday): Push B** — Shoulders + Triceps focus
- **Day 4 (Saturday): Pull B** — Back + Lower Body focus
- Rest days: Tuesday, Thursday, Sunday

**Warmup (every session):** 5 min light cardio in place (march, jog, jumping jacks) + 10 arm circles each direction + 10 bodyweight squats

**Cooldown (every session):** 3–5 min walk in place or slow march + static stretches for worked muscles (hold 20–30 sec each)

**Daily Step Target:** Aim for 6000 steps — just 1000 more than usual. Break it into 3 short walks if needed.

---

**Day 1: Push A — Chest + Shoulders**
1. Dumbbell Bench Press — 4 sets x 8 reps (2 min rest)
2. Dumbbell Shoulder Press — 3 sets x 10 reps (90 sec rest)
3. Incline Dumbbell Press (bench at 30°) — 3 sets x 10 reps (90 sec rest)
4. Dumbbell Lateral Raise — 3 sets x 12 reps (60 sec rest)
5. Push-Ups (on knees if needed) — 2 sets x AMRAP (as many reps as possible, 60 sec rest)

---

**Day 2: Pull A — Back + Biceps**
1. Bent-Over Dumbbell Row (both arms) — 4 sets x 8 reps (2 min rest)
2. Single-Arm Dumbbell Row — 3 sets x 10 reps per arm (90 sec rest)
3. Dumbbell Pullover (lie across bench) — 3 sets x 12 reps (90 sec rest)
4. Dumbbell Bicep Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps (60 sec rest)
5. Hammer Curl — 2 sets x 15 reps (60 sec rest)

---

**Day 3: Push B — Shoulders + Triceps**
1. Dumbbell Shoulder Press (standing or seated) — 4 sets x 8 reps (2 min rest)
2. Dumbbell Front Raise — 3 sets x 10 reps (90 sec rest)
3. Dumbbell Lateral Raise — 3 sets x 12 reps (60 sec rest)
4. Overhead Dumbbell Tricep Extension — 3 sets x 10 reps (90 sec rest)
5. Dumbbell Kickbacks — 3 sets x 12 reps per arm (60 sec rest)

---

**Day 4: Pull B — Back + Lower Body**
1. Goblet Squat — 4 sets x 10 reps (2 min rest)
2. Bent-Over Dumbbell Row — 3 sets x 10 reps (90 sec rest)
3. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift — 3 sets x 10 reps (90 sec rest)
4. Dumbbell Reverse Fly (bent over or on bench) — 3 sets x 12 reps (60 sec rest)
5. Dumbbell Goblet Squat (lighter, tempo 3-0-1) — 2 sets x 12 reps (60 sec rest)

---

**Cardio:**
- 3 sessions of 25–30 min brisk walk (aim for moderate pace, conversational but purposeful)
- Schedule: ideally on rest days (Tuesday, Thursday, Sunday) or post-workout if time allows

**Recovery Notes:**
- Prioritize 7–8 hours sleep — non-negotiable for muscle repair and energy.
- Drink 2.5–3L water daily (especially on training days).
- If a session feels impossible, do just the first 2 exercises and call it done. Showing up > perfection.

**Progression:**
If you complete all 4 workouts this week with good form, next week we'll add 1–2 reps per set or increase dumbbell weight by 1–2 kg on compound lifts (bench, row, squat, shoulder press). If life throws curveballs again, we stay here and build the habit first — strength will follow consistency.

You've got this, Meera. One workout at a time.

**WARM-UP (Every Session — 10 minutes)**
Start every evening with: 2 minutes brisk walk or light jog on the treadmill, 5–6 arm circles and leg swings each side, 10 bodyweight squats, 10 push-ups (or incline push-ups if needed), 5–10 cat-cow stretches. Get the blood moving and loosen up the joints. Then before your first heavy compound lift, knock out 2 lighter warm-up sets to dial in the movement.

---

**Day 1 — Lower Power (Monday)**
This is your squat day. Heavy, controlled, and the foundation of your muscle-building week.

• Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps — leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set. Pause for a half-second at the bottom; this builds strength and control.
• Leg Press: 3 sets x 8 reps — after the squat, your legs are primed. Keep the tempo steady, no bouncing at the bottom.
• Walking Lunges (dumbbells): 3 sets x 10 reps per leg — controlled steps, chest up. This is your unilateral strength work.
• Cable Woodchops (standing): 3 sets x 12 reps per side — anti-rotation core work, keeps your spine stable.
• Hanging Leg Raises (or cable machine if needed): 2 sets x 8–10 reps — strict form, no swinging.
• Pallof Press (cable): 2 sets x 12 reps per side — finish with this for core endurance.

Target steps for today: 7,500–8,000 (include your walk to/from the gym).

---

**Day 2 — Upper Hypertrophy (Tuesday)**
This is where we pump up the chest, shoulders, and arms. Higher reps, more volume, closer to failure.

• Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 6 reps — strong and controlled. Leave 1–2 reps in reserve; we're building strength with some hypertrophy.
• Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 10 reps — upper chest gets extra love here. Lower the dumbbells with control, squeeze at the top.
• Cable Flyes: 3 sets x 12 reps — stretch at the bottom, feel the chest working. You can push closer to failure on this one.
• Dumbbell Lateral Raises: 3 sets x 12 reps — shoulder width and definition. Light weight, perfect form, no jerking.
• Barbell Curls: 3 sets x 8 reps — compound arm work first. Strict form, elbows locked at the sides.
• Tricep Rope Pushdowns: 2 sets x 12 reps — finish the arms with a squeeze at full extension.
• Ab Wheel Rollouts (or cable machine variation): 2 sets x 8–10 reps — anti-extension core work.

Target steps: 6,500–7,000 (lighter day, but still moving).

---

**Day 3 — Lower Hypertrophy (Wednesday)**
Rowing, deadlifting, and posterior chain focus. This balances Monday's squat day.

• Barbell Deadlift: 4 sets x 5 reps — your second heavy compound. Drive through the heels, keep your chest up. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank.
• Barbell Rows: 4 sets x 6 reps — one of your favorites. Retract the scapula hard, drive the elbows back. This is strength work, so keep it controlled and heavy.
• Leg Curls (machine): 3 sets x 10 reps — hamstring isolation, tempo: 2 seconds down, 1 second up.
• Bulgarian Split Squats (dumbbells): 3 sets x 10 reps per leg — single-leg strength and hypertrophy.
• Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 10 reps — posterior chain stretch and strength. Hinge at the hips, feel the hamstring.
• Cable Crunches: 3 sets x 12 reps — flexion work for the abs.
• Side Plank Hold: 2 sets x 30–45 seconds per side — lateral core stability.

Target steps: 7,500–8,000 (good training day, move well).

**Day 4 — Active Recovery (Thursday)**
No heavy lifting today. This is mobility, light movement, and preparation for the final push.

Instead of a workout: 20–30 minutes of easy walking (treadmill or outside), light stretching routine covering all major muscle groups (hamstrings, hip flexors, chest, shoulders, lower back — 30–60 seconds each), 10 minutes of gentle yoga or mobility work (cat-cow, child's pose, thread-the-needle, wrist circles, ankle circles). Keep your heart rate low, focus on breathing and loosening up.

Target steps: 8,000–9,000 (this is your active recovery day, so prioritize movement and walking to hit your step count).

**Day 5 — Upper Power (Friday)**
Final session of the week. Heavy rows, pressing, and some arm work to finish strong.

• Barbell Rows: 4 sets x 5 reps — heavy rows again, but lower reps this time. Maximum strength. Retract hard, drive those elbows back.
• Overhead Press (barbell): 4 sets x 5 reps — vertical pressing power. Strict form, no leg drive (this is shoulder and chest, not a push press). Leave 1–2 reps in the tank.
• Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets x 8 reps per side — unilateral back work. One knee on the bench, drive the elbow high.
• Dumbbell Bench Press: 3 sets x 8 reps — unilateral pressing. More range of motion than barbell, more core stability needed.
• Face Pulls (cable): 3 sets x 12 reps — rear delts and shoulder health. Squeeze the rear delts at full contraction.
• Dumbbell Hammer Curls: 2 sets x 10 reps — neutral grip arm work, finish the session strong.
• Decline Sit-ups: 2 sets x 12 reps — flexion-focused core work to close out the week.
• Dead Bugs (bodyweight): 2 sets x 10 reps per side — anti-extension core stability.

Target steps: 6,500–7,000 (last heavy session, don't push the step count too hard).

**Day 6 & 7 — Rest (Saturday & Sunday)**
Complete rest days. No training, but stay active.

Target steps: 8,000–10,000 each day. This is your time to recover, but don't sit around all day. Go for a walk, spend time with family, do light stretching if you feel tight, and prepare for the week ahead. Get your meal prep sorted if you can.

**Training Intensity & Effort**

Here's how to think about effort: On your main compounds (squats, deadlifts, bench, overhead press, rows), you should leave 1–2 reps in reserve. This means you could do one or two more reps if you really pushed, but you don't. This keeps you strong, safe, and able to recover for the next session.

On isolation and hypertrophy work (leg curls, flyes, lateral raises, curls, pushdowns), you can push closer to failure. If you hit failure on a set, you can do 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" after that — but only on isolation moves, never on compounds like squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges. The idea is to extend the set and squeeze out a bit more volume, but keep your spine and joints safe.

If you're unsure, err on the side of caution. Consistency beats heroics every single time.

---

**Cool-Down & Stretching (After Every Session — 5–7 minutes)**

Don't skip this. After your last set, spend a few minutes stretching the muscle groups you just trained:

• Quad Stretch (standing or lying): 30–45 seconds each leg
• Hamstring Stretch (seated or standing): 30–45 seconds each side
• Hip Flexor Stretch (low lunge): 30–45 seconds each side
• Chest Stretch (doorway or cross-body): 30–45 seconds each side
• Shoulder Stretch (cross-body arm pull): 30–45 seconds each side
• Lower Back Stretch (child's pose or knee-to-chest): 45–60 seconds

Breathe deeply, relax, and let your body transition out of "workout mode." This also helps with recovery and mobility over time.

**WARMUP ROUTINE (10 minutes — do this before every session)**

Start with a 2–3 minute brisk walk or light jog on the treadmill to get your heart rate up and joints moving. Then flow through:
- 10 arm circles each direction
- 10 bodyweight squats (slow, full range)
- 10 cat-cows (on hands and knees, smooth spinal flexion/extension)
- 5 inchworms
- 10 glute bridges
- 10 scapular pull-ups on the pull-up bar (just hanging and retracting your shoulder blades — no heavy pulling yet)

Then move to your first compound lift and knock out 2 warm-up sets — nothing heavy, just 50% of your working weight for 5–8 reps each. This preps your nervous system and gets your form dialed in.

**DAY 1 — LOWER POWER & CORE**

Focus: Heavy compound leg work, controlled tempo, explosive intent.

**Main Lifts:**

**Core:**
- Ab Wheel Rollout (or Kneeling version): 3 sets × 8–12 reps (tough one — control the eccentric)

**TRAINING INTENSITY & REP RANGES — HOW TO APPROACH IT**

On your heavy compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows, bench, overhead press), aim for 3–6 reps and leave 1–2 reps in reserve each set — you should feel like you *could* grind out 1–2 more, but you don't. This keeps you safe, especially as you build volume over time, and it means your form stays clean. On isolation and accessory work (leg curls, flyes, lateral raises, curls), push a bit harder — aim to hit close to failure on your last 1–2 sets. If you hit strict failure and want to squeeze out 1–2 more controlled reps with a tiny bit of momentum, that's fine on isolation work, but *never* do this on barbell compounds like squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges — form breaks down fast there and injury risk shoots up. Save your cheat reps for machine and dumbbell accessory work only.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (5–10 minutes after every session)**

After your last set, spend a few minutes on these major muscle groups:

- **Quads:** Quad stretch (standing or lying), 30–45 seconds each leg
- **Hamstrings:** Forward fold or seated hamstring stretch, 45–60 seconds
- **Hip Flexors:** Kneeling lunge stretch, 30–45 seconds each side
- **Chest & Shoulders:** Cross-body shoulder stretch, 30–45 seconds each arm
- **Lats & Back:** Child's pose or lat stretch against a pole, 45–60 seconds
- **Glutes:** Pigeon pose or figure-4 stretch, 30–45 seconds each side

Just breathe, relax, and let your muscles settle. This helps with soreness and keeps you mobile.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already hitting 7–8 hours, which is solid — let's aim for 7.5–9 hours most nights. Here's what I'd suggest for your schedule:

**Ideal bedtime:** 11:00 PM–11:30 PM (gives you 7.5–8.5 hours before a reasonable wake time around 6:30–7:00 AM).

**Practical tips for better sleep:**

1. **Cut chai/tea after 5:00 PM** — I know chai is life, but the caffeine will mess with your sleep. Have your 6:30 PM snack with water or herbal tea instead.

2. **Dinner timing:** You're eating at 9:00 PM, which is late. Try to eat by 8:30 PM if possible — gives your body 2.5–3 hours to digest before bed, which helps sleep quality.

3. **Phone/screen cutoff:** Put your phone down by 10:30 PM. The blue light messes with melatonin production. I know it's hard, but it's worth it.

4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 18–20°C if possible) and dark. If you're in a hot climate, a fan helps.

5. **Pre-bed routine:** 15–20 minutes before bed, do some light stretching or breathing — box breathing (4 count in, 4 count hold, 4 count out) is great for calming your nervous system.

6. **Hydration:** Drink most of your 2L water earlier in the day — aim to finish by 7:00 PM so you're not waking up for bathroom trips at night.

7. **Avoid heavy meals close to bed:** Your 9:00 PM dinner is on the later side, so keep it lighter than lunch — roti, paneer, and salad is good, just don't go overboard with portions.

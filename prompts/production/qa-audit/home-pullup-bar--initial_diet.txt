**MONDAY**

(P: 18g | C: 68g | F: 7g | ~420 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm):** Tea with 1 cup roasted chana [~80g] and a small handful of peanuts [~20g].
(P: 16g | C: 28g | F: 10g | ~280 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** 2 rotis [~80g], 150g paneer bhurji (paneer scrambled with onion and tomato), and a small bowl of salad — cucumber, tomato, lettuce [~80g]. Mix 1 tbsp curd [~15g] into the paneer.

**A few practical notes as you go:**

— You're eating at 9pm for dinner, which is late, but that's your routine so we're sticking with it. Just make sure you're not lying down immediately after — give yourself at least 30 mins to wind down.

— Your roasted chana at 6:30pm is solid — it's filling, high protein, and pairs perfectly with tea. If you ever want to swap it for a small bowl of peanuts or a handful of mixed nuts, go ahead; the macros are similar.

— Dal is your best friend here. It's cheap, it's protein-packed, and it's versatile. Don't be afraid to rotate between arhar, moong, chana, and masoor — variety keeps things interesting and hits different micronutrients.

— Paneer can get pricey if you're buying it fresh every day. If budget's tight some weeks, swap a paneer meal for 150g soya chunks (soaked and cooked) or an extra katori of dal with an extra roti. Protein lands in the same ballpark.

— Ghee is your friend for flavor and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins. 1 tsp per meal is the sweet spot — don't go overboard, but don't skip it either.

— On your training days (sounds like you're hitting it 4 days a week in the evenings), you might feel a bit hungry post-workout around 10pm. If that happens, have a small glass of milk [~200ml] or a handful of peanuts [~30g] — it'll settle you without derailing anything.

— Stick to 2L water daily as you mentioned, and if you're training hard, bump it up slightly on those days.

You've got a solid routine, a clear goal, and now a plan that fits your life. The key to beating that consistency struggle is keeping it simple — same meal times, foods you actually like, nothing fancy. Trust the process, hit your meals, and show up for your training.

Let's go get those 8kg off and build some serious strength. You've got this.

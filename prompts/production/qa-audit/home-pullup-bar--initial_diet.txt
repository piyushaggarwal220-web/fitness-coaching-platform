**A few quick notes as you go:**

- **Protein boost on training days:** On the days you're hitting your pull-ups and dips in the evening, consider adding an extra glass of milk or a small bowl of curd after your workout — it'll help with recovery without bloating you before dinner at 9.

- **Dal variety:** Don't stick to just one dal all week. Rotate between moong, masoor, chana dal, and toor — it keeps things interesting and you get different nutrients. Plus, your budget stretches further when you buy in bulk.

- **Paneer hack:** If paneer feels expensive some weeks, swap it for soya chunks (soaked and cooked with the same spices) — nearly identical protein, half the cost.

- **Ghee and oil:** I've kept it minimal (1 tsp per meal where needed), but don't shy away from it — a little ghee with your dal or sabzi actually helps with nutrient absorption and keeps you satisfied.

- **Water:** You mentioned 2L a day — that's a solid baseline. On training days, try to push it to 2.5–3L, especially since you're training in the evening. Your body will thank you.

- **Flexibility:** If you're not in the mood for rice one day, swap it for 2–3 rotis instead. The macros stay roughly the same, and it keeps you from getting bored.

You've got a solid routine already — consistent meal times, foods you actually enjoy, and a realistic budget. This plan builds on that. Stick with it for the week, track how you feel (energy, recovery, hunger levels), and we'll adjust if needed.

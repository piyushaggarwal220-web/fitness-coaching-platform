Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Manish!

Alright, let's get you started on a solid week of eating to support that muscle gain goal. I know you're juggling office work and hitting the gym 4 evenings a week, so I've kept this practical and built around the foods you already enjoy — dal, paneer, idli, curd rice — with enough protein to fuel recovery without blowing your ₹8k budget. You'll see some variety across the week, but nothing fancy or time-consuming. Let's make this one count.

---

**Day 1 (Monday)**

**Breakfast — 8:30 am**
Have 3 idlis (approx 150g total) with 1 tablespoon coconut chutney and a small bowl of sambar (1 katori, around 150ml). Or go for 1.5 cups poha (flattened rice, cooked) with peanuts (15g) and some green chutney on the side.
(P: 12g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~350 kcal)

**Lunch — 1:30 pm**
1 cup cooked rice (approx 150g), 1 katori moong dal (around 150g cooked), 1 katori mixed veg sabzi (beans, carrot, peas — about 100g), and a small bowl of curd (100g). Simple, filling, and hits the spot.
(P: 18g | C: 72g | F: 6g | ~420 kcal)

**Evening Snack — 6:30 pm** (Pre-Gym)
Your usual tea with 30g roasted chana. If you're still a bit hungry before heading out, add a banana.
(P: 6g | C: 32g | F: 2g | ~170 kcal)

**Post-Workout — Around 7:45 pm**
1 scoop whey protein (25g powder) mixed in water or milk (200ml). If you're using milk, that bumps the protein and cals a bit — totally fine.
(P: 26g | C: 6g | F: 2g | ~145 kcal with water)

**Dinner — 9:00 pm**
2 rotis (approx 60g total), 100g paneer bhurji cooked with onions and tomatoes, a side of cucumber-tomato salad (no dressing needed, just a pinch of salt and lemon), and 100g curd on the side.
(P: 28g | C: 46g | F: 18g | ~460 kcal)

**Before Bed** (optional, if hungry)
1 glass milk (200ml) or a small handful of roasted peanuts (15g).
(P: 7g | C: 10g | F: 7g | ~130 kcal)

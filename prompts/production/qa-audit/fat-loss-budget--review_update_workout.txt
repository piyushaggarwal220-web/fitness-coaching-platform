Ravi,

I see you skipped 2 workouts this week and motivation has dipped. That's completely normal — we all have tough weeks. The key now is to simplify and rebuild consistency. You don't need a harder plan; you need one that's easier to start and finish.

This week I'm keeping you on 4 days but reducing the session complexity. Each day will hit 2 muscle groups instead of full-body or single upper/lower splits. This gives you variety without overwhelming you. Volume stays similar, but exercises are streamlined so you spend less time deciding what to do and more time doing it. We'll keep squats and rows since you enjoy them, and I've made sure every session fits in 60 minutes including warmup and cooldown.

Your job this week: show up for all 4 sessions. Even if energy is low, do the warmup and first exercise — momentum will carry you through the rest.

**Weekly Structure:**
- Day 1 (Monday): Chest + Back
- Day 2 (Wednesday): Legs + Shoulders
- Day 3 (Friday): Chest + Back
- Day 4 (Saturday): Legs + Shoulders
- Rest: Tuesday, Thursday, Sunday

**Warmup (every session, 5 min):**
- 3 min brisk walk or cycle
- 10 arm circles forward and backward
- 10 bodyweight squats
- 10 scapular retractions

---

**Day 1 — Chest + Back (Monday)**

1. Barbell Bench Press — 4 sets x 8 reps
   Rest 90 sec between sets. Use a weight you can control for all 8 reps with 1-2 reps left in reserve.

2. Barbell Bent-Over Row — 4 sets x 8 reps
   Rest 90 sec. Keep your back flat and pull to your lower ribs.

3. Dumbbell Incline Press — 3 sets x 10 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Set bench to 30-45 degrees.

4. Cable Seated Row — 3 sets x 12 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Squeeze shoulder blades together at the end of each rep.

5. Cable Chest Fly — 2 sets x 15 reps
   Rest 45 sec. Light weight, focus on stretch and squeeze.

Cooldown: 3 min walk, chest and back static stretches (30 sec each).

Daily step target: 6000 steps.

---

**Day 2 — Legs + Shoulders (Wednesday)**

1. Barbell Back Squat — 4 sets x 8 reps
   Rest 2 min. Go to parallel or just below. Control the descent.

2. Leg Press — 3 sets x 12 reps
   Rest 90 sec. Feet shoulder-width, press through heels.

3. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift — 3 sets x 10 reps
   Rest 90 sec. Hinge at hips, keep knees slightly bent, feel hamstring stretch.

4. Dumbbell Shoulder Press — 3 sets x 10 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Press overhead, don't arch your lower back.

5. Dumbbell Lateral Raise — 3 sets x 12 reps
   Rest 45 sec. Raise to shoulder height, control the descent.

Cooldown: 3 min walk, quad, hamstring, and shoulder stretches (30 sec each).

Daily step target: 6000 steps.

---

**Day 3 — Chest + Back (Friday)**

1. Dumbbell Bench Press — 4 sets x 8 reps
   Rest 90 sec. Lower dumbbells until you feel a stretch in your chest.

2. Cable Lat Pulldown — 4 sets x 10 reps
   Rest 90 sec. Pull bar to upper chest, lean back slightly.

3. Dumbbell Chest Press on Floor — 3 sets x 12 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Lie on floor, press dumbbells up, elbows touch floor each rep.

4. Cable Face Pull — 3 sets x 15 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Pull rope to face level, squeeze rear delts and upper back.

5. Push-Ups — 2 sets x AMRAP (as many reps as possible)
   Rest 60 sec. Go to failure or stop 1 rep before failure.

Cooldown: 3 min walk, chest and lat stretches (30 sec each).

Daily step target: 6000 steps.

---

**Day 4 — Legs + Shoulders (Saturday)**

1. Barbell Front Squat or Goblet Squat — 4 sets x 10 reps
   Rest 90 sec. If front squat is uncomfortable, use goblet squat with dumbbell.

2. Leg Curl (lying or seated) — 3 sets x 12 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Control the eccentric (lowering) phase.

3. Leg Extension — 3 sets x 12 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Squeeze quads at the top.

4. Barbell or Dumbbell Upright Row — 3 sets x 10 reps
   Rest 60 sec. Pull to chest height, elbows lead the movement.

5. Cable Lateral Raise — 3 sets x 15 reps
   Rest 45 sec. Use single handle, one arm at a time or both together.

Cooldown: 3 min walk, hip flexor, hamstring, and shoulder stretches (30 sec each).

Daily step target: 6000 steps.

---

**Cardio:**
Add 2 x 20 min brisk walks on rest days (Tuesday and Thursday). These are optional if you hit your daily step target, but they help with recovery and calorie burn.

**Recovery:**
Sunday is full rest. Sleep 7-8 hours every night. Drink 2-3 liters of water daily.

**Progression:**
If you complete all 4 workouts this week with good form, we'll add 2.5-5 kg to your main lifts (squat, bench, row) next week. If you miss a session, we repeat this week — no shame, just consistency.

Let's get back on track, Ravi. One workout at a time.

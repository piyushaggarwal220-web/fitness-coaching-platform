Here's how your week flows: we're running a Push-Pull-Lower-Upper split across 4 days. This gives your body enough recovery between hitting the same movement patterns, lets you stay fresh for compound lifts, and keeps things interesting. Each session is designed to fit your 60-minute window, so no fluff — just solid work.

Before every session, spend about 10 minutes warming up. Start with a brisk 2-3 minute walk or light jog on the treadmill to get your heart rate up and loosen your joints. Then move through some dynamic stretches: arm circles, leg swings (forward and sideways, 10 each side), hip circles, and a few bodyweight squats and pushups to activate your muscles. This preps you for what's coming. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — nothing heavy, just to groove the movement and dial in your form.

One more thing on intensity: on your compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows, bench), you should leave a rep or two in the tank most days — that's your safety margin while you're building technique. On isolation work — your leg extensions, cable flyes, that kind of thing — you can push harder, getting close to failure on your last set or two. And only on those isolation moves, if you've hit strict failure, you can squeeze out a couple of controlled "cheat reps" to extend the set. Never do this on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound — form breaks down too easily and injury risk shoots up. Keep it clean.

**Day 1 — Lower Power (Emphasis on Strength)**

Focus: Heavy compound work, explosive movement, solid core stability.

Warm-up: 2–3 min light cardio → arm circles, leg swings, hip mobility → 2 bodyweight squats, 5 pushups.

**Main Work:**
- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets × 4–5 reps (rest 3 min between sets)
  *Cue: Chest up, knees track over toes, depth to parallel or just below. Control the descent — don't bounce.*
- Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets × 6 reps (rest 2.5 min)
  *This hits your posterior chain hard and complements the squat beautifully.*
- Leg Press: 3 sets × 8 reps (rest 90 sec)
  *Lower weight, higher reps here — this is your hypertrophy accessory to build muscle endurance.*

**Core Work:**
- Barbell Rollout: 3 sets × 8–10 reps (rest 60 sec)
- Dead Bug: 3 sets × 12 reps each side (rest 60 sec)

Post-workout: 5-min walk to cool down. Stretch your quads, hamstrings, hip flexors, glutes — hold each for 45 seconds.

**Daily Step Target:** 8,000–10,000 steps (training day — you'll naturally accumulate steps around the gym, plus your commute).

---

**Day 2 — Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)**

Focus: Hypertrophy and pressing strength.

Warm-up: 2–3 min light cardio → arm circles (20 each direction), band pull-aparts (15 reps), 5 light pushups.

**Main Work:**
- Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets × 5–6 reps (rest 3 min)
  *Shoulders back, feet planted, lower the bar to your chest with control. This is your main upper-body strength builder.*
- Dumbbell Incline Press: 3 sets × 8–10 reps (rest 90 sec)
  *Hits the upper chest and front delts. Squeeze at the top.*
- Cable Flye: 3 sets × 12–15 reps (rest 60 sec)
  *Lighter weight, higher reps — feel the chest working. You can push the last set close to failure here.*

**Core Work:**
- Pallof Press (cable): 3 sets × 10 reps each side (rest 60 sec)
- Plank Hold: 3 sets × 40–50 seconds (rest 60 sec)

Post-workout: Light walk, stretch chest (doorway stretch), shoulders, triceps — 45 sec each.

**Daily Step Target:** 8,000–10,000 steps (training day).

---

**Day 3 — Active Recovery (Mobility & Light Movement)**

No heavy lifting today. This is your active recovery day.

- 15–20 min easy walk (treadmill at 3.5–4 mph or outdoor walk, whatever feels good)
- 10 min full-body mobility flow:
  - Cat-cow stretches: 10 reps
  - World's greatest stretch: 5 each side
  - Pigeon pose: 60 sec each side
  - Child's pose: 60 sec
  - Thoracic rotations: 10 each side
  - Shoulder dislocates with a light band or stick: 15 reps

This day is about moving without intensity, flushing out soreness, and keeping your joints healthy.

**Daily Step Target:** 6,000–8,000 steps (recovery day — keep it light and easy).

---

**Day 4 — Pull (Back, Biceps, Rear Delts)**

Focus: Back strength and thickness, pulling power.

Warm-up: 2–3 min light cardio → arm circles, band pull-aparts (15 reps), 5 light inverted rows or lat pulldowns.

**Main Work:**
- Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets × 5–6 reps (rest 3 min)
  *Your favorite, and for good reason. Chest to the bar, elbows tucked. This builds a strong, thick back.*
- Lat Pulldown (or assisted pull-ups if you're strong enough): 3 sets × 8 reps (rest 90 sec)
  *Pull elbows down and back. Chest up. Feel the lats engage.*
- Single-Arm Dumbbell Row: 3 sets × 10 reps each side (rest 60 sec)
  *Unilateral work — catches strength imbalances. Squeeze the scapula back.*

**Core Work:**
- Ab Wheel Rollout (or modified on knees): 3 sets × 8–10 reps (rest 90 sec)
- Bird Dog: 3 sets × 12 reps each side (rest 60 sec)

Post-workout: Light walk, stretch back (child's pose, cat-cow), lats (overhead stretch), biceps — 45 sec each.

**Daily Step Target:** 8,000–10,000 steps (training day).

---

**Day 5 — Upper Hypertrophy (Chest, Back, Shoulders)**

Focus: Muscle building, higher reps, metabolic work.

Warm-up: 2–3 min light cardio → arm circles, band pull-aparts (15), 3 light pushups, 3 light rows.

**Main Work:**
- Dumbbell Bench Press: 3 sets × 8–10 reps (rest 90 sec)
  *Lower weight than barbell, but more range of motion. Good for chest development.*
- Barbell Rows (lighter weight, higher reps): 3 sets × 8–10 reps (rest 90 sec)
  *Different rep range than Day 4 — this builds muscle and work capacity.*
- Cable Flye: 3 sets × 12–15 reps (rest 60 sec)
  *Chest focus. Last set, push close to failure.*
- Reverse Pec Deck or Rear Delt Flye: 3 sets × 12–15 reps (rest 60 sec)
  *Rear delts and upper back. Higher reps, lighter weight.*

**Core Work:**
- Hanging Knee Raise (or cable crunch): 3 sets × 12–15 reps (rest 60 sec)
- Side Plank: 3 sets × 30–40 sec each side (rest 60 sec)

Post-workout: Light walk, stretch chest, back, shoulders — 45 sec each.

**Daily Step Target:** 8,000–10,000 steps (training day).

**Daily Step Target:** 5,000–7,000 steps (rest days — just normal living).

**Sleep & Recovery for Better Results:**

You're already hitting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Here's how to optimize it for fat loss and training:

1. **Target 7.5–9 hours** — aim for 8 as your sweet spot. More sleep = better recovery, better hormones, easier fat loss.

2. **Timing:** Your evening gym sessions mean you'll likely finish around 8–8:30 PM. Give yourself 2–3 hours before bed to wind down. So if you're in bed by 11 PM, that's plenty of buffer.

3. **Chai cutoff:** I know chai is part of your routine (and we're not cutting it out), but no chai after 6 PM. Your 6:30 PM snack is perfect — enjoy it then, but skip the evening chai. Caffeine stays in your system 5–6 hours and will mess with sleep quality.

4. **Screens:** Put your phone down 30–45 min before bed. Blue light keeps your brain alert. Use this time to read, stretch, or just wind down.

5. **Meal timing:** Your 9 PM dinner is late. Try to finish eating by 9:30 PM at the latest — a full stomach right before bed disrupts sleep. If you're hungry later, a small glass of warm milk or a few almonds is fine.

6. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 18–20°C if possible) and dark. Even a small fan helps.

7. **Consistency:** Sleep and wake at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body loves routine. This is huge for fat loss because it stabilizes your metabolism.

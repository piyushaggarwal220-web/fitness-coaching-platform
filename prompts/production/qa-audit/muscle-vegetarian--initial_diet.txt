Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Ananya,

Love that you're committed to hitting 62 kg in 12 weeks — that's a solid goal and totally doable with consistency. Since you're training 4 days a week in the evenings and want to build muscle, I've built this plan around your current eating times (breakfast at 8:30, lunch at 1:30, evening snack at 6:30, dinner at 9) and leaned hard into paneer, soya, and dal since those are your go-tos. The protein's built in to support your training without needing anything fancy. Let's dial in consistency this week.

---

**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am)**
Have 1.5 cups poha (approx 120g dry) made with 1 tbsp oil, 1 small onion, 1 tbsp roasted peanuts, and a squeeze of lemon. Pair with 1 glass (200ml) Greek yogurt on the side.
(P: 16g | C: 52g | F: 14g | ~420 kcal)

**Mid-morning (10:30 am)**
1 banana (approx 100g) + 10-12 almonds (approx 15g).
(P: 5g | C: 22g | F: 9g | ~160 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm)**
1 katori cooked rice (approx 150g), 1 katori dal (approx 150g cooked), small bowl mixed sabzi (approx 100g — carrot, peas, beans), 1 tbsp ghee mixed in.
(P: 18g | C: 68g | F: 11g | ~460 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm)**
1 cup tea with 1 tbsp roasted chana (approx 20g), 2 small chikki pieces (approx 25g).
(P: 6g | C: 18g | F: 8g | ~150 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm)**
2 rotis (approx 100g), 150g paneer bhurji (paneer scrambled with onion, tomato, ginger-garlic, 1 tsp oil), small bowl salad (cucumber, tomato, lettuce).
(P: 28g | C: 38g | F: 16g | ~420 kcal)

**Quick Tips for the Week:**
- Drink your 2L of water spread through the day — it'll help with digestion and keep hunger steady.
- On training days (Wed, Fri, Sun), the pre- and post-workout snacks are designed to fuel your squats and rows without feeling heavy. Stick to the timing.
- Ghee is your friend — use it generously in dals and rice; it adds calories and taste without breaking the budget.
- Roast your chana in batches on Sunday so it's ready for the week. Keeps for 4-5 days in a jar.
- If paneer feels repetitive by mid-week, swap it for soya chunks curry (cheaper, same protein punch).
- Salt and spices are free — don't hold back on ginger-garlic, turmeric, and chilli powder. They keep meals exciting.

You've got this week. Stick to the timings, hit your gym sessions, and let's see you move closer to that 62 kg goal. Consistency is the game — you already know that.

Eat well. Train hard. 💪

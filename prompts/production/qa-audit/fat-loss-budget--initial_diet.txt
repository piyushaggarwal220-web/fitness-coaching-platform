Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Ravi,

Great to have you on board! Your goal is solid — dropping from 76kg to 62kg in 12 weeks is totally doable with the right plan, and I've built this around your actual routine: your 8:30am breakfast, 1:30pm lunch, 6:30pm tea, and 9pm dinner. **MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30am)**
Have a bowl of poha (approx 150g cooked, about 1.5 cups) mixed with 2 tbsp roasted peanuts (crushed into it) and a pinch of turmeric and salt. Squeeze a bit of lemon on top if you like. On the side, drink a small glass of milk (150ml) or skip it if you're not a milk person.
(P: 12g | C: 48g | F: 10g | ~320 kcal)

**Mid-morning (if hungry)**
A small handful of roasted chana (approx 30g) with your regular tea or water.
(P: 4g | C: 8g | F: 1g | ~55 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30pm)**
Have 1 katori cooked rice (approx 150g), 1 katori dal (approx 150g cooked — mix moong or masoor, doesn't matter), and a small bowl of seasonal sabzi cooked in minimal oil (approx 100g). Finish with a small pinch of ghee on the rice if you want.
(P: 16g | C: 62g | F: 6g | ~380 kcal)

**Evening Tea (6:30pm)**
Your usual tea with roasted chana — stick with about 40g chana, no sugar or very little. This is your snack, so don't add biscuits or extra stuff.
(P: 6g | C: 12g | F: 2g | ~80 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00pm)**
Have 2 rotis (approx 60g atta), 100g paneer (either as paneer bhurji scrambled with onions and tomato, or cubed in a light curry), and a small bowl of fresh salad — lettuce, cucumber, tomato, carrot (approx 150g total). Drizzle a tiny bit of lemon and salt on the salad.
(P: 22g | C: 38g | F: 14g | ~380 kcal)

**Total for Monday: ~1215 kcal | P: 60g | C: 168g | F: 33g**

**Quick Notes:**

— **Sabzi rotation:** Mix it up — spinach, fenugreek (methi), bottle gourd (lauki), lady finger (bhindi), or whatever's in season. Cook with minimal oil (1 tsp per serving), salt, and maybe a pinch of turmeric or chili powder.

— **Dal:** Stick with moong or masoor — they're cheap, cook fast, and digest well. No need to add cream or extra ghee; just salt and a pinch of turmeric.

— **Roasted chana:** Buy in bulk from a local vendor, roast it yourself at home if it's cheaper, and keep it in a jar. It's your best friend for protein on a budget.

— **Paneer:** Buy it fresh from the market, not packaged. It's usually cheaper and fresher. Cube it, crumble it for bhurji, or even just eat it plain with salt and lemon if you're in a rush.

— **Water:** Aim for 2–2.5L a day, especially on gym days. Drink a glass before your evening workout.

— **Oil:** Use minimal — just enough to cook sabzi and for the tiny bit on rotis. Ghee is fine in small amounts (a pinch on rice or rotis), but don't overdo it.

— **Gym days:** You're training 4 days a week in the evenings. On those days, try to eat lunch a bit earlier (around 1pm if you can) so you're not too full when you train at 6–7pm. Your tea and chana at 6:30pm is perfect — light, quick energy.

— **Hunger:** If you're genuinely hungry between meals, add a small handful of sprouts, a glass of water, or a bit more chana. Don't ignore real hunger, just keep additions light and protein-forward.

You've got a solid routine, a realistic budget, and foods you actually enjoy. This plan fits right into your life. Stick with it, train hard, and check in next week — we'll see how you're feeling and adjust if needed.

Let's do this, Ravi. 💪

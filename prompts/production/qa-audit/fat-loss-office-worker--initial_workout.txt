## Warmup Routine (Do this before every training session — around 10 minutes)

Start with 5 minutes of light cardio: treadmill walk at a brisk pace, or the stationary bike if your gym has one. Then move into some joint mobility — arm circles forward and back, leg swings front-to-back and side-to-side, hip circles, and a few cat-cow stretches if you've got floor space. Finish with a couple of sets of bodyweight squats (10–12 reps) and some band pull-aparts or just arm swings to get the shoulders warm. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — one set at about 40% of your working weight, another at 60–70%, just to groove the movement and wake up the muscles.

## Day 1 — Lower Body Strength

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps
  (Focus on depth — hit parallel or just below if you can do it with control. Keep your core braced the whole way.)
- Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets x 6 reps
  (Hinge at the hips, keep the bar close to your legs, and feel the stretch in your hamstrings. No rounding your lower back.)

**Accessory Work:**
- Leg Press: 3 sets x 10 reps
  (Since you've got access to one, use it. Feet shoulder-width, push through your heels.)
- Walking Lunges (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 12 reps per leg
  (Take your time, control the descent. These will light up your glutes.)
- Seated Calf Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Pause at the top for a second, squeeze.)

**Core Work:**
- Plank: 3 sets x 30–45 seconds
- Hanging Knee Raise (or Lying Leg Raise if no bar): 3 sets x 10–12 reps
- Russian Twists (Dumbbell or Plate): 3 sets x 20 reps total

**Daily Steps:** Aim for 6,000 steps today. You're training, so between your warm-up walk, moving around the gym, and a short post-dinner stroll, you should hit it without forcing anything.

## Day 2 — Upper Body Push

**Main Lifts:**
- Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 5 reps
  (Retract your shoulder blades, press with control. Don't bounce the bar off your chest.)
- Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets x 6 reps
  (Keep your core tight, press straight up. Don't arch your lower back to cheat the weight up.)

**Accessory Work:**
- Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 10 reps
  (Upper chest focus. Set the bench to about 30–40 degrees.)
- Cable Chest Fly: 3 sets x 12 reps
  (Slow and controlled. Feel the stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top.)
- Lateral Raise (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Light weight, strict form. Lead with your elbows, not your hands.)
- Overhead Tricep Extension (Cable or Dumbbell): 3 sets x 12 reps
- Tricep Pushdown (Cable): 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Keep your elbows pinned to your sides. Squeeze at the bottom.)

**Core Work:**
- Cable Woodchop: 3 sets x 12 reps per side
- Dead Bug: 3 sets x 10 reps per side
- Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets x 20 reps total

**Daily Steps:** Target 6,000 steps again. Training day, so keep it steady.

## Post-Workout Stretching Routine (Do this after every training session — 10 minutes)

Once you're done lifting, spend about 10 minutes stretching out the muscles you just worked. Hold each stretch for 30–60 seconds per side, breathe deeply, and don't bounce. Here's what to hit:

- Hamstring Stretch (seated or standing)
- Quad Stretch (standing, pull your heel to your glute)
- Hip Flexor Stretch (lunge position, push your hips forward)
- Glute Stretch (figure-four or pigeon pose)
- Chest Stretch (doorway or against a wall)
- Lat Stretch (reach one arm overhead and lean to the side)
- Shoulder Stretch (pull one arm across your body)
- Tricep Stretch (reach one arm overhead, pull your elbow with the other hand)

If you've got a foam roller, spend a few minutes on your quads, hamstrings, and upper back. It'll help with soreness and recovery.

## Training Intensity

Let's talk effort. On the big compound lifts — squats, deadlifts, RDLs, bench press, rows — you're going to leave a rep or two in the tank. That means if you could do 7 reps, you're stopping at 5. If you could grind out 8, you're stopping at 6. This keeps your form clean and your joints safe, especially since you're new to this.

On the accessory and isolation work (leg curls, lateral raises, curls, etc.), you can push closer to failure. The last couple of reps should feel hard, but you're still in control. On the very last set of isolation moves, if you hit strict failure and want to squeeze out one or two controlled cheat reps (a little body English, a bit of momentum), that's fine — but only on things like curls, lateral raises, or tricep work. Never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those need to stay strict.

## Sleep

You're getting 7–8 hours now, which is solid, but let's aim for the higher end — closer to 8 hours consistently. Given your evening training and late dinner habits, I'd say aim to be in bed by 11:30 pm and up by 7:30 am. If you're training in the evening and eating late (like that 10:30 pm biryani), try to finish eating by 10 pm on training days so you're not going to bed on a full stomach.

A few practical tips for better sleep:
- Cut off chai and coffee by 4 pm. Caffeine sticks around longer than you think, and it'll mess with your sleep quality even if you feel like you're falling asleep fine.
- Dim the lights in your room an hour before bed. If you've got bright overhead lights, switch to a lamp or softer lighting.
- Put your phone on silent and keep it across the room, not on your nightstand. If you're scrolling Instagram or checking work emails at 11 pm, you're not winding down.
- If you're stressed from work (you mentioned stress is high), spend 5–10 minutes doing some deep breathing before bed. In for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6. It helps.
- Keep your room cool if you can — a fan or AC set to a comfortable temperature makes a big difference.
- If you're eating late, keep it lighter on rest days. On training days, you need the food, but on rest days, try to finish dinner by 9 pm if possible.
- Consistency matters more than perfection. Same bedtime, same wake-up time, even on weekends. Your body will thank you.

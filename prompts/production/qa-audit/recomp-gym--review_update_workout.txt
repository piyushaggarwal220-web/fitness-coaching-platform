Thank you for your check-in. I can see the scale has been stuck for two weeks and training felt average. Your adherence during the week is solid, but weekends are looser — we'll address that on the nutrition side. For training, you've been on the current split for a month now, so it's time to progress and give your muscles a new stimulus.

This week we're moving to a 2-body-part-per-day split across your 4 training days. This keeps session length similar (~60 min) but increases training density and frequency per muscle group. Volume is up roughly 15% to match your month of consistent work. We're keeping squats and rows as staples since you enjoy them, and I've programmed compound movements first when you're fresh.

**Weekly Structure:**
- **Day 1: Chest + Back** (Monday or Tuesday evening)
- **Day 2: Legs + Shoulders** (Wednesday or Thursday evening)
- **Day 3: Chest + Back** (Friday or Saturday evening)
- **Day 4: Legs + Abs** (Saturday or Sunday evening)
- Rest days: 3 days spread through the week

**Warm-Up (every session):** 5 min brisk walk or bike, then 2 sets of 10 reps bodyweight squats, arm circles, and band pull-aparts.

**Day 1 — Chest + Back:**
1. Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 8 reps
2. Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 8 reps (your favorite)
3. Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 10 reps
4. Cable Seated Row: 3 sets x 12 reps
5. Cable Chest Fly: 3 sets x 12 reps
6. Lat Pulldown: 3 sets x 12 reps

**Day 2 — Legs + Shoulders:**
1. Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 8 reps (your favorite)
2. Leg Press: 3 sets x 12 reps
3. Romanian Deadlift (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 10 reps
4. Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 4 sets x 10 reps
5. Dumbbell Lateral Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps
6. Cable Face Pull: 3 sets x 15 reps

**Day 3 — Chest + Back:**
1. Dumbbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 10 reps
2. Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 8 reps
3. Dumbbell Incline Press: 3 sets x 12 reps
4. Cable Lat Pulldown (Wide Grip): 3 sets x 12 reps
5. Push-Ups: 3 sets x AMRAP (as many reps as possible)
6. Dumbbell Single-Arm Row: 3 sets x 12 reps each arm

**Day 4 — Legs + Abs:**
1. Barbell Front Squat or Goblet Squat: 4 sets x 10 reps
2. Leg Press: 3 sets x 15 reps
3. Lying Leg Curl: 3 sets x 12 reps
4. Walking Lunges (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 10 reps each leg
5. Plank: 3 sets x 45 seconds
6. Cable Crunch: 3 sets x 15 reps

**Cooldown (every session):** 5 min walk, then stretch quads, hamstrings, chest, lats, and shoulders for 1–2 min total.

**Cardio:**
- 3 sessions of 30 min brisk walking on non-training days or post-workout.
- Aim for 6000–7000 steps daily (up from 5000) to support recomposition without adding fatigue.

**Progression Notes:**
- Aim to add 1–2 kg to barbell lifts each week if all reps completed with good form.
- If you hit all reps easily on dumbbell exercises, increase weight by 1–2 kg next session.
- Track your performance in a notebook or app — this helps you see progress even when the scale is stubborn.

You've built a solid foundation this past month, Neha. This new split will challenge your muscles in a fresh way and keep things interesting. Let's stay consistent through the weekend too — that's where we'll unlock the next level of progress. You've got this!

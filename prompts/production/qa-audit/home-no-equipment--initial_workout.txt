**Training Intensity & Effort:**

Here's the philosophy: On your heavy compound lifts (squats and rows), you're aiming for 3–4 reps in reserve on each set. That means if a set of 5 squats *could* be a set of 8–9 at absolute maximum effort, you stop at 5. This keeps you safe, especially as a beginner, and prevents injury. On your accessory and isolation work (split squats, glute bridges, dips, etc.), push a bit harder — aim to finish each set with 1–2 reps left in the tank. On your last set of isolation exercises, you can push closer to failure and even do a couple of controlled "cheat reps" if you need to (like a slight leg swing on a glute bridge or a tiny bit of momentum on a dip), but *never* on your heavy compounds like squats, deadlifts, or lunges. Form is non-negotiable there. If you're struggling with form, drop the reps or simplify the exercise.

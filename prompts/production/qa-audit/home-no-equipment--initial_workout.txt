**Day 1 — Lower Power + Core (Monday or your preferred evening)**

This is your squat day. We're going deep on strength here, so focus on controlled reps and solid form.

- Bodyweight Squats: 4 sets x 6–8 reps (pause 2 seconds at the bottom; before you start, do 2 warm-up sets with lighter depth to dial in your movement)
- Bulgarian Split Squats (rear foot elevated on a chair or low bed): 3 sets x 8 reps per leg (these are brutal for glute activation and single-leg stability — lean forward slightly if you need balance help)
- Glute Bridges: 3 sets x 12 reps (squeeze hard at the top; this preps your posterior chain and keeps your lower back safe)
- Dead Bug (core): 3 sets x 10 reps per side (extend opposite arm and leg, controlled; great for anti-rotation strength)

**Day 2 — Upper Pull + Chest + Core (Wednesday or your preferred evening)**

Rows are your thing — let's build that back and balance out your pushing muscles.

- Inverted Rows (using a sturdy low table or bed edge; lie underneath and pull your chest to the surface): 4 sets x 5–8 reps (this is your compound row substitute; keep your body straight like a plank, and pull with your elbows)
- Push-Ups (on knees if needed to start; no shame in scaling): 3 sets x 8–12 reps (lower chest to just above the ground, controlled tempo; if full push-ups are tough, do them on your knees and build from there)
- Pike Push-Ups (hands on the floor, hips high, lower your head toward the ground): 3 sets x 8 reps (this hits your shoulders hard and builds pressing strength)
- Tricep Dips (using a chair or low bench, body in front): 3 sets x 10–12 reps (keep your chest upright, elbows close to your ribs)
- Bicycle Crunches (core): 3 sets x 12 reps per side (controlled, no neck pulling)
- Pallof Hold (stand sideways, hands clasped at chest, resist rotation): 2 sets x 20 seconds per side (anti-rotation is key for core stability)
- Hollow Body Hold (lie on your back, arms and legs extended, slight arch in your torso): 2 sets x 15–20 seconds (great for core endurance and posture)

Target steps: 5,500–6,000. This is an upper-body day, so you might be slightly less mobile — that's fine; a normal workday step count is solid.

This day is about building muscle and definition in your lower body. Higher reps, slightly less weight (your bodyweight is the "weight" here), and you'll feel the burn.

**Training Intensity & Form Cues**

Since you're new to structured training, here's how to gauge your effort:

- On compound lifts (squats, inverted rows, push-ups), aim to leave 1–2 reps in the tank at the end of each set. You should feel strong and controlled, not grinding out your last rep. This builds strength safely and keeps you injury-free.
- On isolation and accessory work (glute bridges, leg raises, dips, calf raises), you can push harder — aim for the last 1–2 reps to feel challenging. If you've got strict form left, you can squeeze out 1–2 more with a slight form bend on the final set, but never on compound lower-body moves (squats, lunges, deadlifts). Your knees and lower back will thank you.
- Rest 60–90 seconds between sets on strength work (squats, rows, push-ups), and 30–45 seconds on isolation exercises.
- Tempo matters: lower weights slowly (2–3 seconds), pause briefly at the hardest point, and drive back up explosively. This builds strength and muscle at the same time.

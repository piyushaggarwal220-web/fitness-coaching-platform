Here's how we'll structure your week: we're doing a Lower-Upper-Lower split, which means you hit your legs twice (where the big calorie burn happens) and upper body once, all within 45 minutes per session. Each workout starts with a quick 10-minute warmup, then straight into the main lifts. No fluff, just work that counts.

**WARMUP (Every Session — 10 minutes)**
Start with 2–3 minutes of brisk walking or light jogging on the spot to get your heart rate up. Then move through 5–6 minutes of dynamic mobility: arm circles (10 each direction), leg swings (10 forward/back each leg), bodyweight squats (10 slow reps focusing on depth), cat-cow stretches (8–10 reps), and a few arm circles with torso rotation. Finish with 2–3 minutes of light movement — walk around, shake out your limbs, get ready to lift.

---

**DAY 1 — Lower Power (Monday or your preferred evening)**

After your warmup, do 2 lighter warm-up sets of the first compound lift (squats) before moving into your main sets — something like bodyweight squats or very light band resistance just to prime the movement.

• Goblet Squats (or banded squats): 4 sets × 5 reps — *This is your strength anchor. Go heavy enough that the last rep feels tough but controlled. Keep your chest up, knees tracking over your toes, and pause for a half-second at the bottom. Leave one rep in the tank on the first three sets; the fourth set can be a grind.*

• Romanian Deadlifts (banded or bodyweight): 3 sets × 6 reps — *Hinge at the hips, keep your back neutral, feel the hamstring stretch. This pairs beautifully with squats and builds posterior chain strength for fat loss.*

• Split Squats (alternating legs, banded resistance): 3 sets × 8 reps per leg — *Step forward, sink down, drive through your front heel. Control the descent; don't let your knee cave inward.*

• Banded Leg Curls (lying or standing): 3 sets × 12 reps — *Squeeze hard at the top, 1-second pause, then lower under control. This is accessory work, so push closer to failure on the last set.*

• Core: Dead Bugs (3 sets × 10 reps per side) + Pallof Press holds (3 sets × 15 seconds each side) — *Dead bugs teach core stability; Pallof presses build anti-rotation strength. Both are band-friendly and perfect for home.*

**Daily Step Target:** 7,000 steps (training day — aim for a walk before or after your session, or break it up across the day).

**DAY 2 — Upper Push & Pull (Wednesday or your preferred evening)**

Warmup as above, then 2 lighter warm-up sets before your first compound lift.

• Banded Rows (chest-supported, bent-over, or inverted): 4 sets × 5 reps — *Your favorite, so let's make it count. Row with intent, squeeze your shoulder blades together, control the eccentric. This is a strength day, so heavy tension and low reps.*

• Push-ups (on knees or elevated on a bench if needed): 3 sets × 6–8 reps — *Full range of motion, chest to the mat. If full push-ups feel too hard right now, that's fine — elevate your hands on a chair or low table and build from there.*

• Banded Chest Press (standing or lying): 3 sets × 8 reps — *Anchor the band behind you, press forward and slightly upward. Control the stretch at the chest, drive through the press.*

• Single-Arm Banded Rows (alternating): 3 sets × 10 reps per side — *This unilateral work builds stability and catches any imbalances. Lighter than your main rows, but still controlled.*

• Banded Bicep Curls: 2 sets × 12 reps — *Accessory work — push to near failure, but keep the movement strict. No swinging.*

• Core: Plank Hold (3 sets × 30–45 seconds) + Banded Woodchops (3 sets × 10 reps per side) — *Planks build endurance; woodchops work rotation and obliques. Alternate sides smoothly.*

**Daily Step Target:** 6,500 steps (training day — lighter step day since upper body doesn't burn as many calories as lower body).

**DAY 3 — Lower Hypertrophy (Friday or your preferred evening)**

Warmup, then 2 lighter warm-up sets before your main compound.

• Squats (banded or bodyweight): 3 sets × 8 reps — *Same movement as Day 1, but higher reps and slightly lighter intensity. Focus on the pump and time under tension. Controlled descent, explosive drive up.*

• Banded Deadlifts or Trap-Bar Deadlifts (if you have the equipment): 3 sets × 5 reps — *If you don't have a trap bar, stick with RDLs or single-leg RDLs. Keep your back neutral, drive through your heels.*

• Bulgarian Split Squats (rear foot elevated on a chair or bench): 3 sets × 10 reps per leg — *Higher reps, more burn. This is a unilateral move, so each leg works independently. Control the descent, drive through the front heel.*

• Banded Leg Extensions (or step-ups): 3 sets × 12 reps — *Quad-focused accessory work. If using step-ups, alternate legs smoothly. Squeeze at the top.*

• Banded Glute Bridges: 3 sets × 15 reps — *Squeeze your glutes hard at the top, 1-second pause. This is about the mind-muscle connection, not heavy weight.*

• Core: Reverse Crunches (3 sets × 12 reps) + Side Planks (3 sets × 25–30 seconds per side) — *Reverse crunches target the lower abs; side planks build lateral stability and oblique strength.*

**Daily Step Target:** 8,000 steps (training day — lower body work, so get extra movement in to support the fat-loss goal).

**DAY 4 — Active Recovery (Saturday or Sunday, whichever you prefer)**

No lifting today. Instead, spend 20–30 minutes on light mobility and stretching. Walk for 10–15 minutes at an easy pace, then move through these stretches:

• Quad Stretch (each leg, 45 seconds): Stand on one leg, pull your heel toward your glute.
• Hamstring Stretch (each leg, 45 seconds): Sit or stand, hinge forward gently, feel the back of your leg.
• Hip Flexor Stretch (each side, 45 seconds): Low lunge position, sink your hips forward.
• Chest Opener (45 seconds): Clasp your hands behind your back, open your chest, gaze slightly upward.
• Cat-Cow Flow (10 reps): Slow, controlled spinal mobility.
• Child's Pose (60 seconds): Relax here, breathe deeply.

**Daily Step Target:** 5,000–6,000 steps (easy day, but keep moving).

**DAY 5 — Rest Day (Tuesday or whichever day you choose)**

Full rest. No structured movement. Aim for your normal daily activity (office work, household tasks) and let your body recover.

**Daily Step Target:** 3,000–4,000 steps (just your normal daily baseline).

**COOLDOWN (After Every Training Session — 5–7 minutes)**

Once you're done lifting, spend a few minutes bringing your heart rate down and stretching the muscles you just worked:

• Walk slowly for 1–2 minutes.
• Quad Stretch (30 seconds each leg).
• Hamstring Stretch (30 seconds each leg).
• Hip Flexor Stretch (30 seconds each side).
• Chest Stretch (30 seconds).
• Shoulder Rolls (10 each direction).
• Deep breathing (5 slow, full breaths).

**TRAINING INTENSITY — How Hard Should You Push?**

On your main compound lifts (squats, rows, deadlifts), aim to leave one solid rep in the tank on your first few sets. That last set can be harder, but still controlled — no wild form breakdown. On accessory and isolation work (leg curls, bicep curls, chest presses), push a bit closer to failure, especially on your final set. If you hit true muscular failure on an isolation exercise, you can do 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" after that (a tiny bit of momentum or body English), but *never* on your main compound lifts like squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges. Those need to stay strict. The goal is progress and safety, not ego.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Let's aim for 7.5–9 hours most nights to support fat loss and recovery. Here's what I'd suggest:

• **Timing:** Try to hit bed by 11:00 PM on training nights so you wake refreshed for your day. On rest days, you can be more flexible.
• **Chai Cutoff:** No chai or coffee after 3:00 PM. I know chai is part of your routine, but the caffeine will mess with your sleep, and sleep is where the fat loss magic happens.
• **Screen Time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. The blue light messes with melatonin production.
• **Meal Timing:** Your dinner at 9:00 PM is a bit late — try to eat by 8:30 PM if you can, so digestion isn't keeping you awake. If you're hungry later, a small glass of warm milk or a handful of almonds is fine.
• **Room Temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 18–20°C if possible) and dark. A fan helps.
• **Wind-Down Routine:** Spend 10 minutes stretching or doing some light breathing exercises before bed. This signals to your body that it's time to rest.
• **Hydration:** Drink most of your water earlier in the day; taper off by 8:00 PM so you're not waking up for bathroom trips.

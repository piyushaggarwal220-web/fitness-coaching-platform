- Band pull-aparts: 15 reps

Once you're warm, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets on your first main compound lift before you go into the working weight.

**SESSION 1 — Lower Body Power (Monday or whenever your first evening slot is)**

This is where we build leg strength and muscle. Squats are your best friend here.

- Resistance band squats: 4 sets x 6-8 reps (heavy tension, controlled descent — take 2 seconds down, pause for 1 second at the bottom, drive up hard. Leave 1-2 reps in reserve on each set.)
- Resistance band Romanian deadlifts: 3 sets x 8-10 reps (hinge at the hips, keep your back neutral, feel the hamstring stretch at the bottom)
- Single-leg band glute bridges: 3 sets x 10 reps per leg (squeeze hard at the top, this fires up your glutes and stabilizes your hips)
- Dead bug: 3 sets x 12 reps per side (slow and controlled, press your lower back into the mat)

**Daily step target:** 7,000 steps (you'll naturally get some from moving around, add a short walk after dinner if needed)

---

**SESSION 2 — Upper Body Pull & Core (Wednesday or your second evening slot)**

Rows are your star here. We're building back strength and posture.

- Resistance band rows (standing or half-kneeling): 4 sets x 6-8 reps (pull the band to your ribcage, squeeze your shoulder blades together hard, control the return — don't let it snap back)
- Resistance band face pulls: 3 sets x 12-15 reps (great for shoulder health and rear delts, pull towards your face and flare your elbows out)
- Resistance band bicep curls: 3 sets x 10-12 reps (strict form, no swinging — if the last 2 reps need a tiny bit of momentum after true failure, that's okay, but keep it minimal)
- Pallof press (band anchored at chest height, step perpendicular to anchor): 3 sets x 10 reps per side (anti-rotation core work — resist the band trying to twist you, this is gold for stability)

**Daily step target:** 7,500 steps (training day, so a bit more movement)

---

**SESSION 3 — Full Body Strength (Friday or your third evening slot)**

Balanced session hitting everything with a bit less volume but high quality reps.

- Resistance band squats: 3 sets x 8-10 reps (same cue as Session 1, but slightly higher reps this time)
- Resistance band rows: 3 sets x 8-10 reps (match the quality of Session 2)
- Resistance band lateral raises: 3 sets x 12-15 reps (light band, focus on the shoulder — no momentum, pure shoulder work)
- Resistance band tricep extensions: 3 sets x 10-12 reps (press the band overhead or behind your head, squeeze the triceps hard)
- Bird dog: 3 sets x 10 reps per side (opposite arm and leg, hold for 1 second, great for lower back stability and core endurance)

**TRAINING INTENSITY & FORM NOTES**

Here's the philosophy: on your compound lifts (squats, rows, deadlifts), you want to leave 1-2 reps in the tank — that means you could do 1-2 more reps with good form, but you stop. This keeps your joints safe and lets you recover well for your next session. On isolation work like curls, raises, and tricep extensions, you can push closer to true failure — if the last 1-2 reps need a tiny bit of help (controlled momentum, not wild swinging), that's fine. But never do this on lower body compounds like squats or RDLs; those always stay strict. If form breaks down, you're done with that set.

With resistance bands, tension matters more than speed. Move deliberately — 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up. This builds muscle and keeps you safe.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (5 minutes after every session)**

Do this right after your workout while you're still warm:

- Hamstring stretch (standing forward fold or seated): 60 seconds
- Chest stretch (doorway or band): 45 seconds each side
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 45 seconds each arm
- Child's pose: 60 seconds
- Spinal twist (lying): 45 seconds each side

Hold each stretch steady — no bouncing. Breathe deeply and let your muscles relax.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already nailing 7-8 hours, which is fantastic. Here's how to keep that locked in and maybe push towards 7.5-9 hours on training days:

- **Chai cutoff:** Stop chai/tea by 5 PM. I know it's part of your routine, but caffeine after 5 PM will mess with your sleep. Herbal tea or warm milk after that is your friend.
- **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. I get it — Instagram is tempting — but your sleep quality will jump.
- **Meal timing:** Your 9 PM dinner is a bit late. Try to finish eating by 8:30 PM if you can, so your digestion isn't still running when you're trying to sleep. If you're hungry before bed, a small glass of warm milk with a pinch of turmeric is perfect.
- **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 18-20°C if possible) and dark. A fan helps.
- **Consistent bedtime:** Aim to be in bed by 10:30-11 PM on training nights. Your body loves rhythm.
- **Morning light:** Get some sunlight in the first hour after waking — even 10 minutes helps regulate your sleep cycle.
- **Hydration:** You're drinking 2L a day, which is solid. Just taper it down after 7 PM so you're not waking up to use the bathroom.

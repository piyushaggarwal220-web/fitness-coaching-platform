**Warmup Routine (10 minutes before every training session)**

Start with 5 minutes of light cardio — treadmill walk at an incline, elliptical, or stationary bike, whatever feels good and gets your heart rate up gently. Then move into some joint mobility: arm circles forward and back (10 each way), hip circles (10 each direction), leg swings front-to-back and side-to-side (10 each leg), and some bodyweight squats (15-20 reps, slow and controlled). Finish with a few cat-cow stretches on the floor if your gym has space, or some standing torso twists. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — one set at about 50% of your working weight, then one at 70%, just to prime the movement pattern.

**Day 1 — Lower Power**

This is your heavy lower day. We're prioritizing strength on the big lifts, so keep the reps low and the weight challenging. Aim for around 8,000-9,000 steps today since it's a training day — a short walk after your session or during lunch helps with recovery and keeps your NEAT up.

1. **Barbell Back Squat** — 4 sets x 5 reps  
   Your favorite, so let's make it count. Focus on depth and control. Brace hard at the top, break at the hips and knees together, and drive through your midfoot. Leave 1-2 reps in the tank on each set.

2. **Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)** — 3 sets x 6 reps  
   Hinge from the hips, keep the bar close to your shins, and feel the stretch in your hamstrings. Don't rush the eccentric — that's where the magic happens.

3. **Leg Press** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
   Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Push through your heels and don't lock out completely at the top. Control the descent.

4. **Walking Lunges (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 8 reps per leg  
   Keep your torso upright, step long enough that your front knee stays over your ankle. These are great for glute activation.

5. **Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Squeeze at the top, slow on the way down. Hamstrings respond well to controlled tempo.

6. **Hanging Knee Raises** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Keep your lower back neutral, don't swing. If these are too tough, do them with bent knees or switch to lying leg raises.

7. **Cable Pallof Press** — 3 sets x 10 reps per side  
   Anti-rotation core work. Stand perpendicular to the cable, press out and resist the pull. Great for building core stability.

8. **Plank Hold** — 3 sets x 45 seconds  
   Squeeze your glutes, brace your abs, don't let your hips sag. Quality over time here.

**Day 2 — Upper Hypertrophy**

Today we're chasing volume and muscle fatigue in the upper body. Higher reps, shorter rest, and really focus on the contraction. Another training day, so aim for 8,000-9,000 steps.

1. **Barbell Bench Press** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
   Retract your shoulder blades, lower the bar to mid-chest, and press through your whole hand. Leave a rep or two in reserve.

2. **Barbell Bent-Over Row** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
   You love rows, so let's do them right. Hinge at the hips, keep your torso at about 45 degrees, pull the bar to your lower ribcage. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top.

3. **Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
   Press straight up, don't arch your lower back excessively. Control the descent.

4. **Lat Pulldown (Cable)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Wide grip, pull to your upper chest, lean back slightly. Think about pulling your elbows down and back, not just yanking with your hands.

5. **Dumbbell Incline Chest Fly** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Bench at 30-45 degrees. Slight bend in the elbows, stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top. Don't go too heavy here — form over ego.

6. **Cable Face Pull** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
   Pull the rope toward your face, elbows high. Great for rear delts and upper back health.

7. **Dumbbell Bicep Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Strict form, no swinging. You can allow a couple of controlled cheat reps on the last set after you hit strict failure, but only if you can keep tension on the muscle.

8. **Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
   Keep your elbows pinned to your sides, extend fully at the bottom, and squeeze the triceps.

9. **Russian Twists (Dumbbell or Plate)** — 3 sets x 20 reps (10 per side)  
   Sit with your feet elevated slightly, lean back a bit, and twist from your torso. Control the movement.

10. **Cable Crunch (Kneeling)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
    Pull with your abs, not your arms. Think about bringing your ribcage toward your pelvis.

11. **Side Plank Hold** — 3 sets x 30 seconds per side  
    Stack your feet, keep your hips up, and don't let your torso rotate forward or back.

**Day 3 — Active Recovery**

No lifting today, but we're not totally off. This is about keeping blood flowing, working on mobility, and letting your muscles recover without going completely sedentary. Aim for around 6,000-7,000 steps today — a couple of short walks, maybe one in the morning and one in the evening.

Spend 20-30 minutes doing light movement:

- 10 minutes of easy walking (treadmill incline walk or outdoor stroll)  
- 5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching: leg swings, arm circles, hip openers, cat-cow, child's pose, spinal twists  
- 5-10 minutes of foam rolling or self-massage if you have access to a roller — focus on quads, hamstrings, glutes, upper back

You can also do some light yoga or stretching at home in the evening if you feel like it. The goal is to move gently and feel better, not to add training stress.

**Day 4 — Lower Hypertrophy**

Second lower day of the week, but this time we're working in higher rep ranges and really focusing on time under tension. Training day, so back up to 8,000-9,000 steps.

1. **Barbell Front Squat (or Goblet Squat if front rack position is uncomfortable)** — 4 sets x 10 reps  
   More quad-dominant than back squats. Keep your torso upright, elbows high if you're doing front squats. Control the descent and drive up explosively.

2. **Bulgarian Split Squat (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 10 reps per leg  
   Rear foot elevated on a bench. These are brutal but amazing for glute and quad development. Don't rush them.

3. **Leg Extension (Machine)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
   Squeeze hard at the top, slow on the way down. You can push these closer to failure — even add a couple of controlled partials at the end of your last set if you want.

4. **Lying Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
   Same deal as the seated version earlier in the week. Control and squeeze.

5. **Cable Glute Kickback** — 3 sets x 12 reps per leg  
   Attach an ankle strap, hinge slightly forward, and kick back with control. Squeeze your glute at the top.

6. **Standing Calf Raise (Machine or Dumbbell)** — 4 sets x 15 reps  
   Full range of motion — stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top. Calves respond well to higher volume.

7. **Bicycle Crunches** — 3 sets x 20 reps (10 per side)  
   Slow and controlled, bring your elbow toward the opposite knee, extend the other leg fully.

8. **Dead Bug** — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  
   Lie on your back, press your lower back into the floor, and move opposite arm and leg. Great for core stability.

9. **Plank with Shoulder Taps** — 3 sets x 20 taps (10 per side)  
   Hold a plank position and tap each shoulder with the opposite hand. Keep your hips stable — don't rock side to side.

**Day 5 — Upper Power**

Back to lower reps and heavier loads for the upper body. We're building strength here, so focus on quality reps and good bar speed. Training day — 8,000-9,000 steps.

1. **Barbell Overhead Press (Standing)** — 4 sets x 5 reps  
   Brace your core hard, press straight up, don't lean back excessively. This is a full-body lift when done right.

2. **Barbell Bent-Over Row (Underhand Grip)** — 4 sets x 6 reps  
   Switching to underhand grip hits your lats and biceps a bit differently than the overhand version earlier in the week. Same hinge position, pull to your lower ribcage.

3. **Dumbbell Bench Press (Flat)** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
   Dumbbells allow for a deeper stretch and a more natural pressing path. Control the descent and press through your whole hand.

4. **Pull-Up or Lat Pulldown (if pull-ups aren't there yet)** — 3 sets x 6-8 reps  
   If you can do pull-ups, great — aim for 6-8 solid reps. If not, do heavy lat pulldowns and work toward pull-ups over the next few weeks.

5. **Dumbbell Lateral Raise** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Slight bend in the elbows, raise to shoulder height, control the descent. Don't swing or use momentum.

6. **Cable Chest Fly (Low to High)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Set the cables low, step forward, and bring your hands together in front of your chest. Great for upper chest.

7. **Dumbbell Hammer Curl** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
   Neutral grip, elbows pinned. You can allow a couple of controlled cheat reps on the last set after strict failure.

8. **Overhead Dumbbell Tricep Extension** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
   Keep your elbows close to your head, lower the dumbbell behind your head, and extend fully.

9. **Hanging Leg Raise (Straight Legs if possible, bent if not)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
   Control the swing, lift with your abs, lower slowly.

10. **Cable Woodchop** — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  
    High to low or low to high, your choice. Rotate from your torso, not just your arms.

11. **Mountain Climbers** — 3 sets x 20 reps (10 per side)  
    Plank position, drive your knees toward your chest alternately. Keep your core tight and your hips level.

**Post-Workout Stretching Routine (5-10 minutes after every training session)**

After your workout, spend a few minutes stretching the muscles you just trained. Hold each stretch for 30-60 seconds per side, breathe deeply, and don't bounce. Here's a simple routine you can follow:

- **Lower body days:** Standing quad stretch, seated hamstring stretch, pigeon pose or figure-four glute stretch, standing calf stretch, hip flexor stretch (lunge position)
- **Upper body days:** Doorway chest stretch, overhead tricep stretch, cross-body shoulder stretch, seated spinal twist, child's pose for lats and shoulders

This isn't optional — stretching helps with recovery, reduces soreness, and keeps your joints healthy over time.

**Sleep and Recovery**

You're already doing well with 7-8 hours, which is solid. Let's aim to keep that consistent and maybe push toward 7.5-8 hours most nights, especially on training days. Given your evening training schedule and 9 pm dinner, try to be in bed by 11-11:30 pm and asleep by midnight at the latest. Here are a few practical tips that work well with your routine:

- Cut off chai and coffee by 4-5 pm — caffeine stays in your system longer than you think, and late chai can mess with your sleep quality even if you feel like you're falling asleep fine.  
- Try to finish dinner by 9 pm and give yourself at least 2 hours before bed so you're not lying down on a full stomach.  
- Dim the lights in your room an hour before bed and switch your phone to night mode or, even better, keep it out of arm's reach. Scrolling Instagram or WhatsApp right before sleep kills your sleep quality.  
- If you're wired after your evening workout, a short 10-minute walk outside or some light stretching before bed can help you wind down.  
- Keep your room cool and dark — even a small amount of light can disrupt your sleep cycles.  
- If you wake up in the middle of the night, don't check your phone or the time. Just close your eyes, focus on your breathing, and let yourself drift back.  
- Try to wake up and go to bed at roughly the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body loves routine.

**Training Intensity Guidance**

Let's talk about how hard to push. On your big compound lifts — squats, deadlifts, RDLs, presses, rows — you should be leaving 1-2 reps in the tank on every set. That means if you think you could do 7 reps, stop at 5. If you could grind out 10, stop at 8. We're building strength and technique here, not testing your limits every session. On isolation and accessory work — leg curls, bicep curls, lateral raises, cable work — you can push closer to failure, maybe leaving just 1 rep in reserve or even hitting true failure on the last set. And here's the deal with cheat reps: you're allowed a couple of controlled cheat reps on the last set of isolation moves after you've hit strict failure — like on bicep curls or lateral raises — but only if you can still keep tension on the muscle and you're not turning the exercise into a full-body swing. Never, ever use cheat reps or loose form on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound movement. Those require perfect form every single rep.

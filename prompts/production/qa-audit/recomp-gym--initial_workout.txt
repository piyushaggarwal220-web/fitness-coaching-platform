Before we jump into the week, let's talk warmup. Every training day, start with about 10 minutes to get your body ready — hop on the treadmill or bike for 5 minutes at an easy pace, then run through some joint circles (ankles, hips, shoulders), a few bodyweight squats, some arm circles, and maybe a couple of cat-cows or hip openers if you're feeling tight. Nothing fancy, just get the blood moving. Then, before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — say, one set with just the bar, then one around 50-60% of your working weight. This primes the movement pattern and keeps you safe.

A quick word on intensity: on your big compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, presses, rows), leave 1-2 reps in the tank — you should finish each set knowing you could've done one or two more if someone held a gun to your head, but you're stopping there on purpose. On isolation and accessory work, you can push closer to failure, especially on the last set. If you hit true failure on an isolation move and want to squeeze out 1-2 controlled cheat reps to finish the set, that's fine — but never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those stay strict. Control the weight, don't let it control you.

**Day 1 — Lower Body Power**

This is your heavy lower day. We're starting with your favorite — squats — and building strength in that 3-6 rep range, then adding volume with accessory work.

- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets x 5 reps
  (Your bread and butter. Focus on depth and bracing hard through your core. If you feel your knees caving in, widen your stance a touch or point your toes out slightly.)

- Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets x 6 reps
  (Hamstrings and glutes. Keep a slight bend in the knees, push your hips back, and feel the stretch down the back of your legs. Don't round your lower back.)

- Leg Press: 3 sets x 10 reps
  (Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Push through your heels. This one you can load up a bit since the machine stabilizes you.)

- Walking Lunges (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 12 reps per leg
  (Grab a pair of dumbbells, take controlled steps, and don't let your front knee collapse inward. These burn, but they're gold for glutes and quads.)

- Seated Calf Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Slow and controlled. Pause at the top, squeeze, then lower all the way down for a stretch.)

**Core:**
- Plank: 3 sets x 45 seconds
- Russian Twists (Dumbbell or Plate): 3 sets x 20 reps total
- Lying Leg Raises: 3 sets x 12 reps

Aim for around 7,000-8,000 steps today — you're training in the evening, so try to get a walk in during lunch or after dinner if you can.

**Day 2 — Upper Body Push/Pull**

Today we're hitting the entire upper body with a mix of horizontal and vertical pushing and pulling. Rows are in here as requested.

- Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 6 reps
  (Chest, shoulders, triceps. Keep your shoulder blades squeezed together, lower the bar to mid-chest, and press up strong. Don't bounce it off your chest.)

- Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets x 6 reps
  (Your favorite. Hinge at the hips, keep your back flat, and pull the bar to your lower ribcage. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top.)

- Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated): 3 sets x 10 reps
  (Press straight up, don't arch your lower back excessively. Control the descent.)

- Lat Pulldown (Cable): 3 sets x 12 reps
  (Wide grip, pull to your upper chest, lean back slightly. Think about pulling your elbows down and back, not just yanking with your hands.)

- Cable Chest Fly: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Slight bend in the elbows, bring the handles together in front of your chest, squeeze, then control the stretch on the way back.)

- Dumbbell Bicep Curl: 3 sets x 12 reps
  (Strict form. No swinging. If you need to cheat the last couple reps on the final set after you hit failure, fine, but make it controlled.)

- Overhead Tricep Extension (Cable or Dumbbell): 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Keep your elbows close to your head, extend fully, squeeze at the bottom.)

**Core:**
- Hanging Knee Raises (or Lying if no bar): 3 sets x 10 reps
- Cable Woodchops: 3 sets x 12 reps per side
- Dead Bug: 3 sets x 10 reps per side

Step target: 7,000-8,000 steps. Training day, so keep moving when you can.

**Day 3 — Active Recovery**

No lifting today. This is your chance to let your muscles recover while staying active. Go for a 20-30 minute walk at a comfortable pace — maybe around the neighborhood or a park if you have one nearby. Follow it up with 15-20 minutes of light stretching and mobility work. Hit your quads, hamstrings, hip flexors, chest, shoulders, and lats. Move slowly, breathe deep, and don't force anything. Foam rolling is great if you have access to one.

Step target: 8,000-10,000 steps. You're not lifting, so this is a good day to get your steps up a bit.

**Day 4 — Lower Body Hypertrophy**

Second lower day of the week, but we're shifting the focus to higher reps and more volume. You'll still feel this one, but it's less about grinding heavy weight and more about time under tension and muscle fatigue.

- Goblet Squat (Dumbbell): 4 sets x 12 reps
  (Hold a dumbbell at your chest, squat deep, and keep your torso upright. Great for quad activation.)

- Leg Press: 4 sets x 15 reps
  (We're back here, but with higher reps. Control the descent, don't slam the weight down.)

- Bulgarian Split Squat (Dumbbell): 3 sets x 10 reps per leg
  (Rear foot elevated on a bench. These are brutal but amazing for glutes and quads. Keep your front knee tracking over your toes.)

- Lying Leg Curl (Machine): 3 sets x 12 reps
  (Hamstrings. Squeeze at the top, don't let momentum do the work.)

- Cable Glute Kickback: 3 sets x 15 reps per leg
  (Attach an ankle strap, lean slightly forward, and kick straight back. Focus on squeezing your glute at the top.)

- Standing Calf Raise (Machine or Dumbbell): 3 sets x 20 reps
  (High reps, full range of motion. Calves respond well to volume.)

**Core:**
- Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets x 20 reps total
- Plank with Shoulder Taps: 3 sets x 10 taps per side
- Reverse Crunches: 3 sets x 15 reps

Step target: 7,000-8,000 steps.

**Day 5 — Upper Body Hypertrophy**

Last training day of the week. We're hitting upper body again, but with higher reps and a slightly different exercise selection to hit muscles from new angles.

- Dumbbell Incline Bench Press: 4 sets x 10 reps
  (Upper chest focus. Set the bench to about 30-45 degrees, press up and slightly together at the top.)

- Cable Seated Row: 4 sets x 12 reps
  (Rows again, but on the cable this time. Sit tall, pull to your lower ribcage, and squeeze your shoulder blades together.)

- Dumbbell Lateral Raise: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Shoulders. Slight bend in the elbows, raise to shoulder height, control the descent. Don't swing.)

- Cable Face Pull: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Rear delts and upper back. Pull the rope toward your face, elbows high, and squeeze your shoulder blades together.)

- Dumbbell Hammer Curl: 3 sets x 12 reps
  (Biceps, but with a neutral grip. Keeps tension on the brachialis and forearms too.)

- Cable Tricep Pushdown: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Keep your elbows pinned to your sides, extend fully, squeeze at the bottom.)

- Dumbbell Shrug: 3 sets x 15 reps
  (Traps. Lift your shoulders straight up, hold for a second, then lower slowly.)

**Core:**
- Mountain Climbers: 3 sets x 20 reps total
- Side Plank: 3 sets x 30 seconds per side
- Hollow Body Hold: 3 sets x 20 seconds

Step target: 7,000-8,000 steps.

**Day 6 — Full Rest Day**

No training, no structured activity. Just rest. Your body builds muscle when you recover, not when you're in the gym, so take this seriously. If you feel like moving, a gentle 10-15 minute walk is fine, but don't push it. Read, relax, meal prep for the week ahead, catch up on sleep if you need it.

Step target: 5,000-6,000 steps. It's okay to move less today.

**Day 7 — Stretching and Mobility**

End the week with a full-body stretching session. Spend 20-30 minutes working through all the major muscle groups you trained this week. Here's a simple flow:

- Quad stretch (standing or lying): 60 seconds per side
- Hamstring stretch (seated or standing): 60 seconds per side
- Hip flexor stretch (kneeling lunge): 60 seconds per side
- Glute stretch (figure-4 or pigeon pose): 60 seconds per side
- Chest stretch (doorway or against a wall): 60 seconds
- Lat stretch (overhead or hanging): 60 seconds per side
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 30 seconds per side
- Tricep stretch (overhead): 30 seconds per side
- Cat-cow (spinal mobility): 10-12 reps
- Child's pose: 60 seconds

Breathe deep, don't bounce, and ease into each stretch. This isn't about pain — if you feel a strong pull, that's enough.

Step target: 8,000-10,000 steps. Another light day, good opportunity to get some extra movement in.

**Sleep Guidance**

You're getting 7-8 hours now, which is solid, but let's aim for the higher end — 7.5-9 hours — especially during these 12 weeks. Your body repairs and builds muscle while you sleep, and sleep also regulates hunger hormones, which matters a lot for recomposition. Since you're training in the evenings, try to be in bed by 11 pm and up by 7-7:30 am. Here are a few things that'll help:

- Cut off chai and coffee by 4 pm. Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours, so even that evening cup can mess with your sleep quality.
- Finish dinner by 9 pm (you're already doing this, great). Give yourself at least 2 hours before bed so you're not lying down on a full stomach.
- Dim the lights in your home after 8:30 pm if you can. Bright lights signal your brain to stay awake.
- Put your phone on silent or in another room by 10:30 pm. If you need it for an alarm, fine, but no scrolling in bed. The blue light and mental stimulation will keep you wired.
- Keep your room cool and dark. If you don't have blackout curtains, even an eye mask helps.
- If your mind races at night, try writing down your to-do list for the next day before bed. Gets it out of your head.
- A warm shower or a few minutes of deep breathing before bed can help you wind down, especially after a training day.

Consistency is key here. Your body loves routine, so try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Sanjay,

Here's your first week sorted — built around your travel schedule and the foods you already enjoy. We're aiming for recomp here, so the focus is on hitting solid protein at every meal while keeping energy steady for your evening training sessions. I've kept things simple and flexible so you can stick to it whether you're home or in a hotel. Let's get after it.

---

**DAY 1**

**Breakfast (8:30 am)**
Go with 2 medium idlis (approx 100g total) with 1 bowl sambhar (150ml) and 1 tablespoon coconut chutney, or swap for a plate of poha (150g cooked) with peanuts (15g) mixed in and a side of curd (100g).
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 8g | ~320 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm)**
Have 1 cup cooked rice (150g) with 1 katori dal (approx 150g cooked moong or masoor) and a small bowl of mixed veg sabzi (100g) — add a pinch of jeera and a squeeze of lemon to the dal for extra flavour.
(P: 18g | C: 68g | F: 6g | ~400 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm)**
Tea with a handful of roasted chana (30g) — you can toss the chana with a bit of chaat masala if you want.
(P: 6g | C: 16g | F: 2g | ~100 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm)**
2 rotis (approx 60g total) with 100g paneer bhurji and a big bowl of salad (cucumber, tomato, onion) with a squeeze of lemon — or swap the bhurji for 1 katori rajma (150g cooked) if you prefer something lighter.
(P: 26g | C: 48g | F: 14g | ~440 kcal)

**Post-Workout (if training today)**
1 scoop whey protein (25g) mixed in water right after your session.
(P: 20g | C: 3g | F: 1g | ~100 kcal)

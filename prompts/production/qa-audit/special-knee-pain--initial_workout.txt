**Warmup Routine (Do this before every session, around 10 minutes)**

Start with 5 minutes of brisk walking on the treadmill or a few minutes on the cycle — just enough to get your heart rate up and joints warm. Then move into some joint mobility: arm circles forward and back (10 each way), hip circles (10 each direction), leg swings front-to-back and side-to-side (10 each leg), and some bodyweight glute bridges (15 reps) to wake up your hips. Finish with a few cat-cow stretches on the mat if your gym has space, or just some gentle torso twists. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe 40% of your working weight for 8–10 reps, then 60% for 6 reps — just to groove the pattern and prep your nervous system.

**Day 1 — Lower Body Strength**

*Aim for around 8,000 steps today — you're training, so keep general activity steady but don't go overboard.*

- **Leg Press** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
  *This is your main lower-body builder today. Feet hip-width, toes slightly out, and only go as deep as feels comfortable for your knees — no need to jam them into your chest. Control the descent, explode up.*

- **Romanian Deadlift (Barbell or Dumbbells)** — 3 sets x 8 reps  
  *Hinge at the hips, slight bend in the knees, bar stays close to your legs. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings, not your lower back. This one's gold for the posterior chain.*

- **Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  *Squeeze at the top, control the release. No swinging.*

- **Goblet Box Squat (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  *Hold a dumbbell at your chest, squat back to a box or bench set at a height that keeps your knees happy — around parallel or slightly above. Tap the box lightly, then stand. This keeps depth controlled and knee-friendly.*

- **Standing Calf Raise (Machine or Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
  *Full range — stretch at the bottom, peak contraction at the top. Calves respond well to higher reps.*

**Core Work:**

- **Plank** — 3 sets x 30–45 seconds  
  *Elbows under shoulders, body in a straight line. Squeeze your glutes and brace your abs like someone's about to poke your stomach.*

- **Dead Bug** — 3 sets x 10 reps per side  
  *Lying on your back, opposite arm and leg extend while the other knee stays tucked. Move slow, keep your lower back pressed into the floor.*

- **Pallof Press (Cable)** — 3 sets x 12 reps per side  
  *Stand side-on to the cable, press out and resist the rotation. Great for anti-rotation strength.*

**Day 2 — Upper Body Push & Pull**

*Another training day — aim for around 8,000 steps. Stay active between sessions but listen to your body.*

- **Seated Cable Row** — 4 sets x 10 reps  
  *You love rows, so let's start here. Pull to your lower ribs, squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of each rep. No jerking back with your torso — let your lats do the work.*

- **Dumbbell Chest Press (Flat Bench)** — 4 sets x 8 reps  
  *Press up and slightly in, dumbbells should almost touch at the top. Lower with control until you feel a stretch in your chest.*

- **Lat Pulldown (Cable, Neutral or Wide Grip)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  *Pull to your upper chest, elbows drive down and back. Think about pulling with your elbows, not your hands.*

- **Dumbbell Shoulder Press (Seated)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  *Press straight up, don't arch your back excessively. Controlled descent.*

- **Cable Tricep Pushdown (Rope or Bar)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  *Elbows stay pinned to your sides, squeeze at the bottom.*

- **Dumbbell Bicep Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  *No swinging. Curl up, squeeze, lower slow. You can alternate arms or do both together.*

**Core Work:**

- **Hanging Knee Raise (or Lying Leg Raise if no bar)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  *Lift knees toward your chest, lower with control. If hanging is tough, do these lying on a bench or mat.*

- **Russian Twist (Bodyweight or Light Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 20 reps total (10 per side)  
  *Sit with knees bent, lean back slightly, twist side to side. Keep your core tight.*

- **Bicycle Crunch** — 3 sets x 15 reps per side  
  *Opposite elbow to knee, extend the other leg. Slow and controlled, no neck yanking.*

**Day 4 — Full Body Conditioning**

*Training day again — target around 8,000 steps. You're back in the gym, let's finish the week strong.*

- **Trap Bar Deadlift (or Barbell Deadlift if no trap bar)** — 4 sets x 6 reps  
  *This is your big strength move today. Hinge at the hips, chest proud, drive through your heels. If trap bar isn't available, conventional barbell deadlift works — just keep the bar close and your back neutral.*

- **Dumbbell Goblet Squat (Controlled Depth)** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  *Same idea as the box squat from Day 1, but no box this time. Squat to a depth that feels good for your knees — don't force it deeper than comfortable.*

- **Single-Arm Dumbbell Row** — 3 sets x 10 reps per side  
  *One hand and knee on a bench, row the dumbbell to your hip. Let your shoulder blade move, get a full stretch at the bottom.*

- **Dumbbell Incline Press** — 3 sets x 10 reps  
  *Bench set to about 30–45 degrees. Press up, control the descent. Hits the upper chest nicely.*

- **Cable Face Pull** — 3 sets x 15 reps  
  *Rope attachment, pull toward your face, elbows high. Squeeze your rear delts and upper back. Great for posture and shoulder health.*

- **Leg Curl (Machine)** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  *Hitting hamstrings again to balance out the week. Control and squeeze.*

**Core Work:**

- **Mountain Climbers (Controlled Pace)** — 3 sets x 20 reps total (10 per side)  
  *Hands on the floor, alternate driving knees toward your chest. Keep your core tight and hips level. No need to rush — controlled is better.*

- **Side Plank** — 3 sets x 20–30 seconds per side  
  *Elbow under shoulder, body in a straight line from head to heels. Squeeze your obliques and glutes.*

- **Reverse Crunch** — 3 sets x 12 reps  
  *Lying on your back, lift your hips off the floor by curling your knees toward your chest. Lower with control.*

**Post-Workout Stretching Routine (Do this after every training session, around 10 minutes)**

After you finish your last set, spend a few minutes stretching the muscles you just worked. Hold each stretch for 30–60 seconds, breathe deep, and don't bounce.

- **Hamstring Stretch** (seated or standing, each leg)
- **Quad Stretch** (standing, pull heel to glute, each leg)
- **Hip Flexor Stretch** (lunge position, each side)
- **Chest Stretch** (doorway or hands behind back)
- **Lat Stretch** (reach one arm overhead and lean to the side, each side)
- **Shoulder Stretch** (pull one arm across your chest, each side)
- **Seated Spinal Twist** (sit and twist gently to each side)
- **Child's Pose** (hold for 60 seconds, breathe deep)

This helps with recovery, keeps you mobile, and honestly just feels good after a solid session.

**Sleep & Recovery**

You're already getting 7–8 hours most nights, which is great — let's keep that consistent and maybe push toward the higher end (8–8.5 hours) on training days if you can. Your body repairs and builds muscle while you sleep, so this is non-negotiable if you want results.

**Ideal sleep window for you:** Aim to be in bed by 10:30–11:00 pm and up around 7:00 am. I know you have dinner around 9:00 pm, so try to keep it on the lighter side — roti, paneer, and salad is perfect. Give yourself at least 90 minutes between finishing dinner and lying down so digestion doesn't mess with your sleep quality.

**A few practical tips:**

- **Cut off chai by 4:00 pm** — I know evening chai is a ritual, but the caffeine can linger and mess with your sleep even if you don't feel it. Switch to herbal tea or warm water with jeera after that.

- **Dim the lights after 8:30 pm** — Bright overhead lights signal your brain to stay awake. Use lamps or softer lighting in the evening to help your body wind down.

- **Screen time curfew at 10:00 pm** — No phone or TV in bed. If you need to check something, do it before 10:00 pm, then leave your phone across the room. The blue light and mental stimulation will wreck your sleep.

- **Keep your room cool and dark** — Use a fan if needed, blackout curtains if you have them. Your body sleeps better in a cooler environment.

- **Try a 10-minute wind-down routine** — After dinner, go for a short walk, do some light stretching, or just sit quietly with your family. This signals to your body that the day is ending.

- **Consistency is key** — Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body loves routine, and this will make falling asleep and waking up so much easier.

- **If you wake up in the middle of the night**, don't check your phone or turn on bright lights. Keep a glass of water by your bed, take a sip, do some deep breathing, and let yourself drift back off.

**Training Intensity**

Since you're just getting started, we're building the habit first and adding intensity as you get comfortable. For your big compound lifts — leg press, deadlifts, rows, presses — leave 1–2 reps in the tank. That means if you think you could do 10 reps, stop at 8 or 9. You should feel challenged, but not like you're grinding out every single rep. This keeps your form clean and your joints happy, especially with your knee.

For isolation and accessory work — curls, tricep pushdowns, leg curls, face pulls — you can push closer to failure. On the last set of these, if you hit your target reps and feel like you have 1–2 controlled reps left in you, go for it. But keep them strict — no swinging, no momentum. If you absolutely have to cheat a tiny bit to get the last rep or two on an isolation move, fine, but only after you've hit failure with good form. And never, ever cheat on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or any heavy barbell movement. Those require perfect form every single rep.

Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Karan!

Alright, let's get you set up for the week. I know your schedule is tight with those 10-hour desk days, so I've kept meal timings close to what you're already doing — breakfast around 9:30am, lunch at 2pm, evening snack at 6:30pm, and dinner by 10pm (we'll try to pull that a bit earlier when possible, but I get it, late finishes happen). The focus this week is getting you into a rhythm that supports fat loss without feeling like you're starving or overthinking every meal. You've got 12 weeks to hit 62kg, and consistency beats perfection every time.

I've built this around foods you already enjoy — dal, paneer, idli, curd rice — and kept it vegetarian throughout. No mushrooms anywhere, promise. Your ₹8000 monthly budget is totally doable with these choices; I've leaned on affordable staples like moong dal, masoor dal, chana, soya chunks, and seasonal veggies. Most of these meals you can prep in bulk or grab easily from your office canteen with small tweaks.

One quick note: I've added approximate gram weights in brackets so you can get a feel for portion sizes, but don't stress about weighing everything to the decimal — a katori of dal is roughly 150g, a medium roti is about 40g, and so on. Use your hand and eye as a guide, and you'll be fine.

Daily averages for the week: ~1850 kcal | P: 130g | C: 200g | F: 55g

**Day 1 – Monday**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**  
Skip the usual office canteen sandwich today. Instead, have 3 idlis (approx 150g total) with 2 tbsp coconut chutney and 1 small katori sambar (about 100ml). If you want a bit more protein, add a small bowl of curd (100g) on the side.  
(P: 12g | C: 58g | F: 6g | ~320 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**  
2 medium rotis (80g total) with 1 katori moong dal (approx 150g cooked) and a small bowl of mixed veg sabzi — lauki, tinda, or whatever's going in the thali today. Add a small katori curd (100g) to round it out.  
(P: 28g | C: 68g | F: 10g | ~470 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**  
Tea (no sugar, or minimal) with 30g roasted chana. You're already doing this, so just keep it going — it's a solid, cheap protein hit.  
(P: 8g | C: 18g | F: 2g | ~120 kcal)

**Dinner (10:00pm)**  
Ditch the biryani tonight. Go for 1 bowl curd rice (approx 150g cooked rice mixed with 100g curd) and a side of 100g paneer bhurji (dry, minimal oil). If you're still hungry, add a small katori dal (100g).  
(P: 32g | C: 52g | F: 18g | ~480 kcal)

**Before Bed (optional, if hungry)**  
1 glass warm milk (200ml, toned) with a pinch of haldi.  
(P: 7g | C: 10g | F: 3g | ~95 kcal)

**Day 2 – Tuesday**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**  
2 medium rotis (80g) with 1 small katori besan chilla mix (about 100g batter, cooked) or swap for a bowl of poha (150g) with peanuts (10g) and a squeeze of lemon. Both work, pick what's easier to grab.  
(P: 14g | C: 62g | F: 8g | ~360 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**  
Same structure as Monday — 2 rotis, 1 katori dal (masoor or moong, 150g), and sabzi. Today, try adding a small katori of chana (100g boiled, tossed with onion, tomato, lemon) instead of extra curd if you want more texture.  
(P: 30g | C: 70g | F: 9g | ~480 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**  
Tea with 30g roasted chana, same as yesterday.  
(P: 8g | C: 18g | F: 2g | ~120 kcal)

**Dinner (10:00pm)**  
1 bowl khichdi (150g rice + 50g moong dal, cooked together) with a side of 100g paneer tikka (grilled or air-fried, minimal oil) and a small bowl of curd (100g). Khichdi is easy on the stomach this late and keeps you full.  
(P: 28g | C: 58g | F: 14g | ~460 kcal)

Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Karan!

Alright, let's get you sorted with a solid week of eating that fits your routine and gets you closer to that 62kg target. I know the office canteen and late dinners are part of your reality right now, so I've built this around your actual meal timings — 9:30am breakfast, 2pm lunch, 6:30pm snack, and 10:30pm dinner. We're keeping it vegetarian, simple, and budget-friendly with foods you already enjoy like dal, paneer, idli, and curd rice. No mushrooms, promise. Let's make this week count.

---

**Day 1 (Monday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
Skip the office sandwich today. Instead, have 3 besan chilla (approx 120g batter total) cooked with a little oil, stuffed with grated paneer (30g) and served with green chutney and a small bowl of curd (100g). Or, if you're in a rush, grab 2 boiled eggs with 2 small multigrain rotis and a side of sliced cucumber.
(P: 26g | C: 42g | F: 14g | ~410 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
From the office thali, pick 2 phulkas (small rotis), 1 katori dal (approx 150g), a small portion of sabzi (100g — avoid anything deep-fried), and finish with a small katori of curd (80g). Skip the rice and any fried papad today.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Your usual tea is fine — just have it without sugar or with half your usual amount. Pair it with 30g roasted chana (about 2 tablespoons) and 10-12 raw almonds. If you're genuinely hungry, add a small apple.
(P: 9g | C: 22g | F: 8g | ~190 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
I know the biryani is tempting, but let's swap it out tonight. Have 1 cup cooked brown rice (approx 150g) or 2 rotis with 1 katori rajma (approx 150g cooked) and a side of cucumber-tomato salad with a squeeze of lemon. If you want something quicker, paneer bhurji (100g paneer) with 2 rotis works too.
(P: 22g | C: 62g | F: 10g | ~420 kcal)

---

**Day 2 (Tuesday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
2 idlis (medium size, approx 100g total) with sambar (1 small katori, approx 100g) and coconut chutney (1 tablespoon). Add a side of 2 boiled egg whites if you want extra protein, or a small glass of buttermilk (150ml).
(P: 12g | C: 48g | F: 6g | ~290 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
Repeat Monday's thali setup — 2 phulkas, 1 katori dal, sabzi, and curd. If the office has chana masala or soya chunk curry today, go for that instead of plain dal for a protein boost.
(P: 20g | C: 56g | F: 9g | ~380 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Tea (low sugar) with a small bowl of roasted makhana (fox nuts, about 25g) and 10 peanuts. Makhana is light, crunchy, and keeps you full without adding much fat.
(P: 6g | C: 18g | F: 7g | ~150 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
Curd rice time! Have 1 cup cooked rice (approx 150g) mixed with thick curd (150g), a pinch of salt, and some chopped cucumber and coriander. Pair it with a small katori of moong dal (approx 100g) on the side for extra protein. Super light and easy on digestion before bed.
(P: 16g | C: 64g | F: 6g | ~370 kcal)

---

**Day 3 (Wednesday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
Same as Monday — 3 besan chilla with paneer and curd, or the 2-egg-2-roti option. Whichever you didn't try on Monday, go for that today.
(P: 26g | C: 42g | F: 14g | ~410 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
Office thali again: 2 phulkas, 1 katori mixed dal or sambar (approx 150g), sabzi, and curd. If they have baingan bharta or lauki sabzi today, those are great low-cal options.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Tea with 2 tablespoons roasted chana and a small banana (about 100g). The banana adds natural sweetness and keeps energy steady till dinner.
(P: 8g | C: 28g | F: 4g | ~170 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
2 rotis with 1 katori chana masala (approx 150g cooked chickpeas in gravy) and a side salad. Or swap the chana for 100g grilled paneer tikka (dry, not in creamy gravy) with 2 rotis and mint chutney. Both options are filling and protein-packed.
(P: 24g | C: 60g | F: 11g | ~430 kcal)

---

**Day 4 (Thursday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
2 small parathas (whole wheat, approx 80g total) stuffed with mashed boiled potato and a little jeera, cooked with minimal oil. Serve with curd (100g) and a side of sliced tomato and onion. Keep the oil under 1 teaspoon per paratha.
(P: 14g | C: 54g | F: 10g | ~360 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
Repeat the office thali formula — 2 phulkas, dal, sabzi, curd. You know the drill by now. If you're bored, ask for an extra serving of dal and skip the sabzi if it's oily.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Tea with a small handful of roasted peanuts (about 20g) and 1 small orange or a few guava slices. The fruit adds fiber and keeps things interesting.
(P: 6g | C: 20g | F: 8g | ~170 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
1 cup cooked quinoa (approx 150g) or brown rice with 1 katori dal tadka (approx 150g) and a side of stir-fried bhindi or beans (100g, very little oil). Quinoa is a nice change from rice and packs more protein, but if it's not in budget, brown rice is totally fine.
(P: 20g | C: 62g | F: 9g | ~410 kcal)

---

**Day 5 (Friday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
Repeat Tuesday's idli-sambar combo, or go for 2 dosas (small, approx 100g batter total) with sambar and chutney. Dosas are thinner and lighter than parathas, so they're a good Friday option.
(P: 12g | C: 50g | F: 6g | ~300 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
Office thali: 2 phulkas, 1 katori dal, sabzi, curd. Same reliable setup. If you're craving variety, see if they have kadhi — it's tangy and pairs well with rotis.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Tea with 2 tablespoons roasted chana and 10 cashews. Cashews add a little richness without going overboard on calories.
(P: 8g | C: 20g | F: 9g | ~180 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
2 rotis with 1 katori soya chunk curry (approx 50g dry soya chunks, cooked in tomato-onion gravy, totaling about 150g cooked) and a side salad. Soya chunks are a budget-friendly protein powerhouse — soak them in hot water for 10 minutes, squeeze out the water, and cook like you would paneer.
(P: 28g | C: 56g | F: 10g | ~420 kcal)

---

**Day 6 (Saturday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
Weekend breakfast! Make a veggie omelette with 3 eggs (or 2 whole eggs + 2 whites), onions, tomatoes, and capsicum, cooked in 1 teaspoon oil. Serve with 2 small multigrain rotis or 2 slices of brown bread. Or, if you're feeling lazy, just have 3 boiled eggs with 2 rotis and green chutney.
(P: 28g | C: 40g | F: 16g | ~430 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
Since you're home, make a simple dal-chawal-sabzi meal. Have 1 cup cooked rice (approx 150g), 1 katori moong dal (approx 150g), and a small bowl of stir-fried lauki or turai (100g). Keep it light and homely.
(P: 16g | C: 66g | F: 6g | ~380 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Tea with a small bowl of sprout salad (moong or chana sprouts, about 80g) tossed with chopped onion, tomato, coriander, lemon juice, and a pinch of chaat masala. It's crunchy, filling, and way more nutritious than biscuits.
(P: 10g | C: 22g | F: 2g | ~140 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
Repeat Wednesday's dinner — 2 rotis with chana masala or grilled paneer tikka, plus salad. Or, if you want something different, have curd rice (same as Tuesday's dinner setup).
(P: 24g | C: 60g | F: 11g | ~430 kcal)

---

**Day 7 (Sunday)**

**Breakfast (9:30am)**
Sunday special: 2 small masala dosas (approx 120g batter total) with sambar (1 katori, approx 100g) and coconut chutney. Or, if you're in the mood for something North Indian, go for 2 aloo parathas (whole wheat, minimal oil) with curd and pickle.
(P: 14g | C: 56g | F: 10g | ~370 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00pm)**
Make a paneer pulao or veg pulao at home. Use 1 cup cooked brown rice (approx 150g), mixed veggies (carrots, peas, beans — about 100g total), and 50g paneer cubes, cooked with whole spices and minimal oil. Pair with a small katori of raita (curd with cucumber, about 100g).
(P: 20g | C: 62g | F: 12g | ~430 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30pm)**
Tea with 2 tablespoons roasted chana and a small pear or apple. Keep it simple and satisfying.
(P: 8g | C: 24g | F: 4g | ~160 kcal)

**Dinner (10:30pm)**
End the week light. Have 2 rotis with 1 katori dal (any variety, approx 150g) and a big bowl of mixed salad (cucumber, tomato, carrot, lettuce — about 150g) with a squeeze of lemon and a sprinkle of black salt. Or, if you're too tired to cook, go for the curd rice option from Tuesday.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 8g | ~370 kcal)

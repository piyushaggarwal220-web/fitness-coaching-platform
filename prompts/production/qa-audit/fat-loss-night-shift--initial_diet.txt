Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Divya!

So good to have you starting this journey! I know night shifts make everything harder — sleep, consistency, cravings — but we're going to work with your schedule, not against it. This week's plan is built around your actual meal times and the foods you already love (lots of dal, paneer, idli, curd rice). The goal is simple: eat well, feel satisfied, and start moving toward 62kg without feeling like you're starving or spending hours cooking. Let's make this first week count.

---

**DAY 1**

**Breakfast (7:00 am, post-shift)**
Have 1 medium paratha (whole wheat, approx 40g atta) with 1 small katori (100g) curd and a small side of pickle, or swap the paratha for 2 idlis (about 100g total) with 1 tablespoon coconut chutney and sambar (1 small katori, ~100g). Chai is fine — just go easy on the sugar, maybe half your usual.
(P: 10g | C: 42g | F: 8g | ~280 kcal)

**Lunch (2:00 pm — light, since you're sleeping)**
A small bowl (150g) of curd rice with a pinch of jeera tadka and a few curry leaves, or a banana with a small handful (20g) of roasted chana if you want something even lighter before sleep.
(P: 8g | C: 35g | F: 4g | ~200 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm, pre-dinner)**
Chai with 30g roasted chana (about 2 tablespoons), or swap chana for 1 boiled egg if you're okay with eggs on veg days — otherwise stick with chana or a small fistful of peanuts (20g).
(P: 7g | C: 15g | F: 4g | ~120 kcal)

**Dinner (8:00 pm, pre-shift)**
1 katori (approx 150g) dal (moong or masoor) with 1 medium bowl (120g cooked) rice and a small serving (80g) of sabzi like bhindi or lauki — no heavy gravies. Or swap rice for 2 rotis if you prefer, and add a small katori (100g) of curd on the side.
(P: 18g | C: 65g | F: 6g | ~380 kcal)

**Total for Day 1: ~980 kcal | P: 43g | C: 157g | F: 22g**

**A few quick notes:**
- I've kept your lunch super light since you're sleeping right after — curd rice is easy to digest and won't sit heavy. If you're genuinely not hungry, the banana + chana option is totally fine too.
- On gym days (morning after your shift), you can add 1 scoop of whey protein (if you have it) mixed with water right after your workout — that'll bump your protein by another 24-25g. If not, no stress — just make sure you're eating breakfast within an hour of finishing your session.
- Dal, paneer, chole, rajma — these are your protein anchors. I've rotated them so you're not eating the same thing every day, but the structure is consistent enough that you won't have to think too hard about what's next.
- If you're craving something sweet post-dinner, a small piece of jaggery (10g) or 1 date is fine — just account for it mentally and maybe skip the evening chai sugar that day.
- Water: you're already hitting 2L, which is solid. Try to get most of it in before your shift starts so you're not running to the bathroom all night.

You've got this, Divya. One week at a time. Let me know how it goes!

**Training Intensity & Rep Range Guidance**

Here's how to approach each session: On your compound lifts (goblet squats, dumbbell presses, rows, deadlifts), leave 1–2 reps in the tank. You should feel strong and controlled, not grinding through a rep where your form falls apart. For isolation work (flyes, lateral raises, curls, extensions), push closer to failure — by your last set, you should struggle to get that final rep with good form. If you do get there, you can squeeze out 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" using a bit of momentum on isolation moves, but *never* on compounds like squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges. Keep those locked in with pristine form.

7. **Consistency**: Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even weekends. This is huge for sleep quality.

**WARM-UP (Every Session — 8–10 minutes)**

Start each evening session like this:
- 2 minutes brisk walking on the spot or light jogging in place
- Arm circles (10 each direction), leg swings (10 each leg, forward/back and side to side), cat-cow stretches (8 reps)
- 5 bodyweight squats, 5 push-ups (or incline on bench if needed), 5 glute bridges
- Then ease into your first compound lift with 2 warm-up sets at a lighter weight before going heavy

---

**MONDAY — Lower Power**

Goblet Squats: 4 sets x 5 reps
(This is your main strength driver for the day — keep the weight heavy enough that the last rep feels tough but controlled. Pause for 1 second at the bottom.)

Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 6 reps
(Hinge at the hips, feel the hamstring stretch, and keep your back neutral.)

Dumbbell Lunges (alternating): 3 sets x 8 reps per leg
(Step length matters more than speed — control the descent.)

Core Work:
- Plank hold: 3 sets x 40–50 seconds
- Dead bugs: 3 sets x 10 reps per side
- Pallof press with light dumbbell (if you have space): 2 sets x 8 reps per side

**Daily step target:** 6,500 steps (training day — include your warm-up movement and walking between sets).

---

**TUESDAY — Upper Push**

Dumbbell Bench Press: 4 sets x 5 reps
(Chest-to-bench, full range of motion, controlled eccentric. This is your main upper body strength lift.)

Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 8 reps
(Adjust your bench to an incline, or use a pillow under your upper back if needed. Hits the upper chest and front delts nicely.)

Dumbbell Flyes (on bench): 3 sets x 10 reps
(Lighter weight here — focus on the stretch and squeeze, not ego. You should feel it in the chest.)

Overhead Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 6 reps
(Strict form, no leg drive. If you need to reduce weight compared to bench, that's totally normal.)

Core Work:
- Dumbbell pullovers: 2 sets x 12 reps
(Lie perpendicular on the bench, light weight, feel the stretch across your chest.)
- Suitcase carries (holding one dumbbell at your side): 2 sets x 30 seconds per side
- Hollow body hold: 2 sets x 30 seconds

**Daily step target:** 6,500 steps.

---

**WEDNESDAY — Active Recovery**

No lifting today — let your muscles start recovering. Instead, spend 20–25 minutes on light mobility and stretching:
- 5 minutes easy walking or light yoga flow
- Pigeon pose: 60 seconds each side
- Quad stretch: 45 seconds each leg
- Hamstring stretch: 60 seconds each side
- Shoulder pass-throughs with a light band or towel: 2 sets x 10 reps
- Child's pose: 90 seconds
- Cat-cow stretches: 10 reps
- Spinal twist: 45 seconds each side

If you're feeling stiff, this is your day to move gently and breathe. No intensity needed.

**Daily step target:** 4,000–5,000 steps (keep it light and easy).

---

**THURSDAY — Lower Hypertrophy**

Dumbbell Goblet Squats: 3 sets x 10 reps
(Slightly lighter than Monday, but higher reps — focus on the pump and time under tension. Tempo: 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up.)

Dumbbell Step-Ups (on bench): 3 sets x 8 reps per leg
(This is a great unilateral movement. Control the descent, don't just drop.)

Dumbbell Deadlifts: 3 sets x 8 reps
(Lighter than the RDLs on Monday, fuller range of motion. Hinge and pull.)

Dumbbell Calf Raises: 3 sets x 12 reps
(Hold a dumbbell in each hand, rise up on your toes, pause for 1 second at the top.)

Core Work:
- Bird dogs: 3 sets x 10 reps per side
- Ab wheel rollouts (if you have one at home, or substitute with dumbbell rollouts): 2 sets x 8 reps
- Lying leg raises: 2 sets x 10 reps

**Daily step target:** 6,500 steps.

**FRIDAY — Rest Day**

Full recovery. No structured training, just normal daily movement.

**Daily step target:** 3,000–4,000 steps (genuine rest — don't push it).

**SATURDAY — Upper Pull & Shoulders**

Dumbbell Rows (single-arm, supported on bench): 4 sets x 6 reps per side

Dumbbell Pullovers: 3 sets x 10 reps

**Daily step target:** 6,500 steps.

---

**SUNDAY — Rest Day**

Full rest. Recover, hydrate, prepare for the week ahead.

**Daily step target:** 3,000–4,000 steps.

**Training Intensity & Form Cues**

On your heavy compound lifts (goblet squats, bench press, rows, RDLs), aim to leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set. You want to feel strong and controlled, not grinding out the last rep. For your isolation work (flyes, raises, curls), push a bit closer to failure — go until you feel the burn and can't squeeze out another clean rep. If you want to add 1–2 controlled "cheat reps" at the very end of an isolation set, that's fine, but never on your compound lifts like squats or deadlifts. Form always comes first, especially on single-leg or unilateral moves.

**Cool-Down & Stretching (5–7 minutes after every session)**

After your last set, take 5 minutes to stretch the main muscle groups you trained that day:
- Chest stretch (doorway or arms behind your back): 45 seconds
- Quad stretch: 45 seconds each leg
- Hamstring stretch: 60 seconds each leg
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 45 seconds each arm
- Tricep stretch: 45 seconds each arm
- Child's pose: 60 seconds
- Spinal twist: 45 seconds each side

Breathe deeply and don't force anything — this is recovery time, not another workout.

**Sleep & Recovery**

You're already doing great with 7–8 hours, so let's protect that. Aim for 7.5–9 hours ideally, and try to keep your bedtime consistent (around 10:30–11 pm if you're training at 6–7 pm). Here's what works for most of my clients in India:

1. **Chai cutoff:** Stop drinking tea or coffee after 4 pm. Your 6:30 pm snack with roasted chana is perfect — keep it there.
2. **Screen time:** Put your phone and laptop away 30–45 minutes before bed. Blue light messes with melatonin.
3. **Meal timing:** Your 9 pm dinner is fine, but try to finish eating by 9:30 pm — gives your body time to digest before sleep.
4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool and dark. Even a small fan helps.
5. **Hydration:** Drink most of your 2L water during the day and early evening — not right before bed, so you're not up at night.
6. **Wind-down routine:** 10 minutes of light stretching, reading, or meditation before bed signals your body it's time to rest.
7. **Consistency:** Sleep and wake at the same time every day, even weekends. Your body loves rhythm.

You've got the foundation — just protect it like you protect your training.

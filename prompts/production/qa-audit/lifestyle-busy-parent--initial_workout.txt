**Warmup Routine (10 minutes, do this before every session)**

Start with 3–4 minutes of brisk walking in place or skipping on the spot to get your heart rate up and blood flowing. Then move into some joint mobility: 10 arm circles forward and back, 10 leg swings each side, 10 hip circles each direction, and 10 bodyweight squats focusing on sitting back and opening up the hips. Finish with 10 glute bridges and 10 cat-cows to wake up your posterior chain and spine. Before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets — maybe 40% of your working weight for 8 reps, then 60% for 5 reps. This primes your nervous system and keeps you safe.

**Day 1 — Lower Body Power**

*Aim for 6,000–7,000 steps today — you're training, so keep general activity moderate.*

1. **Barbell Back Squat** — 4 sets x 5 reps
   *Keep your chest proud, core braced, and sit back like you're aiming for a low chair. Control the descent, explode up. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank on these.*

2. **Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)** — 3 sets x 6 reps
   *Hinge at the hips, keep the bar close to your shins, and feel the stretch in your hamstrings. No rounding through the lower back.*

3. **Bulgarian Split Squat (Dumbbells)** — 3 sets x 10 reps each leg
   *Front foot flat, back foot elevated on a low bench or step. Drop straight down, don't let your knee cave in. These burn, but they're gold for glutes and quads.*

4. **Leg Press** — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Press through your heels, don't lock out hard at the top. You can push closer to failure here.*

5. **Standing Calf Raise (Dumbbells)** — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Full range — up on your toes, squeeze at the top, slow descent. Feel the burn.*

**Core Circuit:**
- **Plank Hold** — 3 sets x 30–45 seconds
- **Lying Leg Raises** — 3 sets x 12 reps
- **Russian Twists (Dumbbell)** — 3 sets x 20 reps total

**Day 2 — Upper Body Strength**

*Aim for 6,000–7,000 steps today.*

1. **Dumbbell Bench Press** — 4 sets x 6 reps
   *Elbows at 45 degrees, not flared wide. Press up strong, control the descent. Leave a rep or two in reserve.*

2. **Cable Seated Row** — 4 sets x 8 reps
   *Pull to your lower ribs, squeeze your shoulder blades together at the back. Don't yank with your lower back — this is all upper back and lats.*

3. **Dumbbell Overhead Press (Standing)** — 3 sets x 8 reps
   *Core tight, glutes engaged. Press straight up, don't arch your lower back to cheat the weight up.*

4. **Cable Lat Pulldown** — 3 sets x 10 reps
   *Wide grip, pull to your upper chest. Think about driving your elbows down and back, not just yanking with your arms.*

5. **Dumbbell Bicep Curl** — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Strict form. No swinging. You can push these closer to failure and maybe sneak in 1–2 controlled cheat reps on the last set after you hit strict failure.*

6. **Cable Tricep Pushdown** — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Elbows pinned to your sides. Full extension at the bottom, squeeze the triceps.*

**Core Circuit:**
- **Dead Bug** — 3 sets x 12 reps per side
- **Side Plank** — 3 sets x 20–30 seconds each side
- **Bicycle Crunches** — 3 sets x 20 reps total

**Day 4 — Full Body Metabolic**

*Aim for 6,000–7,000 steps today.*

1. **Goblet Squat (Dumbbell)** — 4 sets x 10 reps
   *Hold the dumbbell at chest height, elbows inside your knees at the bottom. This is a great squat variation to groove the pattern and keep tension on your quads and glutes.*

2. **Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift** — 3 sets x 10 reps
   *Same hinge pattern as Day 1, just lighter and higher reps. Feel the hamstrings and glutes working.*

3. **Dumbbell Chest Press (Floor Press)** — 3 sets x 12 reps
   *Lie on the floor, press up. This limits range slightly and takes stress off your shoulders — great for higher reps.*

4. **Cable Face Pull** — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Pull the rope to your face, elbows high. Squeeze your rear delts and upper back. These are fantastic for posture and shoulder health.*

5. **Dumbbell Goblet Reverse Lunge** — 3 sets x 10 reps each leg
   *Step back, drop your back knee toward the floor, drive through your front heel to stand. Keep your torso upright.*

6. **Dumbbell Lateral Raise** — 3 sets x 15 reps
   *Light weight, strict form. Raise to shoulder height, control the descent. These fry your shoulders in the best way.*

**Core Circuit:**
- **Hollow Body Hold** — 3 sets x 20–30 seconds
- **Mountain Climbers (Controlled)** — 3 sets x 20 reps total
- **Plank with Shoulder Taps** — 3 sets x 16 reps total

**Post-Workout Stretch Routine (5–7 minutes)**

After each training session, spend a few minutes stretching the muscles you just worked. Hold each stretch for 30–60 seconds per side, breathe deep, and don't bounce.

- **Standing Quad Stretch** (pull your heel to your glutes)
- **Seated Hamstring Stretch** (reach for your toes, keep your back flat)
- **Hip Flexor Stretch** (lunge position, push your hips forward gently)
- **Child's Pose** (sit back on your heels, reach your arms forward)
- **Chest Doorway Stretch** (arm on a doorframe, lean forward gently)
- **Overhead Tricep Stretch** (pull your elbow behind your head)
- **Cat-Cow** (a few slow rounds to decompress your spine)

This helps with recovery, keeps you mobile, and reduces soreness.

**Sleep: Your Secret Fat-Loss Weapon**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid, but let's dial it in even more. Aim for 7.5–9 hours consistently, ideally lights out by 10:30–11 pm so you're up fresh before the kids. Here are a few tweaks that'll help:

- Cut off chai and coffee by 4 pm — caffeine lingers longer than you think, especially if you're sensitive.
- Try to finish dinner by 8:30 pm if you can swing it. Eating late (especially heavy meals) can mess with your sleep quality.
- Dim the lights and put your phone on night mode by 9 pm. Blue light from screens tanks melatonin production.
- Keep your bedroom cool and dark — even a small nightlight can disrupt deep sleep.
- If your mind races at night (hello, working parent life), try 5 minutes of deep breathing or a short body scan meditation before bed.
- Stick to a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends. Your body loves routine.
- If you wake up to pee multiple times, ease up on water after 8 pm.

Better sleep = better recovery = better fat loss. It's non-negotiable.

**Training Intensity**

For your big compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, presses, rows), leave 1–2 reps in the tank. You should finish the set knowing you *could* have done one or two more with good form, but you stopped there. This keeps you safe, builds strength steadily, and prevents burnout.

For isolation and accessory work (curls, lateral raises, leg press, etc.), you can push closer to failure — meaning the last rep or two should feel really hard, but your form stays tight. On the very last set of these exercises, if you hit strict failure and want to squeeze out 1–2 more reps with a tiny bit of momentum (a controlled cheat rep), that's fine — but never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound. Those need perfect form every single rep.

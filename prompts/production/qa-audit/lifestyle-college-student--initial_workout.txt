**WARMUP ROUTINE (Every session, ~10 minutes)**

Start every gym session with this flow:
- 2–3 minutes brisk walk or light jog on the treadmill to get your heart rate up
- 30 seconds arm circles (both directions)
- 10 bodyweight squats, slow and controlled
- 10 cat-cow stretches (on the floor)
- 5–8 arm swings across your body
- 5–8 leg swings (each leg, forward/back and side-to-side)
- A few light band pull-aparts if available

Then, before you get into your main lifts, run through a couple of lighter warm-up sets—nothing heavy, just to groove the movement and prep your nervous system.

**DAY 1 — Lower Power & Core (Monday or your first training day)**

This is your squat day. We're going heavy on the compound, then accessorize smart.

**Main Lift:**
- Barbell Back Squat: 4 sets × 5 reps
  *Cue: Chest up, knees tracking over your toes, controlled descent. Leave one rep in the tank on each set—you should feel strong coming out of that last rep. Two warm-up sets with lighter weight before you load up.*

**Accessory Work:**
- Leg Press: 3 sets × 8 reps
  *Go deeper here than your squat if you can—your knees should come close to your chest at the bottom. Controlled tempo.*
- Leg Curl (machine or cable): 3 sets × 12 reps
  *Really squeeze at the top. This balances out the quad-heavy squat work.*
- Calf Raises (standing, barbell or machine): 3 sets × 15 reps
  *Pause for a second at the top—calves respond well to that.*

**Core Circuit (Pick 3, rotate through the week):**
- Dead Bug: 3 sets × 10 reps per side
  *Slow and controlled. Think about pressing your lower back into the floor.*
- Pallof Press (cable): 3 sets × 12 reps per side
  *Anti-rotation work. Don't let your torso twist.*
- Plank Hold: 3 sets × 30–45 seconds
  *Neutral spine, squeeze your glutes.*

**Daily Step Target:** 7,000–8,000 steps (training day—you'll get some walking to/from the gym plus general college movement)

---

**DAY 2 — Upper Hypertrophy & Strength (Wednesday or your second training day)**

Row day. This is where we build a strong back and shoulders.

**Main Lift:**
- Barbell Bent-Over Row: 4 sets × 6 reps
  *Hinge at the hips, chest nearly parallel to the floor, pull the bar to your lower chest. Squeeze hard at the top. This is a heavy lift—two warm-up sets first, then load up. Leave one rep in the tank.*

**Accessory Work:**
- Lat Pulldown (cable): 3 sets × 8 reps
  *Pull elbows down and back. Full range of motion. Treat this as a strength set.*
- Dumbbell Bench Press: 3 sets × 10 reps
  *Dumbbells give you a bigger range of motion than barbell. Lower them under control, press explosively.*
- Cable Chest Fly: 3 sets × 12 reps
  *Stretch at the bottom, squeeze hard in the middle. This is isolation work—you can push closer to failure here.*
- Dumbbell Lateral Raise: 3 sets × 12 reps
  *Slight bend in elbows, raise to shoulder height. Control the descent.*

**Core Circuit (Pick 3, different from Day 1):**
- Ab Wheel Rollout (or machine): 3 sets × 8–10 reps
  *If you're new to this, start on your knees. Slow and controlled.*
- Hanging Knee Raise (or machine): 3 sets × 10 reps
  *Full range. Don't swing.*
- Landmine Rotation (or cable): 3 sets × 12 reps per side
  *Explosive but controlled. Core stability.*

**Daily Step Target:** 7,000–8,000 steps (another training day)

---

**DAY 3 — Lower Hypertrophy & Accessory (Friday or your third training day)**

Deadlift day with a hypertrophy focus. We're building size here.

**Main Lift:**
- Barbell Deadlift: 3 sets × 5 reps
  *Chest up, shoulders over the bar, drive through your heels. This is a heavy compound—two warm-up sets, then load. Leave one rep in the tank. Never sacrifice form for weight.*

**Accessory Work:**
- Romanian Deadlift (RDL): 3 sets × 8 reps
  *Hinge at the hips, slight knee bend. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings. This complements the deadlift nicely.*
- Leg Extension (machine): 3 sets × 12 reps
  *Quad isolation. Full range, squeeze at the top.*
- Walking Lunges (dumbbell): 3 sets × 10 reps per leg
  *Controlled step, front knee tracking over your toes. Good for balance and unilateral strength.*
- Leg Press: 2 sets × 15 reps
  *Higher reps here for a pump. Less weight than Day 1, but controlled.*

**Core Circuit (Pick 3, different from Days 1 & 2):**
- Ab Crunch (machine or cable): 3 sets × 15 reps
  *Squeeze at the top. Hypertrophy work for the abs too.*
- Side Plank: 3 sets × 30 seconds per side
  *Keep your hips level. Oblique stability.*
- Suitcase Carry (dumbbell): 3 sets × 20 meters per side
  *Walk with weight on one side. Core has to stabilize.*

**Daily Step Target:** 7,000–8,000 steps (final training day)

**FULL REST DAY (Sunday or whichever day suits you)**

No structured training. Just move naturally—light walking around campus, daily activities. Aim for 4,000–5,000 steps. This is your recovery day, so prioritize sleep and nutrition. Your body builds muscle when you rest, so don't feel guilty about taking it easy.

**TRAINING INTENSITY GUIDANCE**

Here's the philosophy: On your heavy compound lifts (squat, row, deadlift), you should finish each set feeling like you could do one or two more reps—that's your safety margin as a beginner. Technique and consistency matter way more than ego lifting. For your accessory and isolation work (leg curls, lateral raises, flyes, etc.), you can push harder—aim to finish within 2–3 reps of true failure on the last set. Never sacrifice form to squeeze out extra reps, especially on anything involving your spine (squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges). A controlled "cheat rep" or two is fine on isolation moves after you've hit strict failure, but it doesn't belong on the big compound lifts.

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (After every session, ~5 minutes)**

Before you leave the gym:
- Quad stretch (each leg): 30–45 seconds
- Hamstring stretch (each leg): 30–45 seconds
- Chest stretch (doorway or cross-body): 30 seconds
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body): 30 seconds each side
- Child's pose or cat-cow: 30–45 seconds
- Spinal twist (lying): 30 seconds each side

Just breathe, relax, and let your muscles settle down. This helps with recovery and keeps you mobile.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is excellent—that's your foundation. Try to aim for 7.5–9 hours when you can, and here's how to protect that sleep in a hostel environment:

1. **Chai cutoff:** No chai or tea after 7 PM. I know that's tough in India, but the caffeine will mess with your sleep quality. Stick to herbal tea or warm milk after dinner if you need something warm.

2. **Meal timing:** You're eating dinner at 9 PM, which is a bit late. If possible, try to eat by 8:30 PM so your digestion isn't in overdrive when you're trying to sleep. If 9 PM is fixed, that's okay—just make sure it's not a huge, heavy meal.

3. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30 minutes before bed. The blue light messes with your melatonin. I know it's hard in a hostel with roommates, but even just keeping it face-down helps.

4. **Hostel noise:** If your roommates are loud, consider earplugs or a white noise app. You can't control everything, but you can control your sleep environment as much as possible.

5. **Post-workout:** Try to finish your gym session by 8 PM so you have time to wind down before sleep. Avoid intense training right before bed.

6. **Hydration:** Drink most of your 2 liters during the day and early evening. Cut back after 8 PM so you're not waking up to use the bathroom.

7. **Consistency:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body loves routine.

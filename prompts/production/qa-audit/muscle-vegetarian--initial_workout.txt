I know consistency has been a struggle before, so we're building a plan that's simple enough to stick with but challenging enough to drive real progress. You've got 4 days a week in the evening, and we're using that time smartly with a Push-Pull-Legs-Upper split that keeps things balanced and lets you recover properly.

A quick note on how to train: on your big compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows, presses), aim to leave about 1–2 reps in the tank — you want to be strong and controlled, not grinding out reps where your form falls apart. On isolation and accessory work, push harder; get closer to failure on those last sets, and only grab a couple of controlled "cheat reps" after strict failure if you need them (but never on squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell compound — those stay strict). That balance keeps you safe and growing.

Warm up properly before each session: 5 minutes of brisk walking or light cycling, then spend 3–4 minutes on arm circles, leg swings, cat-cows, and bodyweight squats to get the joints moving. Before your first heavy lift each day, always run through 2 lighter warm-up sets to groove the movement and prepare your nervous system.

**Day 1 — Lower Power (Squat Focus)**

Warm-up: 5 min brisk walk or light cycle, arm circles and leg swings (10 each side), cat-cow (8 reps), bodyweight squats (10 reps).

Back Squat
2 x 5 warm-up (lighter weight, focus on depth and control)
4 x 5 main sets (heavy, compound focus — this is your main strength driver for the day)
*Cue: chest up, knees tracking over toes, full depth if mobility allows. Rest 2–3 min between sets.*

Leg Press
3 x 8 (controlled tempo, 2 sec down, 1 sec pause, 1 sec up)
*Nice complement to the squat — takes some load off the lower back and lets you push volume.*

Romanian Deadlift (Dumbbell or Barbell)
3 x 8 (feel the hamstring stretch, hinge at the hips)

Leg Curl (Machine)
3 x 12 (last set can go to near failure)

Core Circuit (pick 3, rotate weekly):
- Weighted Cable Crunch: 3 x 12
- Pallof Press (cable): 3 x 10 each side
- Dead Bug (bodyweight): 3 x 10 each side

Cool-down: quad stretch (30 sec each leg), hamstring stretch (30 sec each leg), child's pose (45 sec), spinal twist (30 sec each side).

Daily step target: 6,500 steps (include your commute and post-workout walk).

**Sleep & Recovery Protocol**

You're already sleeping 7–8 hours, which is solid, but let's lock it in properly for muscle growth. Aim for 7.5–9 hours most nights, and try to keep a consistent bedtime (ideally 10:30–11 PM if you're training at 6–7 PM). Here's what works well for an office worker with your schedule:

1. **Chai cutoff**: Stop chai/tea by 5 PM — your 6:30 PM snack can be roasted chana with water or herbal tea, but no caffeine after that.
2. **Dinner timing**: Your 9 PM dinner is fine, but try to finish eating by 9:30 PM; gives your body time to digest before sleep.
3. **Screen time**: Put your phone and laptop away by 10 PM — the blue light messes with melatonin production.
4. **Room temperature**: Keep your bedroom cool (around 18–20°C if possible) and dark.
5. **Hydration**: Drink most of your 2 L of water before 6 PM, then sip lightly after; avoids waking up for bathroom trips.
6. **Meal timing before bed**: If you're hungry close to bedtime, a small glass of Greek yogurt (which you love) 30–45 min before sleep is perfect — easy to digest, has casein protein for overnight recovery.
7. **Stress management**: If your mind is racing, spend 5 min on deep breathing (4 sec in, 6 sec out) before bed — helps settle the nervous system after a busy office day.

**Training Intensity & Technique:**

On your compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, rows, bench press, overhead press), aim to leave 1–2 reps in reserve—you should feel like you *could* do 1–2 more, but you stop. This keeps you safe, lets you accumulate volume over weeks, and reduces injury risk. On isolation and accessory work (leg extensions, face pulls, lateral raises, flyes), push harder—go to within 1–2 reps of failure on your last set, but keep your form strict. If you're tempted to use momentum or bounce the weight, you've gone too far. On the very last set of isolation exercises, you can allow a couple of controlled "cheat reps" (slight momentum or form adjustment) *after* strict failure, but only if you've already hit your target reps with good form. Never do this on compound lifts—squats, deadlifts, RDLs, lunges, or any barbell movement. Your spine and joints will thank you.

Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Vikram,

Great to have you on board! So you're aiming for muscle gain over the next 12 weeks—that's solid. I've built this plan around your existing eating times (breakfast at 8:30, lunch at 1:30, evening snack at 6:30, dinner at 9pm) and your favorites like dal, paneer, and idli. The goal here is to give you enough calories and protein to fuel your gym sessions and build muscle, while keeping things practical and within your ₹8000 budget. You've mentioned consistency is a struggle, so I've kept variety but also built in some repeat days so you're not cooking something new every single day. Let's make this work.

---

**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):** Have a bowl of poha (approx 150g cooked poha) with 2 tbsp roasted peanuts mixed in, a small bowl of curd (approx 100g), and a glass of milk (200ml). Or swap the poha for 2 idlis with a small katori of sambar (approx 150g) if you're in the mood.
(P: 18g | C: 52g | F: 14g | ~420 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):** Have 1.5 cups cooked rice (approx 240g), 1 katori cooked dal (approx 150g, mixed veg dal is great), a small bowl of spinach sabzi (approx 100g cooked), and a small bowl of curd (approx 80g) on the side.
(P: 22g | C: 68g | F: 8g | ~440 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm):** Tea with 2-3 roasted chana (approx 30g chana), and 1 roti with 2 tbsp peanut butter mixed with a pinch of salt.
(P: 16g | C: 28g | F: 12g | ~280 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** Have 3 rotis (approx 90g), 150g paneer bhurji (paneer cooked with onions and tomatoes), a small bowl of mixed salad with lemon juice (approx 150g—cucumber, tomato, onion), and a small glass of milk (150ml).
(P: 32g | C: 42g | F: 18g | ~480 kcal)

**Quick Notes:**
- Drink plenty of water throughout the day—aim for at least 2–2.5L. On training days, add a bit more.
- All rotis are whole wheat unless I say otherwise; dal can be any mix you like (masoor, chana, moong, or a blend).
- If paneer or chicken isn't available on any day, swap for eggs (2–3 eggs per meal) or soya chunks (approx 50g dry weight, soaked)—macros will stay roughly the same.
- Curd is your friend—it's cheap, filling, and great for digestion. Use it daily.
- Keep your tea/coffee simple: no sugar, or just a teaspoon if you need it. Milk counts toward your daily intake.
- On days you train hard in the evening, you can add an extra glass of milk or a banana post-workout if you feel hungry.

You've got this, Vikram. Stick to the plan, hit your workouts, and we'll check in soon. Let me know if anything doesn't work with your schedule or if you need a swap.

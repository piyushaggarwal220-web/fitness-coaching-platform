**Quick Notes:**

- **Fish days (Wednesday & Friday):** You're hitting fish twice a week — great for omega-3s and lean protein. Tilapia, pomfret, or mackerel all work and stay budget-friendly.
- **Ghee:** I've kept it at 1 tbsp per meal where it's used (lunch and dinner). It's calorie-dense but also adds satiety and tastes great, so don't skip it — just measure it out.
- **Chana & peanuts at 6:30 PM:** This is your snack anchor. Roast them fresh or buy them pre-roasted to save time. They're cheap, protein-packed, and keep you full till dinner.
- **Dal:** Stick with whatever dal you like — masoor, moong, chana — all work fine. A katori is roughly 150g cooked, which is your daily target.
- **Rotis & rice:** Both are interchangeable in most meals. If you're in the mood for rotis instead of rice at lunch, just swap the portions (1.5 cups rice ≈ 2–3 rotis).
- **Paneer:** Buy it fresh from the market and store it in the fridge. Paneer bhurji is just paneer crumbled and cooked with onions and tomatoes — super quick and tasty.
- **Salad:** Keep it simple — cucumber, tomato, onion, lemon, salt. No dressing needed. Eats up volume without adding much calorie density.
- **Curd rice:** You mentioned it's a favorite — feel free to swap curd rice [approx 200g curd + 1 cup cooked rice + salt + cumin] in place of any lunch option; just track it as roughly 350 kcal with 12g protein.

This plan sits you at a solid 200–250 kcal surplus daily, which is perfect for muscle gain without excess fat gain. You're hitting 155g protein, which is about 2g per kg of bodyweight — ideal for your training. The food is all stuff you actually eat, the budget works out to roughly ₹250–280 per day, and there's enough repetition that you're not cooking something different every meal.

Stick with it for the week, dial in portions if something feels too light or too heavy, and we'll check in after your first training week to see how you're feeling. You've got this — let's build some muscle.

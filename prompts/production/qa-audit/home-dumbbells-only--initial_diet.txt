7-Day Vegetarian Muscle-Gain Diet Plan: Hey Lakshmi,

Great to have you on board! You've got a solid goal — hitting 62 kg in 12 weeks while building strength at home — and your eating pattern is already pretty structured, which makes our job easier. We're going to keep your favorite foods (dal, paneer, idli, curd rice) front and center, and add just enough protein and calories to fuel your training and muscle growth. Nothing drastic, nothing complicated. Let's get into it.

**MONDAY**

*Breakfast (8:30 am):*
Have 2 cups poha (approx 150g) mixed with 1 small onion, 1 tbsp peanuts, and a pinch of turmeric and salt. Serve with a glass of milk (200 ml, full-fat or toned — your choice).
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 8g | ~310 kcal)

*Lunch (1:30 pm):*
1 katori cooked rice (approx 150g), 1 katori cooked dal (approx 150g, any dal you like — moong, masoor, toor), and a small bowl of any sabzi (approx 100g, cooked). Drizzle 1 tsp ghee or oil over the dal.
(P: 18g | C: 68g | F: 10g | ~450 kcal)

*Evening Snack (6:30 pm):*
1 cup tea with 2 tbsp roasted chana (approx 30g) and a small handful of roasted peanuts (approx 20g). If you want something to munch alongside, grab 2–3 digestive biscuits.
(P: 10g | C: 28g | F: 9g | ~210 kcal)

*Dinner (9:00 pm):*
2 rotis (whole wheat, approx 60g dough), 100g paneer (fresh or from a block, crumbled or cubed) cooked into a dry sabzi with onions and tomatoes, and a side salad with cucumber, tomato, and a squeeze of lemon.
(P: 22g | C: 42g | F: 14g | ~400 kcal)

**Daily Total: ~1370 kcal | Protein: 62g**

**A FEW PRACTICAL NOTES:**

— You're hitting roughly 1400 kcal and 63g protein per day on average, which is a solid surplus for muscle gain at your size. Stick to these portions and you'll be on track.

— Dal is your best friend here — it's cheap, filling, and packed with protein. Don't be shy about rotating between moong, masoor, toor, and chana. A squeeze of lemon juice in your dal makes it taste fresher and helps iron absorption.

— Paneer is great, but it's also calorie-dense, so I've kept portions to 100–120g per serving. If your budget allows, you can swap some paneer days for soya chunks (soaked and cooked) — they're cheaper and still give you solid protein.

— On training days (your 3 evening sessions), try to eat your lunch a good 3–4 hours before you train, and have your evening snack right after your workout if possible — it helps with recovery.

— Water: you're already drinking 2L, which is solid. On training days, aim for 2.5L.

— Ghee and oil: I've kept it to 1 tsp per meal where it's listed. That's enough for flavor and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

— If you ever feel hungry between meals, a glass of milk, a small handful of peanuts, or a piece of fruit (apple, banana, orange) won't hurt.

You've got a realistic plan, your favorite foods are in there, and it fits your schedule and budget. Stick with it, train hard, and let's hit that 62 kg target.

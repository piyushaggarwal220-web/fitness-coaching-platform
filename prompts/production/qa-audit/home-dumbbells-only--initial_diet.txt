**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):** Have 2 cups poha [approx 150g] made with 1 tbsp ghee, mixed with 1 small onion, 1 green chilli, and a pinch of turmeric. Serve with 1 small glass (200ml) low-fat milk on the side.
(P: 8g | C: 52g | F: 12g | ~340 kcal)

**Mid-morning snack (11:00 am):** 1 medium banana [approx 100g] with a small handful of roasted peanuts [approx 25g].
(P: 8g | C: 22g | F: 9g | ~180 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):** 1.5 cups cooked rice [approx 220g], 1 katori cooked dal [approx 150g, about 3/4 cup], and a small bowl of mixed sabzi — carrots, beans, peas [approx 100g]. Drizzle 1 tsp ghee on the rice.
(P: 16g | C: 68g | F: 10g | ~440 kcal)

**Evening snack (6:30 pm):** 1 cup tea with 1/4 cup low-fat milk, and a small bowl of roasted chana [approx 40g] mixed with a pinch of chaat masala and lemon.
(P: 8g | C: 18g | F: 4g | ~130 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** 2 rotis [approx 80g], 150g paneer bhurji [paneer scrambled with onions, tomatoes, and spices], and a small bowl of mixed green salad with lemon juice and salt.
(P: 90g | C: 30g | F: 30g | ~720 kcal)

**Daily total: P: 130g | C: 190g | F: 65g | ~1810 kcal**

**A few practical notes as you go:**

— Keep your tea ritual at 6:30pm — that's non-negotiable, and I've built it in. Just watch the milk portion (1/4 cup is about 60ml).

— Dal and paneer are your best friends here. They're affordable, filling, and packed with protein. Rotate between masoor, chana dal, rajma, and mixed dal through the week so you don't get bored.

— Ghee is your friend too — 1 tsp (about 5g) per meal adds richness and helps with absorption. Don't skip it.

— For your sabzi, buy whatever's in season and cheap at the market — carrots, beans, peas, spinach, cauliflower all work. Aim for variety, but don't stress about exact types.

— Roasted chana is your go-to snack. Buy raw chana in bulk, dry roast it in a pan with a pinch of salt and chaat masala, and store it in a jar. Costs almost nothing and lasts the week.

— Curd rice on Saturday is a lighter lunch option — it's easier to digest and still hits your protein target.

— All rotis are whole wheat unless I say otherwise. 1 roti weighs about 40g.

— When you make paneer dishes, use 1 tsp oil per dish maximum — that keeps the fat in check while keeping things tasty.

You've got a solid eating pattern already, and this plan just optimizes it. Stick to the portions, hit your 3 training days a week, and you'll be on track to hit that 62kg target. Let's build some muscle.

**MONDAY**

*Breakfast (8:30 am)*
Have 2 cups poha (approx 150g cooked) with 1/2 cup roasted peanuts mixed in, a squeeze of lemon, and a pinch of turmeric and chilli powder. Pair it with 1 small glass (200ml) low-fat curd on the side. Or if you want to switch it up, make 3 idli (approx 90g) with 1/4 cup sambar and a small bowl of coconut chutney (about 3 tbsp, 50g).
(P: 16g | C: 42g | F: 11g | ~340 kcal)

*Lunch (1:30 pm)*
Have 1.5 cups cooked rice (approx 200g), 1 katori cooked dal (approx 150g — any dal you like), and 1 small bowl of sabzi (approx 100g mixed veg, cooked in minimal oil). Finish with a small side of sliced cucumber and tomato.
(P: 18g | C: 58g | F: 5g | ~385 kcal)

*Evening Snack (6:30 pm)*
Tea (1 cup, 200ml) made with 1/2 cup low-fat milk and a pinch of ginger, plus a small handful of roasted chana (approx 30g). If you're still hungry, add 2-3 whole wheat biscuits or a small piece of jaggery.
(P: 8g | C: 22g | F: 3g | ~145 kcal)

*Dinner (9:00 pm)*
Have 2 rotis (approx 60g) with 100g paneer cooked as paneer bhurji (crumbled paneer sautéed with onion, tomato, and a tiny bit of oil), plus a side salad of lettuce, tomato, and carrot (approx 150g, no dressing or just a squeeze of lemon).
(P: 24g | C: 28g | F: 14g | ~340 kcal)

**Quick Notes for the Week:**

— Keep your dal and sabzi portions consistent — a katori is roughly a standard bowl, and 100g sabzi is about a small cereal bowl's worth cooked.

— When you cook dal or sabzi, use minimal oil (about 1 tsp per serving). A pinch of mustard seeds, cumin, or curry leaves at the start makes everything taste better without adding calories.

— Your budget of ₹8000 is plenty for this plan — dal, rice, paneer, and seasonal veg are all affordable. Stock up on chana and peanuts when they're on sale.

— Drink water throughout the day (you're already at 2L — good!). It helps with hunger and keeps your energy up during training.

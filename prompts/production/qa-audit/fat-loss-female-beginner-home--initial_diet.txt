**MONDAY**

**Breakfast (8:30 am):** 2 idlis with 1 katori sambar (approx 150g) and 1 tbsp coconut chutney. Side: 1 small bowl (approx 80g) curd with a pinch of salt and cumin.
(P: 12g | C: 42g | F: 3g | ~230 kcal)

**Mid-morning (10:30 am):** 1 glass (250ml) low-fat milk with 1 tbsp peanut powder mixed in, or a small handful of roasted peanuts (approx 25g) if you prefer solid.
(P: 10g | C: 8g | F: 8g | ~140 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm):** 1 katori cooked white rice (approx 150g), 1 katori dal (approx 150g cooked), 1 small bowl mixed vegetable sabzi (approx 120g — carrots, beans, peas, onion), 1 small bowl (approx 80g) curd raita with cucumber and a pinch of salt.
(P: 18g | C: 68g | F: 4g | ~390 kcal)

**Evening snack (6:30 pm):** 1 cup (200ml) tea (with 1 tbsp low-fat milk and no sugar, or use 1 tsp jaggery if you need sweetness), 1 small bowl roasted chana (approx 30g).
(P: 8g | C: 15g | F: 2g | ~95 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm):** 2 rotis (approx 60g wheat flour), 100g paneer bhurji (paneer scrambled with onion, tomato, green chilli, turmeric), 1 small bowl green salad (lettuce, tomato, cucumber, approx 100g), squeeze of lemon juice.

**Quick notes as you go:**

— **Cooking tip:** When you make dal, cook it with turmeric, cumin, and a bay leaf. It tastes better and helps digestion. A squeeze of lemon juice at the end lifts the whole thing.

— **Oil:** Use minimal oil (around 1 tbsp per day for cooking sabzi and poha). Paneer has enough fat already, so you don't need extra ghee on rotis.

— **Curd:** Stick to low-fat or regular curd — it's your protein friend and keeps you full. A small bowl (approx 80–100g) is a perfect portion.

— **Roasted chana:** Buy it in bulk from your local grocer. It's cheap, lasts forever, and is a solid snack when you need something quick.

— **Flexibility:** If you're tired of a meal, swap it with another day's meal (e.g., if you want Monday's breakfast on Wednesday, go for it). Just keep portions the same.

— **Water:** You're already hitting 2L, which is solid. Keep it up — it helps with hunger and recovery.

— **Budget check:** This plan sits comfortably within your ₹8000 monthly budget. Rice, dal, and paneer are your anchors; they're affordable and fill you up.

You've got this, Sneha. Consistency is your superpower here — stick to meal timings, hit your protein, and trust the process. Let's make those 12 weeks count. 💪

Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Geeta! So excited to get you started on this journey to 62kg. I've built this week's plan around your love for dal, paneer, and idli — keeping things simple and realistic for your office routine and ₹8000 budget. The portions are designed to create a steady deficit while keeping your energy up for those evening gym sessions. Let's make this week count!

**DAY 1**

**Breakfast (8:30 am)**
Have 1.5 cups poha (about 60g raw rice flakes) cooked with peas, peanuts (15g), and a squeeze of lemon, or go for 3 idlis (medium size, approx 150g total) with 2 tbsp coconut chutney and 1 small bowl sambar (100ml).
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 10g | ~340 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm)**
1 cup cooked rice (about 150g cooked), 1 katori dal (150g cooked moong or toor dal), 1 small katori mixed veg sabzi (100g — beans, carrots, capsicum), and a small bowl curd (80g).
(P: 18g | C: 62g | F: 8g | ~380 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm)**
Tea with 30g roasted chana and 1 small apple (about 100g).
(P: 7g | C: 28g | F: 3g | ~160 kcal)

**Dinner (9:00 pm)**
2 rotis (medium, approx 60g total), 100g paneer bhurji cooked with onions and tomatoes, and a big bowl of cucumber-tomato-onion salad with lemon (150g).
(P: 24g | C: 42g | F: 14g | ~390 kcal)

**Total for Day 1: P: 61g | C: 184g | F: 35g | ~1,270 kcal**

**Quick Tips Woven In:**
- On gym days (let's say Mon/Wed/Fri), try to have your evening snack about 1.5–2 hours before your workout so you're fueled but not too full.
- If you're still hungry after dinner, add an extra 50g curd or a small glass of buttermilk — it's light and won't mess with your sleep.
- Roasted chana is your best friend this week — cheap, crunchy, protein-packed. Buy a big batch and keep it handy at your desk.
- For the sabzis, a pinch of garam masala or a squeeze of lemon at the end makes a huge difference without adding calories.
- Don't stress if you swap rice for 2 rotis at lunch on any day — the macros stay roughly similar, and it's about what keeps you satisfied.

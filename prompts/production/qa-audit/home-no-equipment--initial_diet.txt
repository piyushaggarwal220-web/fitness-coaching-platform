Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Tanvi!

Great to have you on board. Your goal is to hit 62 kg in 12 weeks, and I can see consistency has been tricky for you in the past — so we're keeping this plan simple, realistic, and built around meals you already love (dal, paneer, idli, curd rice). You're eating at fixed times, which is perfect. We'll work within your routine, keep portions controlled, and make sure you're getting enough protein to hold onto muscle while we lean out. Let's make this week solid.

**MONDAY**

*Breakfast (8:30 am):*
Have a bowl of poha (about 1.5 cups cooked, ~150g) with a small handful of roasted peanuts (20g) mixed in, plus a glass of milk (200ml) on the side. Squeeze a bit of lemon on the poha and add a pinch of turmeric — keeps it light and tasty.
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 8g | ~330 kcal)

*Snack (6:30 pm):*
Tea with a small bowl of roasted chana (about 30g) — this is your regular routine, so stick with it.
(P: 8g | C: 18g | F: 3g | ~130 kcal)

*Lunch (1:30 pm):*
One plate of rice (about 150g cooked), 1 katori of dal (approx 150g cooked — moong or masoor work great), and a small bowl of sabzi (about 100g, any seasonal veg). Drizzle a tiny bit of ghee on the rice if you want — no need to skip it entirely.
(P: 14g | C: 68g | F: 5g | ~410 kcal)

*Dinner (9:00 pm):*
Two rotis (about 60g flour total), 100g paneer (paneer bhurji or cubed in a light tomato-based curry), and a small salad with cucumber, tomato, and onion (about 150g total). Add a squeeze of lemon and a pinch of chaat masala.
(P: 22g | C: 38g | F: 14g | ~390 kcal)

**Quick Tips:**

• Keep your water intake steady — you're already at 2L, which is solid. Try to drink a glass before each meal; it helps with fullness.
• On days you train in the evening, eat your snack (tea + chana) about 30–45 minutes *before* your workout, not after. It'll give you energy without sitting heavy.
• Dal and paneer are your protein anchors — they're affordable, filling, and work perfectly for what we're doing. Don't skip them.
• Portion sizes matter more than perfection. If a roti looks thicker than usual, that's okay — just be consistent.
• Avoid adding extra ghee or oil beyond what's mentioned. A tiny drizzle is fine, but "a splash" adds up fast.

You've got this week. Stick to the routine, keep showing up, and consistency will become the default. Let's go.

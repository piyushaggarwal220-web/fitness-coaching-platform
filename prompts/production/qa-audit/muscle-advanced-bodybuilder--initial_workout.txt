**WARMUP ROUTINE (10 minutes — do this before every session)**

Start with 3–4 minutes of light cardio (brisk walk on the treadmill or a few minutes on the bike — nothing intense, just get the blood moving). Then spend 2–3 minutes on joint mobility: arm circles and shoulder dislocations, leg swings (forward/back and side to side), cat-cow stretches, and a few bodyweight squats to open up your hips. Finish with 10–15 light pushups and 10 bodyweight rows (grab a low bar or the cable station) to prime the movement patterns. Before you hit your first heavy compound lift of the day, always run through 2 lighter warm-up sets — around 50% and 70% of your working weight — to groove the movement and prepare your nervous system.

**TRAINING INTENSITY NOTES**

On your main compound lifts (squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows, overhead press), you should always leave 1–2 reps in the tank. This means you're working hard but not grinding out that last rep to absolute failure — it keeps your form tight, minimizes injury risk, and allows for better recovery. On isolation and accessory exercises (leg curls, flyes, lateral raises, etc.), you can push closer to failure and even add a couple of controlled cheat reps after hitting strict failure on the last set (think a slight swing on lateral raises or a bit of body English on a leg curl), but never on any barbell compound movement like squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges. Those demand perfect form.

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're currently getting 7–8 hours, which is solid, but for muscle gain and fat loss at this intensity, aim for 7.5–9 hours per night. Ideally, try to hit the bed by 11 PM on training nights (since you train in the evening) and wake around 7–7:30 AM. Here are some practical tips that fit your lifestyle:

1. **Chai cutoff:** No chai or caffeine after 4 PM. I know chai is a staple, but even one cup at 5 or 6 PM can mess with your sleep quality. Stick to your 6:30 PM snack of roasted chana (great choice, by the way) and herbal tea if you need something warm.

2. **Screen time:** Put your phone away 30–45 minutes before bed. The blue light messes with melatonin production, and scrolling keeps your mind active. Use that time to stretch, read, or just wind down.

3. **Meal timing:** Your 9 PM dinner is fine, but try to finish eating by 9:30 PM, especially heavier meals. This gives your body time to digest before sleep. Your 8:30 AM breakfast and 1:30 PM lunch timing is solid — keep that consistent.

4. **Room temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool (around 16–18°C if possible) and dark. This is one of the most underrated sleep hacks.

5. **Consistency:** Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on rest days. Your body loves routine, and it'll make the 7.5–9 hours feel more natural.

6. **Water intake:** You're at 2L per day, which is good, but try to front-load it — drink most of it before 6 PM. This way you're not waking up multiple times to pee at night.

7. **Stress management:** You mentioned moderate stress. On training days, the gym itself is a great stress outlet. On rest days, try 5–10 minutes of deep breathing or meditation, or just take a walk without your phone. Even small wins here add up.

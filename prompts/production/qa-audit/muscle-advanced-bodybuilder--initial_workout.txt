No injuries, good sleep, and you're already hitting the gym 6 days a week in the evenings — perfect. I've built you a Push-Pull-Legs split across 6 days that leans into your love for squats and rows, keeps compound lifts heavy, and leaves room for the isolation work that'll shape your physique.

Each session is designed to fit your 90-minute window comfortably. You'll warm up for about 10 minutes, hit your main lifts with intensity, and finish with core and mobility work. Recovery days are baked in — one active recovery session with light movement and stretching, one full rest day.

**How to approach the intensity:**
On your big compounds (squats, deadlifts, rows, bench, overhead press), aim for 3–5 reps per set and leave about 1–2 reps in the tank. You're strong enough to know the difference between pushing hard and ego-lifting. For isolation work — leg extensions, cable flyes, dumbbell curls, that kind of thing — push closer to failure, and if you want to squeeze out 1–2 controlled cheat reps on the last set after strict failure, that's fine. But never do this on barbell squats, deadlifts, RDLs, or lunges — those need clean form throughout.

**Warm-up routine (10 minutes):**
Start with a 2–3 minute brisk walk or light jog on the treadmill to get your heart rate up. Then move through some joint mobility: arm circles (forward and back), leg swings (front-to-back and side-to-side), bodyweight squats (just 10–12 slow reps to groove the pattern), and a couple of cat-cow stretches. Finish with some light band pull-aparts or scapular work if you've got a resistance band. This preps your nervous system and gets the blood flowing. Before you get into your main compound lift each day, run through 2 lighter warm-up sets — nothing heavy, just enough to feel the movement.

**Day 1 — Lower Power (Monday)**
Focus: Heavy quads, posterior chain strength, core stability.

Squat (Barbell) — 4 sets x 4 reps
(This is your anchor. Full depth, controlled descent, explosive drive up. Leave 1 rep in the tank.)

Romanian Deadlift (Barbell) — 3 sets x 5 reps
(Keep your chest up, slight knee bend, feel the hamstring stretch. This complements the squat beautifully.)

Leg Press — 3 sets x 6 reps
(Go heavy here since you've got the barbell squat covered. Full range, no locking out at the top.)

Leg Curl (Machine) — 3 sets x 10 reps
(Slow eccentric, squeeze at the top. Balances the quad-heavy day.)

Core: Barbell Rollouts — 3 sets x 8 reps, Pallof Press (cable, both sides) — 3 sets x 10 each side, Dead Bug — 2 sets x 12 reps

Step target: 8,000 steps (training day — get some walking in before or after your session).

**Day 2 — Upper Push (Tuesday)**
Focus: Chest, shoulders, triceps, pressing strength.

Bench Press (Barbell) — 4 sets x 5 reps
(Retract your scapula, touch your chest, drive through your feet. This is a full-body lift.)

Incline Dumbbell Press — 3 sets x 6 reps
(Upper chest and front delts. Control the descent, explosive up.)

Cable Flye — 3 sets x 10 reps
(Stretch at the bottom, squeeze the chest at the top. Really feel the pec contraction.)

Overhead Press (Barbell) — 3 sets x 5 reps
(Strict form, no leg drive. Shoulders, chest, and core all working hard.)

Tricep Rope Pushdown — 3 sets x 12 reps
(Full extension, controlled return. Last set, push to failure.)

Core: Weighted Ab Crunch (cable) — 3 sets x 12 reps, Landmine Rotation — 3 sets x 10 each side, Hollow Body Hold — 2 sets x 20 seconds

Step target: 8,000 steps.

**Day 3 — Pull & Back (Wednesday)**
Focus: Back thickness, lats, pulling strength, biceps.

Barbell Row (Bent-Over) — 4 sets x 5 reps
(One of your favorites — and for good reason. Chest up, elbows tucked, drive through your legs. This is a power move.)

Deadlift (Conventional) — 3 sets x 4 reps
(The king of all lifts. Neutral spine, big breath, explosive drive. Leave 1 rep in reserve.)

Lat Pulldown — 3 sets x 8 reps
(Lean back slightly, pull elbows down and back. Mind-muscle connection here.)

Cable Row (Seated or Standing) — 3 sets x 8 reps
(Squeeze your lats and rhomboids. Controlled return.)

Dumbbell Curl — 3 sets x 10 reps
(Strict form, no swinging. Slow eccentric, peak contraction at the top.)

Core: Ab Wheel Rollout — 3 sets x 8 reps, Suitcase Carry (dumbbell, both sides) — 3 sets x 40 meters each side, Bird Dog — 2 sets x 12 each side

Step target: 8,000 steps.

**Day 4 — Lower Hypertrophy (Thursday)**

Leg Press — 4 sets x 8 reps
(Higher reps today for the pump and metabolic stress. Full range, controlled tempo.)

Squat (Barbell, Paused) — 3 sets x 6 reps

Leg Extension (Machine) — 3 sets x 12 reps

Walking Lunge (Dumbbell) — 3 sets x 10 each leg

Leg Curl — 3 sets x 12 reps
(Hamstring finisher. Squeeze and control.)

Core: Cable Woodchop — 3 sets x 10 each side, Decline Sit-up — 3 sets x 12 reps, Plank with Shoulder Tap — 2 sets x 20 taps total

Step target: 7,000 steps (still training, but a bit lighter on the legs post-workout).

Bent-Over Row (Dumbbell) — 3 sets x 8 reps

**Day 6 — Active Recovery (Saturday)**
Focus: Light movement, mobility, injury prevention, mental reset.

No structured lifting today. Instead, get outside and walk for 30–45 minutes at an easy pace — aim for 10,000 steps. This is your chance to move without intensity, clear your head, and let your body recover.

After your walk, spend 15–20 minutes on mobility work:
- Deep bodyweight squats (hold for 30 seconds, 3 reps)
- World's greatest stretch (both sides, 30 seconds each)
- Pigeon pose (both sides, 45 seconds each)
- Downward dog to upward dog flow (10 reps, 3 seconds each)
- Quad stretch against a wall (both sides, 45 seconds each)
- Child's pose (60 seconds)

This keeps you moving, prevents stiffness, and sets you up for a strong week ahead.

**Day 7 — Full Rest (Sunday)**
Step target: 3,000–4,000 steps (just light daily living — no structured movement, but don't be sedentary).

This is your true recovery day. Sleep in if you can, stay hydrated, and let your body repair. You've earned it.

**Cool-down routine (post-workout, 5–10 minutes):**
After every training session, spend a few minutes stretching the major muscles you've worked:
- Chest stretch (doorway or across-body, 45 seconds each side)
- Lat stretch (reach overhead, lean to the side, 45 seconds each side)
- Quad stretch (standing or prone, 45 seconds each leg)
- Hamstring stretch (forward fold or lying, 60 seconds)
- Glute stretch (pigeon pose or figure-4, 45 seconds each side)
- Shoulder stretch (cross-body, 30 seconds each arm)
- Spinal twist (lying, 30 seconds each side)

Keep it easy, breathe deeply, and use this time to transition from training mode back to recovery mode.

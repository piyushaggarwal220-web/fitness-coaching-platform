Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Priya!

So excited to get you started on this plan. I know juggling two kids and finding time to train isn't easy, so I've kept this super practical and built around the foods you already love — dal, paneer, idli, curd rice. The goal this week is to create a small calorie deficit while keeping protein high enough to protect muscle as you lose fat. You'll feel satisfied, not starved, and everything fits into your existing meal times.

I'm working with roughly 1650-1700 kcal per day and aiming for around 120-130g protein. You won't see a formal macro table here — just trust the portions I've given you. I've added gram weights in brackets so you can eyeball things with a scale if you want, but katori/roti/bowl language works just fine too.

---

**DAY 1 – Monday**

**Breakfast (8:30 am)**  
2 idlis (approx 150g total) with 1 tbsp coconut chutney and a small bowl of sambar (1 katori, around 150ml), or swap for 1 cup poha (150g cooked) with peanuts (10g) and a squeeze of lemon.  
(P: 12g | C: 48g | F: 6g | ~290 kcal)

**Lunch (1:30 pm)**  
1 katori cooked rice (approx 150g), 1 katori moong dal (approx 150g cooked), and a small bowl of sabzi like bhindi or beans (100g). Add a small side of curd (100g) if you like.  
(P: 18g | C: 62g | F: 8g | ~380 kcal)

**Evening Snack (6:30 pm)**  
Tea with 30g roasted chana (about 2 tbsp).  
(P: 6g | C: 15g | F: 2g | ~100 kcal)

**Post-Workout (if training today, around 7:00 pm)**  
1 scoop whey protein (25g powder) mixed in water or with 1 small banana if you're hungry.  
(P: 24g | C: 3g | F: 1g | ~120 kcal)  
*If no workout today, skip this and have your evening tea as usual.*

**Dinner (9:00 pm)**  
2 rotis (approx 60g total) with 100g paneer bhurji (cooked with minimal oil, onions, tomatoes) and a big bowl of salad (cucumber, tomato, carrot — as much as you want, around 150g).  
(P: 24g | C: 42g | F: 16g | ~410 kcal)

**Before Bed (optional)**  
100g curd, plain or with a pinch of jeera powder.  
(P: 5g | C: 6g | F: 2g | ~60 kcal)

**A Few Quick Tips as You Go:**

- On training days (you mentioned 3 days a week in the evening), have your whey shake right after your session. On non-training days, skip the whey and just stick to the meals as written — you'll still hit your protein target with food.
- If you're ever short on time in the morning, keep the idli batter ready in the fridge or batch-cook poha ingredients the night before. You can also swap breakfast for 2 boiled eggs with 1 roti if you're okay with eggs and want a quicker option — but since you're vegetarian, I've kept it fully veg here.
- Roasted chana is your best friend for snacks — cheap, crunchy, protein-rich. Keep a small dabba at your desk or in your bag.
- Don't stress if dinner happens closer to 9:30 pm some nights because of the kids. Just try to keep it light and veggie-heavy, and you'll be fine.
- Water goal is 2-3 litres a day. You're already doing 2L, which is great — if you can push it to 2.5L, even better for fat loss and energy.

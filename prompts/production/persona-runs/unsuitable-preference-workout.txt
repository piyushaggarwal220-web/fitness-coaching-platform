Great to have you on board. I can see you're keen on heavy strength work—that's awesome—and now we're channeling that into a fat loss phase with bodyweight training at home. Three sessions a week is perfect for building consistency without burning out, and 30 minutes keeps it tight and efficient. Since you're starting out with bodyweight, we're going to build real strength and movement quality while keeping your heart rate up enough to support your fat loss goal. No fancy equipment needed—just your bodyweight, a bit of floor space, and commitment.

Here's how the week flows:

**WARMUP (Every Session — 8–10 Minutes)**
- 2 min brisk walk or light jogging on the spot
- 10 arm circles each direction
- 10 leg swings forward/back each leg
- 10 lateral leg swings each leg
- 8 bodyweight squats, slow and controlled
- 5 push-ups, easy pace (just to prime the muscles)
- 30 sec plank hold
- 10 glute bridges

Move through this as a flow—no rest between movements. You should feel warm and ready to go.

---

**DAY 1 — Lower Body Power & Control (Monday or whenever suits your week)**

Focus: Explosive lower body work, building strength in the patterns you love, plus core stability.

1. **Bodyweight Squats** — 4 sets × 6 reps
   - Go slow on the way down (3 seconds), pause at the bottom for 1 second, explode up. This is where the strength lives.
   - Leave 2–3 reps in the tank on each set—you should feel strong, not destroyed.
   - Rest 90 seconds between sets.

2. **Single-Leg Romanian Deadlifts (Hip Hinge Pattern)** — 3 sets × 8 reps per leg
   - Stand on one leg, hinge at the hip, let the other leg extend behind for balance. Feel the hamstring and glute fire.
   - Controlled tempo—this builds posterior chain strength and balance.
   - Rest 60 seconds between sets.

3. **Jump Squats** — 3 sets × 10 reps
   - Explosive power, controlled landing. Land soft and quiet.
   - Rest 90 seconds.

4. **Reverse Lunges** — 3 sets × 10 reps per leg
   - Step back, lower until both knees are at 90°, drive through the front heel to stand.
   - Alternate legs smoothly. Rest 60 seconds between sets.

5. **Core Circuit (Pick 3 of these, rotate daily):**
   - Plank hold — 3 sets × 40–50 seconds
   - Dead bug — 3 sets × 12 reps per side
   - Mountain climbers — 3 sets × 20 reps
   - Rest 45 seconds between core exercises.

**Daily Step Target:** 10,000 steps (training day—stay active outside the session too).

---

**DAY 2 — Upper Body Push & Core (Wednesday or 2–3 days after Day 1)**

Focus: Chest, shoulders, triceps, and upper body pressing strength.

1. **Push-ups (Standard)** — 4 sets × 8–12 reps
   - Hands shoulder-width, body in a straight line, lower until chest nearly touches the floor.
   - If full push-ups feel too hard right now, drop to your knees but keep your core tight—no sagging hips.
   - Rest 90 seconds between sets.
   - On the last set, go to near failure (1–2 reps left).

2. **Pike Push-ups** — 3 sets × 8–10 reps
   - Hands on the floor, hips high (like an inverted V), lower your head toward the floor between your hands.
   - This targets shoulders hard. Controlled descent.
   - Rest 75 seconds between sets.

3. **Tricep Dips (Using a Chair or Bench)** — 3 sets × 10–12 reps
   - Hands behind you on a sturdy chair or low bench, legs extended or bent (easier), lower your body until elbows are at 90°.
   - If you need to, keep feet on the ground for support.
   - Rest 75 seconds.

4. **Incline Push-ups** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
   - Hands on a higher surface (bed, couch, table edge) to reduce the load. Great for building volume without overloading.
   - Rest 60 seconds.

5. **Core Circuit (Different from Day 1):**
   - Plank with shoulder taps — 3 sets × 16 reps (8 per side)
   - Bird dog — 3 sets × 12 reps per side
   - Hollow body hold — 3 sets × 30–40 seconds
   - Rest 45 seconds between exercises.

**Daily Step Target:** 9,000 steps (solid training day, but slightly lighter than lower body days).

---

**DAY 3 — Lower Body Hypertrophy & Conditioning (Friday or 2–3 days after Day 2)**

Focus: Higher reps, time under tension, and metabolic conditioning for fat loss.

1. **Goblet Squat Hold (Bodyweight Variation)** — 4 sets × 15–20 reps
   - Standard squats but with higher reps and a controlled tempo. 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up.
   - This builds work capacity and keeps heart rate elevated.
   - Rest 60 seconds between sets.

2. **Reverse Lunges** — 3 sets × 12 reps per leg
   - Same as Day 1 but higher reps. Steady pace, controlled.
   - Rest 60 seconds.

3. **Glute Bridges** — 3 sets × 15–20 reps
   - Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive through your heels, squeeze glutes at the top.
   - Pause 1 second at the top of each rep.
   - Rest 60 seconds.

4. **Burpees (or Modified Burpees if needed)** — 3 sets × 8–10 reps
   - Step or jump back into a plank, do a push-up, step or jump feet back to standing, stand up.
   - If this is too intense, do a step-back version instead of jumping.
   - These are conditioning work—keep moving, but controlled.
   - Rest 90 seconds between sets.

5. **Core Circuit (Different again):**
   - Bicycle crunches — 3 sets × 20 reps
   - Reverse crunch — 3 sets × 15 reps
   - Side plank — 3 sets × 30–40 seconds per side
   - Rest 45 seconds between exercises.

**Daily Step Target:** 11,000 steps (highest step target—conditioning day, so keep moving throughout the day).

---

**ACTIVE RECOVERY DAY (Pick one day between training sessions—Tuesday or Thursday)**

No structured workout. Instead:
- 20–30 min easy walk (outdoor if possible, or indoors).
- 10–15 min light mobility and stretching:
  - Cat-cow stretches — 10 reps
  - World's greatest stretch — 5 per side
  - Hip 90/90 stretch — 60 sec per side
  - Child's pose — 60 sec
  - Quad stretch — 45 sec per leg
  - Hamstring stretch — 60 sec per leg
- Focus on breathing and moving gently. This day is about recovery, not intensity.

**Daily Step Target:** 7,000 steps (easy day—just move naturally).

---

**REST DAY (One full day—Saturday or Sunday)**

No training. Prioritize sleep, hydration, and eating well. Light walking is fine if you feel like it (3,000–5,000 steps), but nothing structured.

**Daily Step Target:** 5,000 steps (true rest—no pressure).

---

**INTENSITY GUIDANCE FOR THE WEEK**

On Days 1 and 3 (lower body), leave 2–3 reps in the tank on the heavy compound movements (squats, lunges, glute bridges). You want to feel strong and in control, not completely gassed. On the conditioning work (burpees), you can push harder—go closer to failure on those.

On Day 2 (upper body), the first 2–3 sets of push-ups should feel controlled; on the final set, push to near failure (1–2 reps left). Same for tricep dips. The incline push-ups are higher volume, so aim for steady reps without grinding.

On core work, you can push closer to failure since these are smaller movements and lower injury risk. But keep form tight—no sloppy reps.

---

**COOLDOWN & STRETCHING (Every Session — 5–7 Minutes)**

After you finish, spend a few minutes stretching the muscles you just worked:

- **Quad stretch** — 45 sec per leg (grab your foot, pull toward your glute)
- **Hamstring stretch** — 60 sec per leg (fold forward, hands toward toes)
- **Hip flexor stretch** — 45 sec per side (low lunge position)
- **Chest opener** — 45 sec (clasp hands behind back, straighten arms, open chest)
- **Shoulder stretch** — 45 sec per arm (cross one arm across your body, gentle pull)
- **Child's pose** — 60 sec (knees wide, forehead to floor, relax)
- **Cat-cow** — 10 slow reps (on hands and knees, alternate between arching and rounding your spine)

Breathe deeply through each stretch. No bouncing—just hold and relax into it.

---

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already getting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Here's how to optimize it for fat loss and training recovery:

1. **Aim for 7.5–9 hours each night.** You're in the sweet spot, so try to hit closer to 8–9 on training days.

2. **Consistency is key.** Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. Your body loves rhythm.

3. **Dim the lights 30–45 minutes before bed.** This helps your body produce melatonin naturally. If you're scrolling your phone, use a blue light filter or wear blue light glasses.

4. **Keep your room cool** (around 65–68°F if possible). A cooler room helps you sleep deeper.

5. **Avoid heavy meals 2–3 hours before bed.** Light snacks are fine, but don't train hard and then eat a huge meal right before sleep—your digestion will interfere with rest.

6. **Limit caffeine after 2 PM.** I know chai and coffee are part of the routine, but late afternoon caffeine can disrupt sleep quality.

7. **If your mind is racing, try 4-7-8 breathing:** Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale for 8. Do this 4–5 times before bed. It genuinely helps.

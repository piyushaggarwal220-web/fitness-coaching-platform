Weekly Diet Plan: Hey Priya!

So excited to help you work towards that 62kg goal over the next 3 months! I've built this week's plan around your usual meal times and the foods you already love — dal, paneer, idli, curd rice — so it doesn't feel like you're eating "diet food." We're tackling that late-night snacking by making sure dinner is satisfying and protein-rich, and I've kept everything within your 8k/month budget. Let's make this week count!

---

**DAY 1 (Monday)**

**Breakfast — 8:30 am**
Have 1.5 cups poha (approx 50g raw flattened rice) cooked with 1 tsp oil, curry leaves, mustard seeds, peanuts (15g), and mixed veggies like peas and carrots. Pair it with 1 small bowl curd (100g).
(P: 12g | C: 52g | F: 10g | ~340 kcal)

**Lunch — 1:30 pm**
1 cup cooked rice (approx 150g cooked weight) with 1 katori mixed dal (approx 150g — masoor + moong works great), 1 small katori sabzi (beans or lauki, around 100g), and a side of cucumber-tomato salad with a squeeze of lemon.
(P: 18g | C: 62g | F: 6g | ~370 kcal)

**Evening Snack — 6:30 pm**
Your usual tea (no sugar or just 1 tsp) with 30g roasted chana. If you're heading to the gym right after, this is perfect pre-workout fuel.
(P: 6g | C: 18g | F: 2g | ~110 kcal)

**Dinner — 9:00 pm**
2 medium rotis (approx 60g total) with 100g paneer bhurji (cooked with onions, tomatoes, and spices in 1 tsp oil) and a big bowl of mixed salad (cucumber, tomato, carrots, cabbage — as much as you want, around 150g). Add a pinch of chaat masala to the salad for extra flavour.
(P: 26g | C: 48g | F: 16g | ~440 kcal)

**Total for the day: ~1260 kcal | P: 62g**

**A few quick notes as you go through the week:**

- I've kept your meals close to 1200-1300 kcal daily with 55-65g protein, which is a solid deficit for fat loss without feeling deprived. You'll notice energy is good because we're not slashing carbs — just controlling portions.
- On gym days (evening workouts), that 6:30 pm snack is your pre-workout fuel. Don't skip it. Post-workout, your dinner will cover recovery — the paneer, soya, or chana will do the job.
- If you feel hungry between meals, add raw veggies (cucumber, carrot sticks, cherry tomatoes) or a glass of buttermilk (chaas) — basically zero calories and keeps your stomach happy.
- For that late-night snacking struggle: after dinner, brush your teeth right away. Sounds silly, but it's a mental cue that eating time is over. If you're genuinely hungry, have a small glass of warm milk (150ml, around 100 kcal) — it's soothing and won't derail anything.
- Most of these meals can be prepped in bulk — dal, rajma, chana, soya curry all taste better the next day anyway. Cook once, eat twice.
- I've used 1 tsp oil per meal where needed (bhurji, sabzi, tadka) — keeps it light but still flavourful. If you're using ghee for tadka, same rule: 1 tsp max.
- Mushrooms are out (I know you're not a fan), so I've leaned into paneer, soya, and legumes for variety.

You've got this, Priya. One week at a time, one meal at a time. Let me know how you feel by the end of the week — we'll tweak anything that's not working. Now go crush it! 💪

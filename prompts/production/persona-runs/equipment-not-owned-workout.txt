**DAY 1 — UPPER PUSH (Monday or your preferred day)**

*Focus: Chest, shoulders, triceps. This is where we load up the pressing.*

**Band Chest Press** — 4 sets × 8–10 reps
- Stand with the band looped around your back, feet shoulder-width apart. Press the band away from your chest with control, pause for 1 second at full extension, then bring it back slowly (3-second eccentric). You want tension throughout—no slack. Leave 1–2 reps in the tank on each set.
- Coaching note: Lean slightly forward if needed for comfort, but keep your core tight. This is your main pressing movement, so quality over speed.

**Band Chest Fly** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Stand in the middle of the band, arms out to the sides at shoulder height, slight bend in the elbows. Bring your hands together in front of your chest in a controlled arc, focusing on the squeeze. Open back out slowly. This is your isolation piece—push the last set or two closer to failure.
- Coaching note: Think about hugging a barrel. The movement should feel smooth and deliberate, not jerky.

**Band Overhead Press** — 3 sets × 8–10 reps
- Stand on the band, press it overhead from shoulder height. Lower with control. Keep your core braced to avoid arching excessively.
- Coaching note: Shoulder health matters—if you feel any pinching, reduce range of motion. Leave 1–2 reps in reserve.

**Band Lateral Raise** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Stand on the band, raise your arms out to the sides to shoulder height. Lower slowly. This builds shoulder width and stability.
- Coaching note: Control the descent. No swinging.

**Band Tricep Extension** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Anchor the band overhead (loop it around a door frame or hold it with one hand), grip the other end behind your head, and extend your arm upward. Slow eccentric. Push the last set close to failure if you've got it.
- Coaching note: Keep your elbow stationary. The movement is at the elbow only.

**Core Circuit (finish with this)**
- Plank hold: 3 sets × 30–45 seconds
- Dead bugs: 3 sets × 10 reps each side
- Band Pallof press: 3 sets × 12 reps each side (hold the band at chest height, resist rotation)

**DAY 3 — UPPER PULL (Thursday or your preferred day)**

*Focus: Back, rear shoulders, biceps. Pulling strength and muscle.*

**Band Row** — 4 sets × 8–10 reps
- Anchor the band at chest height (loop around a door frame or sturdy object), or hold it in both hands. Row the band toward your torso, squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end, and control the return. This is your main pulling movement—treat it with the same respect as a heavy compound.
- Coaching note: Full range of motion. Feel your lats engage. Leave 1–2 reps in reserve.

**Band Reverse Fly** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Stand in the middle of the band with arms out in front of you at shoulder height. Pull the band apart to the sides, squeezing your rear shoulders. Control back to center. Isolation work—push harder on the last set.
- Coaching note: This balances the pressing from Day 1 and protects your shoulders. Don't go light.

**Band Bicep Curl** — 3 sets × 10–12 reps
- Stand on the band, curl it up toward your shoulders, lower with control. No momentum.
- Coaching note: Squeeze at the top. The eccentric (lowering) is where the magic happens—take 3 seconds on the way down.

**Band Face Pull** — 3 sets × 15–20 reps
- Anchor the band high, grab both ends, and pull toward your face, flaring your elbows out. This is high-rep work for shoulder health and rear delt endurance.
- Coaching note: Higher reps here. Feel the burn. This prevents shoulder issues down the line.

**Band Shrug** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Stand on the band, let your arms hang, and shrug your shoulders toward your ears. Pause at the top, lower slowly.
- Coaching note: Controlled tempo. No jerking.

**Core Circuit (finish with this)**
- Hollow body hold: 3 sets × 20–30 seconds
- Bird dogs: 3 sets × 10 reps each side
- Band anti-rotation hold: 3 sets × 20 seconds each side (hold the band at chest, resist the pull)

**DAY 4 — LOWER BODY (Saturday or your preferred day)**

*Focus: Legs, glutes, hamstrings, quads. Build strength and size in your lower half.*

**Band Goblet Squat** — 4 sets × 10–12 reps
- Hold the band in both hands at chest height (loop it around your hands and under your feet for resistance). Squat down, keeping your chest up and weight in your heels. Drive through your legs to stand. This is your main lower-body compound—go deep and controlled.
- Coaching note: Full range of motion. Pause at the bottom for 1 second if you can. Leave 1–2 reps in reserve. This movement teaches solid mechanics and builds real strength.

**Band Romanian Deadlift** — 3 sets × 10–12 reps
- Stand on the band, hold the ends, and hinge at the hips (slight knee bend). Lower the band down your legs, feeling a stretch in your hamstrings, then drive your hips forward to stand. This is a hip hinge pattern—essential for lower-body strength and posterior chain health.
- Coaching note: Chest stays proud. Hamstrings should feel this hard. Control the descent.

**Band Lateral Walk** — 3 sets × 12 reps each direction
- Loop the band around your legs just above the knees. Squat slightly and step sideways, keeping tension on the band. This builds glute activation and lateral stability.
- Coaching note: Glutes should be firing. Feel that side-to-side tension.

**Band Leg Extension** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Sit on the floor, loop the band around one foot and hold the other end. Extend your leg straight, pause, lower with control. Repeat on the other side. This isolates the quads.
- Coaching note: Higher reps here. Go for the pump. Control the movement—no snapping.

**Band Hamstring Curl** — 3 sets × 12–15 reps
- Lie on your back, loop the band around one foot, and curl your heel toward your glute. Slow eccentric. Repeat on the other side.
- Coaching note: Isolation work. Feel the hamstring contract. Push close to failure on the last set.

**Core Circuit (finish with this)**

- Dead bug with band: 3 sets × 10 reps each side (hold the band as you perform the movement for extra tension)
- Band Pallof press: 3 sets × 12 reps each side

**SLEEP & RECOVERY**

You're already hitting 7–8 hours, which is solid. Here's how to optimize it for muscle gain:

1. **Aim for 7.5–9 hours nightly.** Your muscles grow during sleep—don't shortchange this. If you can, push toward 8–8.5 hours consistently.

2. **Consistent bedtime.** Try to hit the pillow around the same time each night. Your body loves routine. If your schedule is flexible, aim for 10:30 PM–11 PM to catch the early sleep cycles.

3. **Cool room.** Keep your bedroom temperature around 16–18°C (60–65°F) if possible. Cooler rooms improve sleep quality.

4. **No screens 30–45 minutes before bed.** Blue light messes with melatonin. Use this time to wind down—read, stretch, or just sit quietly.

5. **Light dinner 2–3 hours before sleep.** Heavy meals close to bedtime disrupt sleep. If you're hungry before bed, a small snack (banana, yogurt, handful of nuts) is fine.

6. **Hydration matters—but not right before bed.** Drink plenty during the day, but taper off 1–2 hours before sleep to avoid midnight bathroom trips.

7. **Manage stress.** Training is a stress on your body—good stress, but still. If you're wired before bed, try 5 minutes of breathing work (4-second inhale, 6-second exhale) to calm your nervous system.

Sleep is where the adaptation happens. Treat it like another training session.

**INTENSITY & EFFORT GUIDANCE**

Here's the philosophy: on heavy compounds (chest press, rows, squats, deadlifts), leave 1–2 reps in the tank. This means you could do one or two more reps, but you stop. This keeps your form tight, your joints healthy, and your nervous system from burning out. On isolation and accessory work (flies, curls, raises, extensions), push the last set or two closer to actual failure—that's where the muscle-building magic happens. For core work, go hard but controlled. Never sacrifice form for ego, especially on risky movement patterns.
